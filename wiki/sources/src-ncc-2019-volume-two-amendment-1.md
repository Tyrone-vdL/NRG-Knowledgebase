# NCC 2019 Volume Two Amendment 1 – Building Code of Australia (Class 1 and 10 Buildings)

## Summary

This document is Amendment 1 to the National Construction Code (NCC) 2019, Volume Two (Building Code of Australia), published by the Australian Building Codes Board (ABCB) on behalf of the Commonwealth and all State and Territory governments, with a release date of July 2020. Volume Two sets the minimum technical design and construction requirements for Class 1 and Class 10 buildings, including houses, small sheds, carports and private bushfire shelters. The document is a performance-based code structured around Governing Requirements, Performance Requirements, and Deemed-to-Satisfy (DtS) Provisions, with State and Territory variations and additions included throughout and in Schedule 1.

## Key claims

- Volume Two primarily covers Class 1 and 10 buildings; Class 2–9 buildings are primarily covered by Volume One.
- Compliance can be achieved via a Performance Solution, a Deemed-to-Satisfy Solution, or a combination of both (A2.1).
- A Performance Solution must be assessed using one or more of: evidence of suitability (Part A5), a Verification Method, Expert Judgement, or comparison with DtS Provisions (A2.2(2)).
- A Deemed-to-Satisfy Solution in Volume Two may be satisfied by either an "acceptable construction practice" (written into Section 3) or an "acceptable construction manual" (a referenced document), but not necessarily both, depending on whether they address the same or different components of a Performance Requirement (A2.3(3)).
- Words in italics must be interpreted using definitions in Schedule 3, with additional definitions in State or Territory appendices as appropriate (A1.0(2)).
- "Explanatory information" and "Introduction to this Part/Section" content is non-mandatory and informative; Application, Limitation, Exemption statements and Notes are mandatory (A1.0(1), A1.0(3)).
- NCC provisions override any conflicting requirements in a primary referenced document, except where an acceptable construction manual is used in its entirety (A4.1 and its Exemption 1).
- Referenced documents are adopted only at the specific edition or issue listed in Schedule 4; later editions are not automatically adopted (A4.0(1), A4.0(3)).
- A Class 1a building is a single dwelling, including a detached house or one of a group of two or more attached dwellings (e.g. terrace, town house, villa unit) separated by a fire-resisting wall (A6.1(1)).
- A Class 1b building includes a boarding house, guest house or hostel ordinarily accommodating not more than 12 people with a total floor area not more than 300 m² measured over enclosing walls, or four or more single dwellings on one allotment used for short-term holiday accommodation (A6.1(2)).
- A Class 1 building cannot be located above or below another dwelling or another class of building, other than a private garage (A6.1 Limitation 1).
- Where a minor part of a storey (less than 10% of floor area) is designed for a different purpose, the classification of the major part may apply to the whole storey, except where the minor use is a laboratory or a Class 2, 3 or 4 part (A6.0 Exemption 1 and Limitation 1).
- Energy efficiency requirements for Volume Two are contained in Part 3.12, covering building fabric (3.12.1), external glazing (3.12.2), building sealing (3.12.3), air movement (3.12.4), and services (3.12.5).
- State and Territory variations, additions and deletions appear throughout Volume Two and in Schedule 1, and must be complied with in conjunction with the NCC; State or Territory legislation may override NCC provisions (A3.0(2), A3.0(3)).
- Aluminium Composite Panels must be labelled in accordance with SA TS 5344 (A5.7).

## Caveats

- This is the 2019 edition with Amendment 1 (released July 2020); it has been superseded by later NCC editions (NCC 2022 and NCC 2025) and may not reflect current requirements in all jurisdictions.
- Adoption dates are determined individually by each State and Territory, so the version in force varies by jurisdiction and should be confirmed with the relevant building authority.
- The document extract provided covers only the Contents, Introduction, and Governing Requirements sections in full; technical provisions in Sections 2 and 3 (including specific R-values, U-values and other quantitative requirements) are listed in the table of contents but their detailed text is not reproduced in the uploaded extract.
