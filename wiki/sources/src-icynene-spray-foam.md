# Icynene LD-C-50 Spray Foam Insulation — Information Kit, CodeMark Certificate and SDS

## Summary

Compiled information kit for Icynene LD-C-50 open-cell spray polyurethane foam insulation (distributed in Australia by Era Polymers), bundling the product specification, the thermal-resistance-by-thickness table from its CodeMark Certificate of Conformity against the BCA 2013, acoustic test results, and the Australian Safety Data Sheets for Part A (isocyanate) and Part B (resin). LD-C-50 is a light-density (nominal 8 kg/m³), 100% water-blown, vapour-permeable foam that insulates and air-seals in one application. Key compliance figure: R2.60 m²K/W at 100 mm thickness.

## Key claims

- Thermal resistance (ASTM C518, core samples): RSI 0.65 m²·°C/W per 25.4 mm at 24°C (R 3.7 hr·ft²·°F/BTU per inch at 75°F).
- CodeMark Certificate GM-CM30009 Rev D (Global-Mark; issued 13/05/2010, last update 15/08/2013, expiry 26/07/2016) thermal resistance table, assessed to ASTM C518 as referenced in AS/NZS 4859.1:2002 incl. Amdt 1: 25 mm = R0.65; 50 mm = R1.30; 75 mm = R1.95; 100 mm = R2.60; 125 mm = R3.15; 150 mm = R3.90; 175 mm = R4.55; 200 mm = R5.20 (all m²K/W).
- Certificate conditions: reported thermal resistances are based on a mean temperature of 24°C and a temperature differential of 22°C; values are the foam alone ("added insulation"), exclude thermal bridging at studs, and are based on an upper 10-percentile thermal conductivity of 0.038 W/m·K.
- The certificate states compliance with BCA 2013 Volume One JP1 and Volume Two P2.6.1 (energy efficiency of walls, ceilings and floors) when Table 1 values are used, and explicitly excludes fire (BCA Vol One Section C, Vol Two Part 3.7), bushfire (G5, P2.3.4/P2.3.5), vermin proofing and condensation control.
- Marketing/spec sheet claims R-2.6 per 100 mm; average full-fill stud wall contribution: 38×89 mm stud = RSI 2.3 (R13); 38×140 mm stud = RSI 3.6 (R20).
- Air permeability of a 2"×6" wood-framed wall assembly (ASTM E2178): 0.01 L/s·m² @ 75 Pa at 140 mm thickness; buildings sealed with the product must include adequate mechanical ventilation.
- Water vapour permeance (ASTM E96 desiccant method): 1218 ng/(Pa·s·m²) at 25.4 mm (21 perms @ 1") — vapour permeable; a supplementary vapour barrier (polyethylene or low-permeance paint) is needed where conditions warrant.
- Expansion ratio approximately 100:1 on application; open-cell, so water can be forced in under pressure but drains and properties restore on drying.
- Acoustics (full-fill 38×89 mm wood stud wall): STC 37 (ASTM E90) and NRC 0.70 (ASTM C423), tested May 1988 by Gold Bond Building Products Research Center.
- Combustible: consumed by flame but will not sustain flame on removal of source; will not melt or drip; thermal barrier coverings required per the applicable building code; not to be installed within 75 mm of heat-emitting devices or where temperatures exceed 93°C (ASTM C411).
- Installation must be by Icynene-trained approved installers in accordance with AS 3999:1992 (incl. Amdt 1); electrical installation where Icynene is installed must comply with AS/NZS 3000:2007.
- SDS Part A (issue 19/11/2013): polymeric MDI, GHS DANGER — respiratory sensitiser Cat 1, suspected carcinogen (Carc. 2), Australian exposure standard for isocyanates (as NCO) TWA 0.02 mg/m³ / STEL 0.07 mg/m³. SDS Part B: alkanolamine/alkylamine resin blend, WARNING — skin and eye irritant Cat 2.

## Caveats

- The CodeMark certificate is against BCA 2013 and shows an expiry date of 26/07/2016 — verify current certification status before relying on it for NCC 2022 compliance; abcb.gov.au should be checked per the certificate's own advice.
- Much of the product specification is the Canadian datasheet (CCMC references, CAN/ULC fire tests); Australian-specific compliance rests on the CodeMark certificate section only.
- SDS documents are dated 19/11/2013 and are pre-GHS-7; occupational data may be superseded.
