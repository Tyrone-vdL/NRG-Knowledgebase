# NCC 2025 Public Comment Draft — Volume Two (extract incl. Specification 42): Proposed Changes vs NCC 2022

> **STATUS: PUBLIC COMMENT DRAFT — NOT IN FORCE.** Every provision on this page is a *proposed* change shown in tracked-changes format. NCC 2022 (as amended by Amendment 1) remains the in-force code until NCC 2025 is finalised and adopted. Final text may differ from this draft.

## Summary

A tracked-changes extract of the NCC 2025 Public Comment Draft of Volume Two (Class 1 and 10 buildings) covering Parts H1–H5, H7, H8 and Specification 42 (house energy rating software), showing the proposed deltas from NCC 2022. For energy efficiency the key finding is what does NOT change: Spec 42 retains the **7-star NatHERS minimum**, the climate zone 1–2 outdoor living area credits (6.5★/6★) and the **whole-of-home rating of 60** — the substantive proposed edits are a re-pointed slab edge insulation reference in S42C4, a structural reliability overhaul (H1P1/H1V1), new swimming pool drainage and roofed-outdoor-area fire DtS, a natural ventilation option, and new livable housing step-free access exemptions for steep sites.

## Specification 42 — house energy rating software (proposed NCC 2025 state)

- **S42C2(1) unchanged in substance:** a building must achieve an energy rating, including the separate heating and cooling load limits, of **≥7 stars**; or in **climate zones 1 or 2**, **6.5 stars** with an outdoor living area fully covered by an impervious roof with Total R-Value ≥1.5 (downward heat flow); or **6 stars** with that roof plus at least one permanently installed ceiling fan.
- Load limits still per the **ABCB Standard for NatHERS Heating and Cooling Load Limits** (S42C2(2)); the explanatory note confirms three separate limits: total load limit for the star rating, heating load limit, cooling load limit.
- Outdoor living area definition unchanged: directly adjoining/accessible from a general purpose living area (not bedrooms/study/home theatre); floor area ≥12.0 m²; length and width each ≥2.5 m; opening height above floor ≥2.1 m (a breeze-path height, not a ceiling or fan height); one side permanently open and a second side permanently open or readily openable; open sides ≥900 mm from boundary or breeze-path obstruction (S42C2(3)–(4)).
- Ceiling fan rules unchanged: permanently installed, speed controller, serves the whole room; one fan serves ≤15 m² (blade diameter <1200 mm) or ≤25 m² (≥1200 mm) (S42C2(5)).
- **S42C3 unchanged: whole-of-home rating ≥60** using house energy rating software.
- **S42C4 proposed edit:** the additional DtS provisions when using software still require ABCB Housing Provisions 13.2.2 (fabric insulation), 13.2.3(7) and 13.2.5(5) (thermal breaks), 13.2.3(5) (ceiling insulation loss compensation unless the software compensated), and Part 13.4 (sealing) for H6P1; the floor edge insulation reference is re-pointed to **"13.2.6(4), 13.2.6(5)(a)(i), 13.2.6(5)(b)(i) and 13.2.6(6) for slab edge insulation"** — i.e. wording changes from "floor edge insulation" to "slab edge insulation" and the sub-clauses target only the *vertical edge* components of the new CZ6–8 slab insulation regime (the under-slab components 13.2.6(5)(a)(ii)/(b)(ii) are NOT called up when rating with software). Part 13.7 still applies for H6P2 (S42C4(2)).

## Proposed changes elsewhere in Volume Two (captured in this extract)

- **H1P1 structural reliability overhaul:** rewritten performance requirement with quantified minimum annual reliability indices — Table H1P1a additive gravity-only combinations **β = 4.30** (all Importance Levels; ≈1/117,000 per year probability of failure); Table H1P1b combined gravity+other actions β = 3.45 (IL1) / 3.70 (IL2); Table H1P1c action reversal/stability β = 3.00 (IL1) / 3.35 (IL2). Five-percentile characteristic material properties with allowances for construction activities, material type, site characteristics, settlement and restrained dimensional changes; termite actions and construction activity actions added to the action list.
- **H1V1 rewritten:** verification of structural reliability of components limited to resistance coefficient of variation 10–40% (use 10% minimum where calculated lower); new annual action models table (e.g. permanent action mean/nominal 1.05, CoV 0.10; cyclonic wind IL2 0.16/0.76; earthquake IL2 0.15/0.90) and annual target reliability indices (permanent 4.3 — increased to 5.0 for timber-like creep; imposed 4.0; wind/snow/earthquake 3.7).
- **H1V2 structural robustness:** building must remain stable (collapse not extending beyond immediately adjacent storeys) upon notional removal of any supporting column, beam supporting columns, or load bearing wall segment equal in length to its height.
- H1D7/H1D8 cladding and glazing DtS substantively carried over (AS 1562.1/.3, AS 2050/AS 2049/AS 4597, AS 2047, AS 1288, AS 4055 N3 limits, cyclonic Low-High-Low fatigue sequence Table H1D7 for regions B2, C, D).
- **NEW H2P4 + H2D2(2): swimming pool drainage** — a pool must have adequate means of draining without causing illness or affecting other property; DtS: pumped discharge connected to the sanitary drainage system per AS/NZS 3500.2.
- H2V1 weatherproofing verification carried over (prototype test; risk score ≤20; ULS wind pressure ≤2.5 kPa; AS 2047 windows).
- **H3D2 fire hazard properties:** sarking-type materials ≤1 mm with Flammability Index ≤5 retained; NEW (b): flexible ductwork transferring products from a flame-containing heat source must meet the fire hazard properties of **AS 4254.1**; fire hazard properties must be determined by an Accredited Testing Laboratory (note: until NCC 2028 adoption, determination need not be by an Accredited Testing Laboratory for the H3V heat-flux test).
- **NEW H3D7: fire separation of roofed outdoor areas** — compliance with new Part 9.6 of the ABCB Housing Provisions satisfies H3P1 (see [[src-ncc-2025-pcd-abcb-housing-provisions-v1-1]] for the Part 9.6 detail).
- **H4D7 ventilation:** H4P5 now satisfied by Part 10.6 of the Housing Provisions **or, for natural ventilation, AS 1668.4** (new alternative); mechanical systems still per AS 1668.2.
- H4D9 condensation management: compliance with Part 10.8 of the Housing Provisions (the substantially rewritten Part 10.8 — see companion page).
- **H8D2 livable housing — NEW exemptions (2)–(4):** Clause 1.1 (step-free access path) of the ABCB Standard for Livable Housing Design need not be complied with if step-free access via garage/carport/parking space is not provided AND one of: average ground slope along the path exceeds **1:14**; required ramping would exceed Clause 1.1(4) length/gradient limits; insufficient site space; or the vertical level difference from pedestrian entry/parking to the entrance door floor (measured per (3), from the lowest point of any connecting deck/balcony) would require non-compliant ramping. All other provisions of the Standard still apply even when Clause 1.1 is exempted. A parallel exemption appears against H8P1 where site conditions leave no suitable location for the path.
