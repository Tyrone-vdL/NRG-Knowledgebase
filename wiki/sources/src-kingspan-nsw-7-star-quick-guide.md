# Kingspan NSW 7 Star NatHERS Quick Guide (Class 1a, NCC 2022)

## Summary

Kingspan Insulation's New South Wales quick guide (first issue 08/2024, doc KIAU0214 NSW) showing insulation specifications that achieve a 7 Star NatHERS rating under NCC 2022 for example single- and double-storey Class 1a homes in NSW Climate Zones 28 and 56, in timber and steel frame, across floor types (uninsulated slab, wafflepod, slab + under-slab insulation). It is the NSW state variant — the companion VIC guide covers Climate Zone 21 only; this NSW version uniquely ties the 7 Star requirement to BASIX. Solutions centre on Kooltherm K12 Framing Board (walls), Kooltherm K3 Floorboard (under slab) and GreenGuard GG350 (slab edge).

## Key claims

- NSW-specific framing: BASIX is the NSW assessment tool, and "a BASIX compliant NatHERS assessment can not be less than 7 Stars"; heating/cooling load stringency increased and a Total Load metric identical to the NatHERS 7 Star Total Load was added to BASIX software in each affected climate zone.
- Covers NatHERS Climate Zones 28 and 56 (the VIC variant covers only Zone 21/Melbourne — the zone tables are the substantive state difference).
- NCC 2022 raised the minimum NatHERS rating from 6 to 7 stars in some regions and updated climate files (69 climate zones, weather data 1990–2015 replacing 1970–2005); buildings cause around 18% of Australia's carbon emissions.
- Example homes: single-storey 235.17 m² total (184.43 m² ground floor, 25.72 m² garage, 4.68 m² porch, 10.34 m² alfresco); double-storey 384.70 m² total (168.06 m² ground, 176.12 m² first floor, 36.59 m² garage, 3.93 m² porch). Medium wall/roof/window-frame colours, west-facing front, north-facing solar panels, IC4 downlights, ceiling perimeter insulated to R2.5 per AS 3999:2015.
- Zone 28 timber single-storey example: uninsulated slab + double glazed Low-E windows + R2.7 wall batts with 25 mm Kooltherm K12 (R1.1) + R7.0 ceiling + R2.7 laundry perimeter walls achieves 7 Stars; the wafflepod variant drops ceiling to R5.0 with plain double glazing.
- Zone 28 timber single-storey with insulated slab (50 mm K3 Floorboard + 40 mm GG350): single-glazed sliding doors become acceptable with R2.7 walls + R6.0 ceiling; all-single-glazing variants need R7.0 ceiling plus an R1.3 roof blanket.
- Zone 56 single-storey uninsulated-slab solutions require R6.0 ceiling plus R6.0 garage ceiling insulation (Zone 56 tables generally demand more garage-ceiling insulation than Zone 28); steel-frame Zone 56 solutions add R1.3 roof blankets in most rows.
- Zone 56 double-storey uninsulated-slab solutions specify Double Glazed Low-E Thermally Broken windows and sliding doors — the heaviest glazing spec in the NSW guide (the VIC guide instead escalates to triple glazing for its uninsulated-slab single-storey cases).
- Steel-frame solutions consistently use lower stud-cavity R-values (R2.2 in 75 mm batts within 76 mm studs) plus 25 mm Kooltherm K12 thermal-break layer, reflecting steel's thermal bridging; one Zone 28 steel single-storey all-single-glazed row needs R7.0 ceiling + R7.0 garage ceiling + R2.5 roof blanket + R2.2 internal walls.
- Wall build-ups: 90 mm stud + 90 mm glass-fibre batt (R2.7, R2.5 or R2.0) + 25 mm Kooltherm K12 (R1.1) inside or outside; 76 mm steel stud + 75 mm batt (R2.2) + 25 mm K12 (R1.1); or full-fill 80 mm Kooltherm K12 between 90 mm studs giving R3.6.
- Floor build-up: 50 mm Kooltherm K3 Floorboard under slab = R2.3, plus 40 mm GreenGuard GG350 slab-edge insulation = R1.0.
- Pack sizes: K12 Framing Board 25 mm × 2400 × 1200 mm (12/pack) and 80 mm × 1200 mm in 403/413/553/563 mm widths (6/pack); K3 Floorboard 25 mm (12/pack) and 50 mm × 2400 × 1200 mm (6/pack); GG350 40 mm × 2400 × 300 mm (5/pack); Thermakraft Watergate Plus wall wrap rolls 45–90 m² nominal.

## Caveats

- First issue 08/2024; solutions are modelled on two specific example house designs — they are guidance starting points, not deemed-to-satisfy specifications, and actual ratings require a NatHERS assessment of the real design.
- Applies to NSW (NatHERS Climate Zones 28 and 56) only; do not transfer rows to other zones or states — see the VIC variant for Climate Zone 21.
- The compliance tables are heavily layout-degraded in extraction; row pairings quoted above were reconstructed from column order — verify any row used for client advice against the source PDF.
