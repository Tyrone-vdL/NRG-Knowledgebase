# NRG Internal Guide — Getting a Dwelling to 7 Star (Upgrade Sequence)

## Summary

NRG Efficient Homes' internal two-page assessor procedure (dated Monday 20 January 2025) setting out the standard baseline inputs and the step-by-step upgrade sequence — ceiling, wall, roof, floor insulation then glazing — used to lift a dwelling's NatHERS rating to 7 stars. It specifies exact starting R-values, maximum practical R-values with space constraints, generic glazing codes, lowest achievable frame U-values, and fallback strategies (thermal mass, colour/solar absorptance, fans, ventilation, shading, glazing reduction) for stubborn cooling or heating loads.

## Key claims

- Minimum insulation values to input as standard: R3.5 ceilings; R1.5 walls; R1.5 garage internal walls; R1.5 timber sub-floors and upper-floor areas that overhang the ground floor.
- Baseline glazing is generic single glazed clear: ALM-001-01 Type A (awning, bifold, casement) and ALM-002-01 Type B (sliding, fixed, louvre).
- Upgrade step 1 — ceiling insulation: try R4.0, R5.0, R6.0, max R7.0; R7.0 is approx. 290mm thick and needs roughly 350mm of roof space including a ventilation gap.
- Upgrade step 2 — wall insulation: try R2.0, R2.5, max R2.7 HD batt to external walls; add R2.0 batt to internal walls between unconditioned and conditioned zones; R2.5 and R2.7 require a 90mm stud; an air gap can be requested if boundary setbacks allow.
- Upgrade step 3 — roof insulation: try reflective foil, R1.3 blanket, max R1.8 blanket (metal roof only); minimum 25mm gap required between reflective foil and top of ceiling batt.
- Upgrade step 4 — floor insulation: timber floors try R2.0, max R2.5; concrete floors try 20/30/40mm, max 50mm extruded polystyrene (rigid foam); upper floors above a garage or overhanging the ground floor try R2.5, max R3.5.
- Upgrade step 5 — glazing: generic double glazed values first (ALM-005-03 Type A; ALM-006-03 Type B); then custom values from A&L, Bradnams, AWS or Dowell; lowest U-value for aluminium frames is 2.5 (SHGC varies by climate; SHGC under 0.40 requires a darker tint that may hurt the facade), and lowest U-value for timber or uPVC frames is 1.9.
- To reduce cooling loads: add thermal mass (exposed slabs, reverse brick veneer, concrete block or double-brick internal feature walls); lighter colours with solar absorptance SA<0.40 on roof, walls and window/door frames; ceiling fans up to 1400mm diameter; more cross ventilation; vertical adjustable shading to east and west; Alphafloor for timber sub-floors; reduce east/west glazing while keeping sufficient northern glazing with horizontal shading.
- To reduce heating loads: reduce glazed area; darker colours with SA>0.60; thermal mass only where it receives direct sun (otherwise it can worsen heating loads by drawing heat from the space); insulate between unconditioned and conditioned zones; increase living-zone spaces (better natural heating load than day-time zones); reducing southern glazing also helps.
- If the standard upgrade specs still fail compliance, client consultation may be required before pursuing the fallback strategies.

## Caveats

- NRG internal copyright document (NRGEH) dated 20 January 2025 — reflects NRG assessor practice, not a regulatory instrument; values are simulation inputs/targets, not NCC deemed-to-satisfy requirements.
- Generic glazing codes (ALM-xxx) are NatHERS default window library identifiers; custom manufacturer values supersede them when specified.
