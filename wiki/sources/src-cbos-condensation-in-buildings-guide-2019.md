# Condensation in Buildings — Tasmanian Designers' Guide (Version 2, CBOS 2019)

## Summary

Tasmanian Consumer, Building and Occupational Services (CBOS) designers' guide (Version 2, April 2019, ISBN 978-0-9954065-8-2) on minimising condensation in cool-climate housing, covering roof spaces, walls and subfloors with specific ventilation gap sizes, vent areas and vapour-permeable membrane guidance. Its strategies exceed NCC minimums and are based on University of Tasmania research; it is the practical reference for roof/wall/subfloor condensation detailing in climate zone 7-type climates.

## Key claims

- Condensation in homes is driven by two causes: **thermal bridging** and **too much water vapour** — an adult releases about **3 litres of water vapour per day** just by breathing.
- Vapour-permeable building fabrics (plasterboard, timber, clay bricks, some pliable membranes) allow passive escape of vapour; non-permeable fabrics include glass, aluminium, steel and some pliable membranes. **Perforated wraps do not have the same permeability properties as breathable wraps** — use second-generation vapour-permeable wall wraps, not punched sarkings.
- Roof space strategy: duct extraction systems outside; provide supply and exhaust roof-space ventilation; **install sarking under battens** (not in contact with roofing) to minimise thermal bridging; ventilate the sarking zone; use vapour-permeable sarking.
- **Continuous gap ventilation sizes:** at the eaves — 25 mm for roof pitch <16°, 10 mm for >16°; at the ridge — at least 5 mm for all pitches.
- **Roof vent areas:** minimum vent area = ceiling area/150 for pitch <16°, or ceiling area/300 for >16°; ventilation split **75% supply / 25% exhaust**; gable vents within 900 mm of the ridge; exhaust vents within 900 mm of the ridge cap/top edge.
- **Sarking zone air space:** maintain a minimum 25 mm path between sheet metal roofing and sarking (medium/high pitch); maintain a minimum 25 mm air gap into the roof space; typical foil-blanket systems with no air gap are flagged as the problem detail for low-pitch roofs.
- Roof insulation should provide full coverage, be fully lofted, and never compressed; avoid downlights that leak warm moist air into the roof space (use surface-mounted or sealed downlights).
- Wall strategy: all external wall layers should be vapour permeable; where cladding contacts the membrane, provide a **vented vapour cavity of minimum 10 mm width** between the vapour-permeable pliable membrane and the external cladding; tape membrane joins and penetrations; a pressurisation test can verify air leakage in new builds.
- Subfloor: NCC prescribes minimum subfloor ventilation for enclosed-perimeter platform floors; exceed NCC rates where ground-level cross ventilation is limited; control ground moisture with sealed impervious membranes over soil and site drainage per AS 2870:2011; if subfloor moisture persists after drainage works, cover the ground with a sand layer.
- In bushfire-prone areas, all vents/gaps must comply with AS 3959:2018 (ember-resistant steel mesh, non-combustible compressible materials, ember-resistant vents).
- Owner-side strategies include ducting (non-condensing) dryers and rangehoods outside, avoiding unflued gas heaters, and ventilating rooms with spas/aquariums/indoor plants.

## Caveats

- Guidance only — explicitly a non-mandatory resource whose strategies **exceed** the NCC's mandatory condensation management requirements; the NCC remains the legal minimum.
- Published April 2019, pre-dating the strengthened NCC 2022 Part 10.8 condensation provisions and the further-expanded NCC 2025 PCD provisions (see [[src-ncc-2025-pcd-abcb-housing-provisions-v1-1]]) — some of its recommendations have since been partially codified with different numbers (e.g. NCC roof-space ventilation uses mm²/m opening rates, not area ratios).
- Tasmanian focus (cool climates); the diagrams are explicitly "diagrammatic only" and not full NCC construction details.
