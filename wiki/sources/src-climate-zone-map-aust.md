# ABCB Climate Zone Map — Australia (Zones 1-8)

## Summary

ABCB national map of the eight NCC climate zones (Zone 1 through Zone 8) used for thermal design across Australia, drawn against Local Government Area boundaries and developed from a Bureau of Meteorology map. Last amended August 2015, version VC00031.3; this is the map referenced under the "climate zone" definition used by NCC Volume Two energy efficiency provisions.

## Key claims

- Legend lists the eight NCC climate zones: Zone 1, Zone 2, Zone 3, Zone 4, Zone 5, Zone 6, Zone 7 and Zone 8, mapped against Local Government Area boundaries.
- Developed from a map produced by the Bureau of Meteorology; published by the ABCB (www.abcb.gov.au), version VC00031.3, last amended August 2015.
- The map labels capital cities and ~95 regional locations nationwide (e.g. Sydney, Brisbane, Darwin, Perth, Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart, Melbourne, Alice Springs, Townsville, Kalgoorlie-Boulder, Broome, Cairns, Mount Isa, Lord Howe Island) so users can read their zone off the map.
- Map graticule spans roughly latitudes 10°-40° S and longitudes 105°-165° E, covering the whole continent plus Tasmania and offshore islands.
- The PDF contains no zone-to-location table — zone assignment for a given location is conveyed visually by the colour-coded map only.

## Concepts touched

NCC, climate zones, thermal design, energy efficiency, ABCB, Bureau of Meteorology

## Caveats

- This is a map: the staged text extraction captures only place names, legend labels and coordinates — the actual zone boundaries and zone-per-location assignments are visual content. Verify any location's climate zone against the original PDF (Z - NCC/_CLIMATE ZONE MAPS/ClimateZoneMapAUST.pdf) or the ABCB website.
- Version last amended August 2015; the ABCB has since reissued climate zone maps (e.g. Sep 2019 releases with WCAG tables) — check abcb.gov.au for the current version before relying on boundaries.
