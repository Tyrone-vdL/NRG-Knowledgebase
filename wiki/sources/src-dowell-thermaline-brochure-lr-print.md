# Dowell ThermaLine Thermally Broken Windows and Doors Brochure

## Summary

Marketing and technical brochure (© November 2014) for the Dowell ThermaLine range of double-glazed, thermally broken aluminium windows and sliding doors, with WERS-rated whole-of-window Uw-values, SHGC, visible transmittance and air infiltration figures for every product/glazing combination. Covers the range (awning, multi-transom awning, sliding, fixed windows; sliding door), climate-zone window selection guidance, condensation/dew-point performance, Passive House context, and standard size charts. Awarded HIA GreenSmart Product of the Year 2012 and approved under the National Asthma Council's Sensitive Choice program.

## Key claims

- ThermaLine frames use a polyamide/glass-fibre thermal break between outer and inner aluminium sections; complete thermal breaks in all parts of the window. Over 400 thermal-modelling iterations in development.
- Headline performance: Uw-values as low as 1.7 (awning, 4SolB/12Ar/4Clr glazing) with SHGC 0.18; the fixed window achieves Uw 1.6 with SHGC 0.20 (DOW-023-06). All values are WERS-certified whole-of-window figures (13 November 2014 directory).
- Awning window comparison chart: single-glazed standard aluminium awning Uw 6.5 / SHGC 0.65; double-glazed standard aluminium Uw 3.9 / SHGC 0.58; ThermaLine double glazed Uw 2.6 / SHGC 0.52; ThermaLine DG low-e Uw 1.9 / SHGC 0.45; ThermaLine Sol B Uw 1.7 / SHGC 0.18.
- Air infiltration: 0.04 L/s.m² at 75 Pa for awning and fixed windows; 0.25 for the sliding door; 0.74 for the sliding window.
- WERS table ranges: awning DG Uw 1.7–2.6; sliding window DG Uw 1.9–2.9; fixed window DG Uw 1.6–2.6; fixed single-glazed (thermally broken) Uw 3.4–5.4; sliding door DG Uw 1.8–2.9. SHGC across the range spans 0.18–0.72.
- Reference points quoted: Passive House window Uw 0.7 ("energy neutral"); standard single-glazed non-thermally-broken aluminium window Uw 6.5.
- SHGC selection guidance: as low as 0.18 for hot climates, around 0.55 for cool climates; the brochure says ThermaLine typically helps designs improve from 4–5 stars up to 6 stars or greater.
- Dowell suggested ranges by NCC climate zone: zones 1–3 (hot) Uw 4.5–6.5, SHGC 0.3–0.60 (tinted/low-gain low-e glazing); zones 4–5 (mixed — Sydney, Perth, Adelaide) Uw 3.5–6.5, SHGC 0.5–0.75; zones 6–8 (cold — most of VIC, ACT, TAS) Uw 2–4.8, SHGC 0.6–0.75 (clear low-e / clear IGU).
- Heat loss/gain framing: as much as 49% of winter heat loss and 87% of summer heat gain can come from windows (AWA figure, based on an average house with 8% glazing area). Around 75% of Melbourne household energy is for heating; around 90% of Brisbane home energy use is for cooling.
- Condensation (21°C internal): dew point on a conventional double-glazed aluminium frame occurs at 8°C outside at 50% internal humidity (12°C at 60%); ThermaLine frame dew point is −4°C at 50% humidity and 2.5°C at 60%. Condensation Resistance Factor: standard awning window CRF 11 vs ThermaLine awning CRF 55.
- Passive House criteria quoted: ≤15 kWh/m²/yr heating and cooling (or 10 W/m² peak), ≤120 kWh/m²/yr total primary energy, airtightness n50 ≤ 0.6 ACH at 50 Pa. Typical pre-2000 Australian home: ~10 ACH at 50 Pa, up to 25 ACH.
- U-value definition given: Uw covers the whole window (glass Ug + frame Uf); the brochure warns against suppliers quoting glass-only U-values.
- Sliding door panels up to 2400 mm high; selective glazing can cut over 30 dB of outside noise. Standard window sizes from 600–2400 mm high and 610–3010 mm wide (product dependent), rated N1–N3.

## Caveats

- Published November 2014 — references the 6-star era (pre-NCC 2022 7-Star); WERS data is from the 13 November 2014 certified products directory and may have been re-rated since.
- Climate-zone Uw/SHGC ranges are "Dowell suggested thermal performance ranges", not regulatory DtS values.
- Several performance tables (WERS data, size charts) have degraded column alignment in extraction; verify individual product figures against the source PDF before quoting in assessments.
