# NatHERS Whole of Home Guidance Note for Assessors (v20220927)

## Summary

The Australian Government's NatHERS Whole of Home Guidance Note for Assessors (published version 20220927), the interim step-by-step manual for conducting a Whole of Home assessment — covering the reference dwelling, energy value, the 12-step assessment workflow (heating/cooling, hot water, lighting, pools/spas, solar PV, batteries), Class 2 handling, and the new certificate. It is the most detailed procedural source in this folder on how the 0-100 Whole of Home performance rating is produced.

## Key claims

- The Whole of Home rating runs 0 to 100; higher ratings correspond to lower energy value and better performance. A score of 50 means the dwelling performs the same as the reference dwelling.
- Reference dwelling characteristics: 7-star thermal shell; 3-star ducted heat pump (reverse-cycle air-conditioning) for heating and cooling (per the 2019 GEMS Determination for air-conditioners); 5-star instantaneous gas hot water; lighting power density 4 W/m2; no spa or pool; no onsite energy production or storage.
- Energy value is the net cost to society of energy use (costs to the building user, environment and energy networks, as defined in the ABCB Housing Provisions); it is affected by fuel type and time of use — solar PV lowers energy value, and batteries lower it by shifting grid imports away from peak times.
- From 1 October 2022 the NCC has two residential energy efficiency performance requirements: thermal performance (addressed by the thermal star rating) and energy usage (addressed by the Whole of Home performance rating). An elemental method also exists, and in New South Wales BASIX is used in place of the NatHERS Whole of Home assessment.
- Four NatHERS-accredited software tools: AccuRate (CSIRO), BERS Pro (Energy Inspection), FirstRate5 (Sustainability Victoria), HERO (HERO Software); the software must also be accredited for Whole of Home.
- Heating/cooling rule: where more than one appliance type serves a conditioned zone, select the equipment serving at least 70% of the zone; if none reaches 70%, select the type producing the lowest Whole of Home rating.
- Lighting: enter calculated lighting power density complying with Section 13.7.6 of the ABCB Housing Provisions; if the software doesn't support it or specs are unavailable, apply the default of 5 W/m2.
- Pools/spas: enter volume (litres) or surface area (m2); from 1 October 2022 new pool pumps must meet GEMS and display a star rating on a 10-star scale. Pool cleaner categories include ancillary-pump cleaners (assumed 1 kW) and robotic cleaners (assumed 0.07 kW); main-pump cleaners with flow >6,000 L/h add no extra load. Pool heating is not yet included in the calculations.
- Solar PV: enter rated system size (kW), inverter capacity (kW) and export limit — software defaults to 5 kW per phase (the limit in most jurisdictions); azimuth relative to true north (0 or 360° = due north) and slope (0-90°); each array entered separately. Only solar PV is supported (not wind/micro-hydro).
- Batteries: enter technology type (lithium ion, lead acid or zinc bromine) and nominal storage capacity (kWh); all other characteristics (depth of discharge, charge/discharge efficiency, round-trip efficiency etc.) use defaults. Reducing imports is more effective than increasing exports for the rating.
- Cooking and plug loads are estimated by the software: assessors enter only the cooking energy source (gas, electric or both); plug-load is based on occupant numbers and needs no input.
- Class 2: the summary certificate reports the lowest Whole of Home rating across SOUs (NCC sets minimums per SOU, no building average). Centralised services are proxied by matching technology type/efficiency (centralised heating/cooling modelled as ducted); solar/batteries can only be included if connected solely to the SOU.
- A higher thermal star rating increases the Whole of Home rating because heating and cooling energy requirements are lower.
- Changes to appliances after certification: replacement with lower efficiency or different technology requires a revised assessment and reissued certificate; a higher-efficiency same-technology replacement needs no change.
- Application of Whole of Home to alterations and additions was yet to be confirmed and likely to vary between state and territory jurisdictions.

## Caveats

- Interim guidance, version 20220927 (publish version); explicitly nonbinding — where it conflicts with the NatHERS Technical Note, the Technical Note takes precedence. Several modules (spa/pool, centralised services, alterations and additions) were flagged as under review/to-be-updated at drafting.
- Commonwealth document; state and territory variations apply (notably NSW uses BASIX instead).
