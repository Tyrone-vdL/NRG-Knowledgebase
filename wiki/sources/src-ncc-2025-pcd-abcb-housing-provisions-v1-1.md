# NCC 2025 Public Comment Draft — ABCB Housing Provisions (V1.1): Proposed Changes vs NCC 2022

> **STATUS: PUBLIC COMMENT DRAFT — NOT IN FORCE.** Every provision on this page is a *proposed* change shown in tracked-changes format in the PCD. Nothing here is law. NCC 2022 (as amended by Amendment 1) remains the in-force code until NCC 2025 is finalised and adopted by the states and territories. Final NCC 2025 text may differ from this draft.

## Summary

The NCC 2025 Public Comment Draft of the ABCB Housing Provisions (V1.1), a marked-up extract showing the proposed changes from NCC 2022 across structure, fire safety, wet areas, condensation management (Part 10.8) and — most significantly for energy assessors — Section 13 energy efficiency: a complete rebuild of the elemental insulation DtS into climate-zone/solar-absorptance/overhang lookup tables, a mandatory roof solar absorptance cap of 0.64 in climate zones 1–5, under-slab insulation in climate zones 6–8, prescriptive water heater requirements, and new electrification-readiness rules (switchboard spare capacity and EV charging circuits). This page catalogues the proposed deltas so NRG can anticipate what changes when NCC 2025 lands.

## Energy efficiency — Section 13 proposed changes (the headline items)

### 13.2.3 Roofs and ceilings — rebuilt as performance-matrix tables

- Ceiling insulation R-values are proposed to be set per climate zone via new Tables 13.2.3a–13.2.3r, with the required R-value now depending on: **roof ventilation status** (vented vs standard), **presence of reflective insulation under-roof**, **under-roof insulation R-value band** (<1.0 / ≥1.0 to <1.5 / ≥1.5) and **roof solar absorptance (SA) band** (≤0.23 / ≤0.32 / ≤0.42 / ≤0.53 / ≤0.64). Example (Table 13.2.3a, CZ1 single storey, vented roof with reflective under-roof insulation): ceiling R1.5 at SA ≤0.23 rising to R3.5 at SA ≤0.64; an unvented roof without reflective insulation and under-roof R <1.0 needs R2.5 at SA ≤0.23 and is **not permitted (X)** at SA >0.32.
- **NEW 13.2.3(10): in climate zones 1 to 5, the solar absorptance of the upper surface of a roof must not be more than 0.64** — a proposed mandatory cap on dark roofs in the elemental pathway (NCC 2022 had no such cap; SA only modified required R-values).
- A roof is "vented" if it has one wind-driven roof ventilator per 50 m² of ceiling area, with gable, eave or ridge vents (Table note).
- Metal-framed roof thermal bridging options expanded (13.2.3(3)): achieve Total R-Value per Tables 13.2.3s/13.2.3t accounting for bridging; or add R0.5 to between-frame insulation; or add a continuous ceiling insulation layer ≥R0.13; or stack two insulation layers with the top layer (≥R0.5) covering the joists/chords; flat/skillion/cathedral roofs per Table 13.2.3u.
- NEW 13.2.3(4): where roof-space ventilation (10.8.3(1)) applies, continuous insulation above the primary insulation layer must have **vapour permeance not less than the primary insulation layer**.
- Edge-of-ceiling rule retained/refined (13.2.3(6)): insulation >R3.0 to ≤R4.5 may reduce to R3.0 within 450 mm of an external wall; >R4.5 may reduce to R3.0 within 450 mm provided all other ceiling insulation is increased by R0.5.
- Metal roof thermal break retained: R ≥0.2 between metal sheet roofing and metal supports where no ceiling lining or directly-fixed lining (13.2.3(7)).
- NEW: insulated sandwich panel roofs exempt from (1)–(7) but must achieve minimum Total R-Value per Table 13.2.3x (13.2.3(8)–(9)).
- Reflective insulation for roofs: surface emittance ≤0.05, adjacent roof space ≥20 mm, downward facing in CZ 3–8 (13.2.3(2)).

### 13.2.5 External walls — rebuilt as SA/overhang/wall-height tables

- Wall insulation R-values proposed per climate zone via Tables 13.2.5a–13.2.5o, keyed to **wall construction type** (concrete block, lightweight, masonry veneer, masonry cavity), **solar absorptance band**, **overhang depth** (0 to ≤2400 mm) and **wall height** (≤2.4 / ≤2.7 / ≤3.0 / ≤3.6 m), with "X = not permitted" combinations (e.g. zero-overhang lightweight walls are not permitted in most zones/bands) and "Reflective" entries (reflective insulation + ≥20 mm airspace; emittance ≤0.1 outward / 0.05 inward).
- **Wall solar absorptance cap (13.2.5(3)): in climate zones 1 to 5, the outer surface SA of a wall used in the tables must be ≤0.7.**
- Lightweight wall constructions in CZ 2, 4, 5, 6, 7 take the base table **plus R0.3**; two-storey dwellings add R0.25–R1.5 depending on table and wall height.
- Example values: masonry veneer CZ5, SA ≤0.35, 600–900 mm overhang, ≤2.4 m wall → R2.0; masonry veneer CZ4 same configuration → R2.5; masonry cavity CZ6, SA ≤0.35, no overhang → R0.51 rising with height to R1.44.
- Metal-framed wall thermal bridging: achieve Total R-Value per Tables 13.2.5p–13.2.5r calculated to **AS/NZS 4859.2** (e.g. lightweight metal-framed wall: required R1.0 → Total R1.32; R2.5 → 2.06; ≥R3.0 → 2.27), or comply with options in Tables 13.2.5s–13.2.5u; thermal break R ≥0.2 retained at cladding/metal-frame contact points (12 mm EPS strips or 20 mm timber deemed to achieve R0.2).
- CHANGE: insulated sandwich panel walls are no longer simply exempt from the thermal break rule — proposed wording requires sandwich-panel walls to address thermal bridging by achieving the Total R-Value in Table 13.2.5q per AS/NZS 4859.2.
- NEW table note in CZ4–7 tables: an overhang greater than the table maximum is allowable where ≤15% of the total opaque wall area (excluding windows/doors) is covered by an overhang.

### 13.2.6 Floors, subfloor walls and slab insulation

- Floor over **unenclosed** space (Table 13.2.6a): CZ1 R2.0, CZ2 R2.0, CZ3 R1.5, **CZ4 and CZ5 = X (insulation not permitted)**, CZ6/7/8 R4.0 (or R3.5 with a reflective airspace).
- Floor over **enclosed subfloor**: proposed subfloor-wall insulation regime — CZ1 subfloor wall R1.5; CZ2/CZ3 per subfloor wall height (≤600 mm → R0.5; >600–900 → R1.0; >900 → R1.5), with note that **under-floor insulation is not permitted in climate zone 2**; CZ4–8 per Tables 13.2.6d–13.2.6h.
- **Slab-on-ground insulation (13.2.6(5)) — major proposed change:** in **climate zones 6 and 7**, slab-on-ground (other than waffle pod) must have vertical edge insulation **R ≥0.64** AND insulation **R ≥0.64 underneath the slab**; in **climate zone 8**, edge **R ≥1.0** AND **R ≥2.0 underneath the slab**. (NCC 2022 required slab edge insulation only in CZ8 and for in-slab heating; under-slab insulation in CZ6–7 is new.)
- In-slab/in-screed heating or cooling: edge insulation R ≥1.0 retained (13.2.6(4)); exemption retained for in-screed systems solely in bathrooms/amenity areas (13.2.6(7)).
- Edge insulation must be water resistant and continuous from finished ground level to ≥300 mm depth or the full slab edge depth (13.2.6(6)).
- Metal-framed floors: Total R-Value per Table 13.2.6i (method accounting for bridging for suspended floors over enclosed subfloors; AS/NZS 4859.2 otherwise) or options in Table 13.2.6j.

### 13.7 Services — water heaters, electrification readiness, EV charging

- **13.7.7 rebuilt:** instead of deferring to PCA Volume Three Part B2, the PCD prescribes that a water heater in a new Class 1/10 building must be: a **solar water heater** or **heat pump water heater** meeting minimum small-scale technology certificates per Table 13.7.7a (e.g. 3–4 bedrooms: 19 STCs in 2022 stepping down to 13 in 2025) or minimum energy savings per AS/NZS 4234 (Table 13.7.7b: 40% small load for 1–2 bedrooms; 60% medium/large load); or a **gas water heater rated ≥5 stars** per AS 4552; or a **wood-fired thermosiphon/direct-fired heater** per AS/NZS 3500.4; or an **electric resistance water heater only in limited circumstances** — no storage or delivery ≤50 L per AS 1056.1, and the building has ≤1 bedroom with ≤1 such heater, or already has a compliant solar/heat-pump/gas heater, or the grid's greenhouse gas emission intensity is low.
- **NEW 13.7.10 Switchboard capacity (electrification readiness):** where gas is used for any domestic service, a minimum of **8 empty single-phase circuit breaker slots** must be provided in a switchboard (reducible to a floor of 4 where electric space heating/hot water/cooking already consume slots); remote switchboards must have supply sized for at least 32 A single-phase maximum demand.
- **NEW 13.7.11 Electric vehicle charging:** where one or more car parking spaces are provided, the main switchboard must have at least one single-phase circuit sized for a **32 A** load with active conductors **≥6 mm²**, terminating at a parking space with either a **≥15 A general purpose outlet labelled for EV charging** or EV charging equipment.
- Swimming pool heating (13.7.8) and spa heating (13.7.9) requirements substantively carried over from NCC 2022: solar (not electric-resistance boosted)/reclaimed energy/gas/heat pump; pool cover **R ≥0.05** + time switch where gas or heat pump heating is used; time switch on circulation pumps (spa ≥680 L).

## Condensation management — Part 10.8 proposed changes

- **10.8.1 external walls rewritten:** pliable membranes/sarking must comply with AS 4200.1, be installed per AS 4200.2, and sit on the exterior side of the primary insulation layer. Vapour permeance is now set by **Table 10.8.1** with both lower and upper bounds by climate zone and wall type — e.g. CZ1 no-cavity: ≥0.0022 to <1.1403 μg/N.s (low permeance to block inward vapour drive); CZ2/3 no-cavity ≥0.1429; CZ4/5 no-cavity ≥1.1403; **CZ6/7/8 walls without a drained and vented cavity are NOT PERMITTED (X)**; CZ6–8 with cavity ≥1.1403 μg/N.s. (NCC 2022 only set minima of 0.143 μg/N.s in CZ4–5 and 1.14 μg/N.s in CZ6–8.)
- **Drained and vented cavity specification (new 10.8.1(4)):** located between cladding and the outermost of the primary insulation layer/control layer/water barrier/sheathing; **≥18 mm wide**; drained to the exterior at the base (including vertically compartmentalised cavities in multi-storey); openings with free area **≥1,000 mm²/m of wall at the base and top of the cavity at each storey**; masonry open perpends per Part 5.7 acceptable if the cavity top also has ≥1,000 mm²/m openings.
- Exemptions (10.8.1(5)): single-skin masonry and single-skin concrete in CZ1–3; insulated sandwich panel walls; walls not part of the building envelope.
- **10.8.3 roof space ventilation rewritten and extended to climate zones 4 and 5** (NCC 2022 applied roof-space ventilation requirements only in CZ6–8): roofs with a ceiling not parallel to the roof plane need a roof space ≥**20 mm** high above the primary insulation layer (or above sarking ≥1.14 μg/N.s permeance, or above compliant ceiling insulation), ventilated per revised Table 10.8.3 — pitch <10°: **20,000 mm²/m** at the roof perimeter or two opposing ends (draft strikes the 2022 figure of 25,000 mm²/m); pitch ≥10° and <75°: **7,000 mm²/m at eaves/low level + 5,000 mm²/m at high level/ridge**, plus an additional 18,000 mm²/m at the eaves for cathedral ceilings; high-level openings within 900 mm of the ridge or 1/3 roof height below the highest point, whichever is less. Exemptions: concrete roofs, structural insulated sandwich panel roofs, BAL-FZ roofs.
- **NEW 10.8.4 — roof systems with a ceiling parallel to the roof plane (cathedral/skillion):** in CZ4–8, a ventilated cavity immediately above the primary insulation layer (or above a control layer/water barrier with vapour permeance ≥1.14 μg/N.s), unobstructed by insulation, **≥18 mm** measured perpendicular to the roof plane, ventilated with **≥20,000 mm²/m at eaves/low level and ≥5,000 mm²/m at high level/ridge**. Exemptions: concrete roofs, insulated sandwich panels, BAL-FZ, tiled roofs without a control layer/water barrier above the primary insulation.
- 10.8.2 exhaust/make-up air carried over: venting clothes dryers must duct directly outdoors; make-up air per AS 1668.2 for non-naturally-ventilated rooms (a 14,000 mm² opening ≈ 20 mm undercut on a 700 mm door).

## Other notable proposed changes (non-energy)

- **NEW Part 9.6 — Fire separation of roofed outdoor areas** (verandahs, alfresco areas, including under upper balconies): default separation 900 mm from boundary/Class 10/detached part, or 1.8 m from another building or roofed outdoor area; a concession (9.6.2(3)) allows down to 500 mm from a boundary / 1 m from another building if ≥2 sides open and ≥1/3 of perimeter open, non-combustible supporting structure/floor/ceiling/cladding (timber roof framing only if fully concealed by non-combustible linings), polycarbonate or non-combustible roof cover, no direct vertical support to the Class 1 building, and solid non-combustible fences/screens ≥1.5 m high with ≤100 mm ground clearance; insulated panels containing polystyrene are not permitted. Referenced from Volume Two H3D7.
- Wet areas (Part 10.2): shower areas restructured as enclosed/unenclosed with explicit options — stepdown ≥25 mm, hob, **level threshold**, or preformed shower base; floor waste falls 1:80–1:50; "compressed" deleted from fibre-cement references (AS/NZS 2908.2); waterstop/flashing details including vertical legs ≥25 mm above finished floor and vertical flashings ≥1800 mm.
- Structural: Importance Levels simplified to Levels 1 and 2 with design events table (e.g. IL2 non-cyclonic wind 1:500, region D cyclonic 1:1000); construction activity actions and termite actions added to the action list; stable rock site cut ratio 8:1; masonry articulation joints ≥10 mm at ≤6 m centres (openings ≤900×900) or ≤5 m centres (openings >900×900); structural steel corrosion protection tables (HDG75–HDG900, duplex, paint systems by corrosivity environment).
- Gutters (7.4.4): eaves gutters minimum fall 1:500, brackets ≤1.2 m centres, overflow volumes per Tables 7.4.4a/7.4.4b (e.g. 250 mm/h intensity, 10 m ridge-to-gutter → 0.69 L/s/m continuous); valley gutters ≥12.5° pitch with ≥15 mm freeboard and dimension table by rainfall intensity.
- Room heights (10.3.1) unchanged in substance: habitable 2.4 m, kitchen/corridors/bathrooms 2.1 m, attic habitable 2.2 m over 2/3 floor area, stairs 2.0 m.
- 11.2.7 NEW: fixed platforms/walkways/stairways/ladders for Class 10b structures may comply with AS 1657.
