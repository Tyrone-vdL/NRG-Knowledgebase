# QLD Building Act 1975 s 61 — Alterations Approved Under Earlier Building Assessment Provisions

## Summary

Section 61 of the Queensland Building Act 1975 lets a building certifier assess alterations to a safe existing building or structure against the *earlier* building assessment provisions (the provisions in force at an earlier time) rather than the current ones. This is the key Queensland concession that can allow alterations/additions to an existing dwelling to be assessed under an older BCA/NCC edition, provided the certifier is satisfied general safety and structural standards are not at risk. The screenshot is the full section text as published on AustLII (Queensland Consolidated Acts).

## Key claims

- Section heading: "Alterations to safe existing work may be approved on basis of earlier building assessment provisions" (Building Act 1975 (Qld), s 61).
- s 61(1): the section applies for a building development application if — (a) the building work is alterations to an existing building or structure; and (b) the building certifier is satisfied the general safety and structural standards of the building or structure would not be at risk if the alterations were to be carried out under earlier building assessment provisions.
- s 61(2): the building certifier may carry out building assessment work for the application on the basis that the building work is to be carried out under the earlier building assessment provisions.
- s 61(2A): however, subsection (2) does not apply to the extent a regulation mentioned in section 37(5) states an amendment of a building assessment provision does not apply — in that case the approval must be given under the building assessment provisions as amended.
- s 61(3): subsection (4) applies if the building's BCA classification as shown on the certificate of occupancy has changed only because of an amendment to building classifications under the BCA made after the certificate was given.
- s 61(4): without limiting subsection (2), the building certifier may carry out building assessment work on the basis that the building work is to be carried out under the building assessment provisions applying for the BCA classification shown on the certificate of occupancy.
- s 61(5) definitions: "building assessment provisions" includes the former Standard Building By-laws and Standard Building Law under this Act and the repealed Standard Building Regulation 1993; "earlier building assessment provisions" means the building assessment provisions as they were in force at a particular time before the application was made.
- Practical use for energy assessors: s 61 is the statutory basis on which a QLD certifier may accept an older NCC edition's energy-efficiency provisions for alterations to an existing dwelling (see also [[src-qld-building-act-1975-sect-81]] for the volume-based limit, and [[src-qld-building-act-s81-form-pinnacle]] for a certifier's application of both sections).
