# Top Tips for Improving Whole of Home Ratings (NatHERS Assessor Fact Sheet)

## Summary

Official NatHERS "Information for assessors" fact sheet (dated 22 March 2023) giving practical tips for improving a dwelling's Whole of Home rating and reducing energy use under NCC 2022. It positions the Whole of Home assessment as a streamlined NCC compliance pathway and lists the levers assessors can use — more efficient appliances, solar PV, batteries, and exceeding 7 stars on the thermal shell.

## Key claims

- The NatHERS Whole of Home rating provides households, for the first time, with information on which appliances have the biggest impact on energy bills and carbon footprint.
- Predicted annual greenhouse gas emissions and annual energy use from the assessment can be used to demonstrate whether a home is "net zero carbon" or "net zero energy".
- Homes that exceed 7 stars (thermal) are rewarded with a higher Whole of Home rating.
- Three tips for a higher Whole of Home rating: (1) select more energy efficient appliances, (2) add or increase solar PV (offsets daytime heating/cooling energy), (3) add a battery (reduces grid imports during peak energy use periods).
- A Whole of Home assessment takes only an extra 10-15 minutes on top of a thermal rating, making it a streamlined, flexible and cost-effective way to comply with the new NCC (2022) requirements.
- NatHERS Whole of Home allows a wider range of appliances, energy generation and storage options compared with the NCC Deemed-to-Satisfy elemental provisions.
- Assessors can trade off between technologies — e.g. more efficient appliances might reduce or remove the need for solar PV while still meeting NCC 2022 minimum requirements.
- Thermal performance above the 7-star minimum carries through: reduced heating/cooling loads applied to the Whole of Home assessment mean less efficient appliances or less solar PV are needed to comply.
- Whole of Home can demonstrate performance on a scale from minimum compliance up to homes generating more energy than they use.

## Concepts touched

NatHERS, Whole of Home, 7-Star, NCC 2022, Deemed-to-Satisfy, solar PV, batteries, appliances, net zero, assessors

## Caveats

- Dated 22 March 2023; audience is NatHERS accredited assessors. Tips are qualitative — no specific R-values, system sizes or rating thresholds are given.
- NCC version context is NCC 2022; does not reflect any later NCC amendments.
