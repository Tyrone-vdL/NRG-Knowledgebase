# ABCB Livable Housing Design Standard 2022 (v1.2, 6 April 2023)

## Summary

The ABCB Livable Housing Design Standard 2022, version 1.2 (released 6 April 2023), the regulatory standard called up by NCC 2022 Volume One Part G7 and Volume Two Part H8 that sets the minimum accessibility provisions for Class 1a dwellings and Class 2 sole-occupancy units — adapted from the 'Silver' level of the Livable Housing Design Guidelines. It contains the six Parts (dwelling access, dwelling entrance, internal doors/corridors, sanitary compartment, shower, wall reinforcement) with exact dimensional requirements.

## Key claims

- **Part 1 — Dwelling access (Class 1a only):** a continuous step-free path to a dwelling entrance door from the allotment-boundary pedestrian entry, an appurtenant Class 10a garage/carport, or an exclusive-use parking space; path max gradient **1:14** (crossfall ≤1:40), minimum width **1000 mm**; gates min clear opening **820 mm**; ramp landings at intervals ≤9 m (1:14) or ≤15 m (1:20), landings ≥1200 mm long and level (≤1:40); one step ramp allowed (height ≤190 mm, gradient ≤1:10, length ≤1900 mm).
- **Clause 1.2 — Parking space on the access path:** minimum unobstructed space **3200 mm wide × 5400 mm long**, gradient ≤1:33 (bitumen) or ≤1:40 (other surfaces).
- **Part 2 — Dwelling entrance:** at least one entrance door with minimum clear opening width **820 mm** (an 870 mm door leaf generally achieves this); threshold level, or sill ≤5 mm (rounded/bevelled), or ramped threshold ≤1:8 within the jamb depth; where weatherproofing demands a raised sill — no lip/upstand >15 mm within the sill profile and ≤5 mm height difference to adjoining surface; landing area **1200 mm × 1200 mm** on the arrival side (level or ≤1:40); the entrance need not be the front door.
- **Clause 2.4 — Weatherproofing options for step-free entrances:** channel drain complying with Volume Two H2D2 for the entrance width; or drainage surface below decking with gaps ≤8 mm (or as permitted by AS 3959 in bushfire areas); or a roof covering ≥1200 mm × 1200 mm with fall away from the building ≤1:40.
- **Part 3 — Internal doors and corridors (ground/entry level):** internal doorways serving habitable rooms, laundries, compliant sanitary compartments/showers and connected garages must have **820 mm** minimum clear opening; thresholds level/≤5 mm/ramped ≤1:8; corridors connected to such doors minimum clear width **1000 mm**.
- **Part 4 — Sanitary compartment:** at least one on the ground or entry level; clear width **≥900 mm** between walls either side of the pan (separate compartments); fixed obstructions ≥450 mm from pan centreline; clear circulation space **1200 mm × 900 mm** forward of the pan, clear of door swing.
- **Part 5 — Shower:** at least one shower with **hobless and step-free entry**; a lip ≤5 mm for water retention is permitted and is not a step. The compliant shower need not be on the entry level.
- **Part 6 — Wall reinforcement:** walls of the Part 4 sanitary compartment, Part 5 shower and any bath (except freestanding) must have reinforcement for future grabrails — minimum **12 mm structural plywood**, **25 mm timber noggings**, or light-gauge steel noggings/plate per the NASH Standard; not required where walls are concrete/masonry capable of supporting grabrails; specific nogging/sheeting locations per Figures 6.2a–6.2g (e.g. shower reinforcement band 900–1850 mm zones; wall adjacent to pan within 460 mm of centreline).
- Version history: v1.0 (1 May 2022), v1.1 (19 Dec 2022), v1.2 (6 April 2023) — both updates were corrections/clarifications with **no policy change**.
- The Standard is not intended to achieve equivalence with AS 1428.1 and is not for specialist accommodation; a non-mandatory 'Gold' level version was also planned by the ABCB.

## Caveats

- Version 1.2 (6 April 2023). State adoption varies — e.g. Tasmania's application of Part H8 is governed by a Director's Determination (per NCC 2022 Amendment 1), WA deferred adoption, NSW did not adopt the livable housing provisions; check jurisdictional adoption before applying.
- NCC 2025 PCD proposes new H8D2 exemptions for steep sites (see [[src-ncc-2025-pcd-volume-two-2]]) that interact with Clause 1.1 of this Standard.
- Figures (2.1, 4.2, 6.2a–6.2g) carry dimension detail that partially scrambled in extraction; dimensions quoted above are from the unambiguous text.
