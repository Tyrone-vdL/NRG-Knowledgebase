# NatHERS Heating and Cooling Load Limits Standard 2022 (ABCB)

## Summary

ABCB Standard (2022 edition) containing the separate heating and cooling load limit tables (in MJ/m².annum) that a dwelling must satisfy — in addition to its star rating — when using the NCC 2022 NatHERS compliance pathway, referenced by clause J3D3 of NCC Volume One and Specification 42 of NCC Volume Two. The limits are set per NatHERS climate zone (69 zones), per floor type (concrete slab-on-ground vs suspended floor) and per target star band (7, 6.5 and 6 stars), and were developed using the 2022 NatHERS climate files and star bands to reflect the NCC 2022 7-star stringency increase. Load limits do not apply in NSW, NT, Tasmania and parts of Queensland and WA.

## Key claims

- The NatHERS compliance pathway requires a building to achieve the minimum star rating AND satisfy the separate heating and cooling load limits corresponding to that star rating (clause 2.2). Limits are an additional requirement, not a substitute.
- Applies to Class 1 buildings, Class 2 sole-occupancy units (SOUs) and Class 4 parts of buildings. Referenced in DtS provisions J3D3 (NCC Volume One) and Specification 42 (NCC Volume Two). Not a stand-alone requirement — must be read with the NCC.
- Units are MJ/m².annum (clause 1.5). Heating/cooling load = the heat energy NatHERS software calculates must be added to/removed from a space to maintain an acceptable temperature range.
- Load limits do NOT apply in NSW (BASIX already has its own heating/cooling caps), NT, Tasmania, and parts of Queensland and WA — these climate zones are shown as N/A in the tables (clauses 1.2, 2.1).
- Targets covered: Class 1 must achieve NatHERS 7 stars, but 6.5 stars is allowed with an outdoor living area, and 6 stars with an outdoor living area plus ceiling fan, in NCC climate zones 1 and 2; Class 2 SOUs and Class 4 parts must achieve 7 stars on average with a 6-star minimum per SOU (clause 1.2).
- For Class 1 buildings, the applicable limits are determined by the floor type of the **lowest living area** (e.g. ground floor for a two-storey house, or first floor if the ground floor is a garage). Tables 1/3/5 apply to Concrete Slab-on-Ground (CSOG); Tables 2/4/6 apply to Suspended Floor (timber or concrete) or a mixture of CSOG and SF (clauses 2.3-2.4).
- For Class 2/Class 4: regardless of floor type, the average of all SOUs' loads must not exceed the 7-star limits (Table 7) and the worst-performing SOU must not exceed the 6-star limits (Table 8).
- Example 7-star Class 1 CSOG limits (Table 1): NatHERS climate zone 24 (ACT/Vic, Canberra region) heating 129 / cooling 34 MJ/m².annum; zone 21 (Vic) heating 48 / cooling 41; zone 60 (Vic) heating 95 / cooling 27; zone 66 (Vic) heating 169 / cooling 21; zone 13 (WA) heating 53 / cooling 34.
- The 6.5-star and 6-star Class 1 tables (Tables 3-6) only contain values for Queensland climate zones 7, 9 and 10 — consistent with the outdoor-living-area concessions being limited to NCC climate zones 1 and 2; all other zones are N/A.
- Heating and cooling load limits were first introduced in NCC 2019; the 2022 limits were redeveloped using the 2022 NatHERS climate files and star bands. Technical basis: Tony Isaacs Consulting, "Developing new heating and cooling load limits for updated NatHERS weather data" (2021), available on the NatHERS website.

## Caveats

- Table layout is degraded in text extraction — climate zone, state, heating and cooling figures run together (e.g. "24ACT, Vic12934" = zone 24, ACT/Vic, 129 heating / 34 cooling). The example values quoted above were parsed from unambiguous rows; verify any figure against the source PDF before relying on it for a compliance decision.
- 2022 edition aligned to NCC 2022; check abcb.gov.au for amendments and for any NCC 2025 PCD changes.
- Does not apply in NSW (BASIX governs), NT, Tas and parts of Qld/WA — always confirm jurisdiction before applying a limit.
