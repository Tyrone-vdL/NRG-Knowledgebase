# Why Choose Specification 44 over NatHERS? (Explainer)

## Summary

A one-page explainer listing six reasons a project might use NCC Specification 44 (first-principles thermal load limits) instead of the NatHERS star-rating pathway for residential energy efficiency compliance, plus when not to use it. Core argument: Spec 44 gives explicit, quantifiable thermal load targets in MJ/m²/year and design flexibility for buildings that model poorly in NatHERS, at the cost of more complex modelling and documentation.

## Key claims

- **Greater design flexibility** — Spec 44 accommodates creative shapes, orientations and non-standard materials that may not rate well under NatHERS.
- **Transparent, quantifiable targets** — Spec 44 sets explicit thermal load targets in **MJ/m²/year**, versus NatHERS's star-rating proxy, making performance goals easier to track and validate.
- **Fallback for non-compliant NatHERS designs** — a design that cannot reach the required **7-star rating** may still comply via Spec 44 if it meets the defined heating and cooling load limits.
- **Performance-first alignment** — focuses on actual energy performance rather than star-based proxies; claimed to align better with carbon accounting, Net Zero goals and lifecycle analysis.
- **Supports innovation** — alternative construction types that model poorly in NatHERS (e.g. earth buildings, SIPs) may be easier to justify through Spec 44.
- **Possible cost/time saving** — projects with in-house modelling capability may avoid full third-party NatHERS certification.
- **When NOT to use it** — Spec 44 requires more complex modelling and documentation; for standard homes NatHERS is usually quicker and cheaper, and building certifiers are more familiar with the NatHERS pathway.
