# ACT Appendix to the BCA (NCC 2022 Schedule, from 15 Jan 2024) — Key Variations

## Summary

Schedule 2 of the Building (ACT Appendix to the Building Code) Determination 2023 (No 3) — the NCC 2022-based ACT Appendix to BCA Volumes 1 and 2 that applies to ACT building approvals determined on or after 15 January 2024, with detailed ACT-specific energy efficiency, livable housing and condensation provisions for alterations to existing buildings. Its centrepiece for energy assessors is ACT Part H6 (ACT H6D10–H6D14): the ACT's compliance pathways and concessions for house extensions/alterations, including the window-treatment U-value credit table and the exclusion of H6P2 (Whole-of-Home) from alterations.

## Key claims

- **Definitions:** an "alteration" is building work on an existing building per s 6 Building Act 2004 (includes additions/extensions and internal layout changes; excludes basic building work per s 10). "Substantial alteration" is defined in s 23 of the Building (General) Regulation 2008.
- **ACT H6D10 — alterations pathways:** H6P1 and H6P2 for an alteration are satisfied by either (a) **NatHERS pathway** — house energy rating software per Vol 2 S42C2(1)(a) applied to the **entire house (altered + unaltered parts)**, with the S42C2(2) separate heating/cooling load limits NOT required, H6P2(1) energy value NOT required, plus Housing Provisions 13.2.2 (fabric), Part 13.4 (sealing) and Part 13.7 (services); or (b) **DtS elemental pathway** — Housing Provisions Parts 13.2 (fabric), 13.3 (glazing/shading), 13.4 (sealing) and 13.7 (services), each subject to ACT concessions H6D11–H6D14.
- **ACT H6P2 carve-out:** H6P2(1) (energy value of domestic services, i.e. the Whole-of-Home requirement) **does not apply to alterations to an existing building** in the ACT.
- **House energy rating software may not be used on the extension alone** — only whole-of-building modelling (new + existing) is permitted; hypothetical upgrades of existing elements (e.g. assuming old glazing performs like new) are NOT permitted in the ACT — actual performance of existing elements must be assessed.
- **ACT H6D11 (fabric):** at the interface where a new wall/floor/ceiling/roof joins an existing element, the new insulation need not form a continuous thermal barrier with the existing element (per 13.2.2(1)(a)/(b)) unless the existing element also requires new insulation.
- **ACT H6D12 (glazing):** whole-storey glazing assessment includes existing glazing in the unaltered part, but the Total System U-Value of existing glazing may be improved by window treatments per **Table ACT H6D12(a)** (e.g. a U7.8 glazing unit improves to 6.32 with Holland blinds or closed-weave curtains, 5.46 with heavy drapes, 4.20 with closed-weave curtains + pelmet, 2.18 with heavy drapes + pelmet; a U3.0 unit improves to 2.75/2.75/2.58/2.26/1.51 respectively), or by closed shutters per Annex G of ISO 10077-1.
- Table notes set strict qualifying criteria: curtains/drapes must fully cover the window and enclose the air layer (abut reveals/sill or overlap side edges ≥150 mm and abut the floor; "abut" = within 10 mm); pelmets must be box pelmets overlapping the curtain top by ≥50 mm; open-weave curtains give negligible benefit.
- **ACT H6D12(3) winter glazing concession:** if an alteration cannot incorporate north-sector translucent glazing of at least 1 m² or 1% of the alteration's floor area (whichever greater, unshadowed in winter), all glazing in the storey need not meet the Part 13.3.2 aggregate conductance ("Winter Outcomes" in the 2022 ABCB Glazing Calculator) requirements provided the alteration's floor area is ≤50 m² AND bulk roof insulation of the unaltered part has been topped up to **minimum R5.0**.
- **ACT H6D12(4) isolated glazing:** isolated glazing units (in a zone enclosed by walls and doors) need not comply per the Alternative requirements for unaltered parts Determination; the storey area entered in the 2022 glazing calculator must be reduced by each zone area ΔA.
- **ACT H6D13 (sealing) and H6D14 (services):** concessions allow re-use of relocated/pre-existing evaporative coolers without self-closing dampers (if outlets serving heated spaces have automatic or readily accessible manual closing means), and re-use of pre-existing heaters, pumps, water heaters and lighting that pre-date Parts 13.7.5–13.7.8, provided new ducting/piping within the extension complies with 13.7.3/13.7.4 and new lights comply with 13.7.6.
- **Energy metrics replace emissions:** in Volume One verification methods (J1V1 NABERS, J1V2 Green Star, J1V3/J1V5 reference building, Spec 34), all references to annual greenhouse gas emissions are replaced with "annual modelled energy use" because since 2020 the ACT's electricity is renewable or offset; electric heater options are correspondingly permitted (ACT J6D10(1)(e)).
- **ACT Part H8 (livable housing) for alterations:** basic building work is exempt; altered front/main entrances must comply with Part 2 (heritage-listed facades exempt; exemption if another compliant entrance exists); altered internal doors/corridors must not be narrower than original where full compliance is impossible due to space restrictions; new ground/entry-level sanitary compartments must comply with Part 4 unless another compliant one exists (concrete slab modification and space restriction exemptions); altered showers must comply with Part 5 unless slab or unplanned structural work would be needed; Part 6 wall reinforcement applies to those altered rooms; unaltered parts and Part 1 (dwelling access) do not apply to alterations.
- Condensation: from 15 January 2024 H4P7, H4V5 and H4D9 of NCC 2022 Volume Two apply in the ACT (replacing P2.4.7/V2.4.7/Part 3.8.7 of NCC 2019 Amd 1).

## Caveats

- This PDF is the Schedule 2 extract of the Building (ACT Appendix to the Building Code) Determination 2023 (No 3) — it applies only to ACT building approvals determined **on or after 15 January 2024**; jurisdiction is the ACT only.
- The determination is a disallowable instrument "as amended from time to time" — check the ACT Legislation Register (legislation.act.gov.au) for any later determination (No 3 was made 13 December 2023 and revoked No 2).
