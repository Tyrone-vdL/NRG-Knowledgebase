# How to Navigate the 7 Star NatHERS Upgrade in 2023 (Certified Energy Guide)

## Summary

Industry guide by consultancy Certified Energy explaining the 2023 transition from 6 to 7 star minimum NatHERS ratings under NCC 2022, with cost-benefit data from Sustainability Victoria's 7-star upgrade analysis and a state-by-state table of enforcement dates. Useful for upgrade cost estimates ($10,000-$15,000 depending on orientation) and the state cutoff dates as understood in 2023.

## Key claims

- NCC 2022 (triennial edition) increased the minimum NatHERS standard from 6 to 7 stars nationwide, with a 12-month transition agreed by federal ministers to commence 1 October 2023.
- New heating and cooling load limits accompany the 7-star increase; these load limits do not apply in the Northern Territory, Tasmania and parts of Queensland and Western Australia, while NSW covers separate heating/cooling load limits through BASIX.
- The new Whole of Home rating measures whole-home energy use including appliances, solar and batteries, as a separate rating out of 100, against the NCC annual energy use budget.
- Sustainability Victoria case studies estimate $10,000-$15,000 extra cost to introduce additional insulation, shading and glazing to comply with 7 stars.
- Cost table (200 sqm example house): best orientation $19.85-$48.22 per sqm (average $43.96/sqm, ~$8,792); worst orientation $40.58-$71.52 per sqm (average $76.34/sqm, ~$15,268); average orientation cost difference $3,555. Utility bills approximately $183 lower per year per the NatHERS website.
- Homeowners building with solar and no gas connection can save approximately $1,000 per year, paying back upgrade costs within roughly 10 years.
- Specifying branded (custom) windows rather than default windows can improve NatHERS performance by up to half a star, because default window U and SHGC values are relaxed to accommodate many manufacturers.
- Six low-cost improvement tips: orientation (living rooms/kitchen north, garages west, bedrooms east, bathrooms south), colour selection (lighter colours north of Sydney latitude, darker for southern states), shading devices, deleting non-IC4 downlights/roof windows/skylights, ceiling fans in all rooms, and better windows.
- An early "value engineering" study at concept design (wall-to-floor and glass-to-floor ratios) costs approximately $400 per assessment and can avoid thousands in construction costs.
- State cutoff dates (as published): QLD voluntary from 1 Oct 2022, transition until 1 Oct 2023; NSW 1 Oct 2023 (BASIX rises from 5.5 to 7 stars for new homes and renovations over $50,000; BASIX certificates have 90-day currency); ACT 1 Oct 2023 (plus minimum accessibility standards); VIC commences 1 May 2023 with transition to 1 Oct 2023; TAS not until 2025; SA compulsory from 1 Oct 2023; WA full effect 1 May 2023 with transition until 1 Oct 2023; NT not adopting, continuing with 2009 ABCB standards.

## Concepts touched

NatHERS, 7-Star, Whole of Home, NCC 2022, BASIX, climate zones, insulation, glazing, orientation, load limits, state adoption dates, costs

## Caveats

- Commercial marketing guide by Certified Energy (2023), not a government source; verify state dates and load-limit claims against current regulator publications — several have since changed (e.g. WA/TAS timing).
- The cost table's per-sqm figures appear internally inconsistent (the "average" exceeds the "maximum" in both rows) — table layout degraded in extraction; verify figures against the source PDF and the Sustainability Victoria analysis.
- The "Climate Zone: 60" label in the case study appears to be a typographical/extraction artefact (Craigieburn is NatHERS climate zone 60 — a NatHERS zone, not an NCC climate zone).
