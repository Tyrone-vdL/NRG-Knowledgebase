# ABCB Handbook — NCC Volume Two Energy Efficiency Provisions 2016 (historic edition) (surgical extract)

## Summary

This is the ABCB's non-mandatory handbook (third edition, 2016) explaining the **NCC 2016 Volume Two Part 3.12 Deemed-to-Satisfy energy efficiency provisions** for Class 1 houses and Class 10 buildings — the era of the **6-star minimum**, Performance Requirements P2.6.1 (thermal performance) and P2.6.2 (services/GHG), Verification Method V2.6.2.2 (reference building), and DtS Parts 3.12.0–3.12.5 (application, building fabric, external glazing, building sealing, air movement, services). It is a historic edition: relevant only to buildings approved under BCA/NCC 2016-era provisions and for understanding how today's rules evolved — the equivalent current provisions are NCC 2022 Part H6 / Spec 42 / Spec 44 and the ABCB Housing Provisions Section 13, with a 7-star minimum plus Whole-of-Home.

1 Introduction; 2 Definition of terms; 3 Energy efficiency (GHG background, climate philosophy); 4 Introduction to the performance-based NCC (assessment methods, verification, expert judgement); 5 Performance Requirements (P2.6.1, P2.6.2); 6 Verification Method V2.6.2.2; 7 Part 3.12.0 — Application of Part 3.12 (heating/cooling loads, star ratings); 8 Part 3.12.1 — Building Fabric (insulation, roofs, roof lights, external walls, floors, attached Class 10a); 9 Part 3.12.2 — External Glazing (glazing calculator, shading); 10 Part 3.12.3 — Building Sealing (chimneys, roof lights, windows/doors, exhaust fans, construction, evaporative coolers); 11 Part 3.12.4 — Air Movement (ventilation openings, ceiling fans); 12 Part 3.12.5 — Services (insulation of services, central heating piping, ductwork, electric resistance heating, artificial lighting, water heaters, pool/spa); 13 State and Territory administration (including alterations/additions); 14 Further reading; Appendix A — NCC building classifications.

## What the 2016 DtS provisions covered — key claims

- **Two DtS pathways for P2.6.1** (3.12.0): **Option 1 — energy rating approach** using NatHERS-accredited software (plus 3.12.1.1 fabric insulation, thermal breaks 3.12.1.2(c)/3.12.1.4(b), ceiling insulation loss compensation 3.12.1.2(e), floor edge insulation 3.12.1.5(c)–(d), and Part 3.12.3 sealing); or **Option 2 — elemental provisions** (Parts 3.12.1 fabric + 3.12.2 glazing/shading + 3.12.3 sealing + 3.12.4 air movement). Either way, **P2.6.2 (services, "reducing greenhouse gas emissions") is satisfied by Part 3.12.5**. The energy rating approach had been relocated from former Verification Method V2.6.2.1 into the DtS Provisions.
- **Star rating requirement (3.12.0.1): minimum 6 stars.** Outdoor living area concession in **climate zones 1 or 2 only**: 0.5-star credit each for (a) a fully covering impervious roof with **Total R-Value ≥1.5 (downward heat flow)**, and (b) **at least one permanently installed ceiling fan** complying with 3.12.4.3 — i.e. minimum becomes **5.5 stars with one condition met, 5 stars with both**. (Compare NCC 2022 S42C2: same credit structure shifted to 7/6.5/6 stars — see [[src-ncc-2022-vol-2-h6-energy-efficiency]].)
- **Outdoor living area definition (3.12.0.1(b)–(c))** — essentially identical to today's S42C2(3)–(4): directly adjoining and accessible from a general purpose living area (not sleeping/study/home theatre); floor area **≥12.0 m²**; length and width each **≥2.5 m**; opening height above floor **≥2.1 m**; one side permanently open + a second side permanently open or readily openable; open sides **≥900 mm** from a boundary or breeze-path obstruction.
- NatHERS software recognised **69 climates**, vs **8 NCC climate zones** for the elemental approach; loads measured in MJ/m².annum of conditioned floor area.
- **Roofs (3.12.1.2):** minimum Total R-Values from Table 3.12.1.1a vary by climate zone, altitude, heat-flow direction and **roof solar absorptance** (typical values quoted: dark grey slate 0.90; red/green 0.75; yellow/buff 0.60; dull zincalume/galvanised 0.55; light grey 0.45; off white 0.35; light cream 0.30 — lighter roofs get reduced R-Value requirements). Worked example: climate zone 6, light cream pitched metal roof (absorptance 0.30), upwards heat flow → minimum Total R-Value **4.6**; CZ 1 example with absorptance 0.4 and >0.5% ceiling penetrations → **R4.1** for the roof/ceiling. Pitched roof with flat ceiling: ≥50% of added insulation laid on the ceiling. A **R0.5 concession** applies in CZ 1–5 where all insulation is on the ceiling and the roof space is ventilated (passive or wind-driven ventilators).
- **Thermal breaks:** required where a metal framing member connects outer roof cladding to inner lining (or no inner lining), and similarly for walls — material **R-Value ≥0.2** (20 mm timber, 12 mm EPS strips, plywood, or bulk insulation deemed R0.2). Reflective insulation alone is NOT acceptable as a thermal break (needs an adjoining airspace). Without a break, insulation effectiveness can halve.
- Reflective insulation R-value contributions determined per **AS/NZS 4859.1** (emittance, airspace dimensions, ventilation); inherent Total R-Values of typical roof/ceiling constructions in Figure 3.12.1.1 (e.g. unventilated pitched metal roof + flat ceiling, upwards flow: 0.39).
- **External glazing (Part 3.12.2):** elemental glazing provisions with aggregate allowances by climate zone and orientation, supported by the ABCB **Glazing Calculator**; shading provisions in 3.12.2.2.
- **Building sealing (Part 3.12.3):** chimneys/flues, roof lights, external windows and doors (weather seals), exhaust fans, roof/wall/floor construction, evaporative coolers.
- **Air movement (Part 3.12.4):** ventilation opening requirements and ceiling fan/evaporative cooler provisions (3.12.4.3 — the clause the outdoor-living ceiling fan credit hooks into).
- **Services (Part 3.12.5):** insulation of services, central heating water piping, heating/cooling ductwork, electric resistance space heating limits, artificial lighting, water heaters, pool/spa heating and pumping. **Lighting power allowances: 5 W/m² inside the Class 1 dwelling; 4 W/m² for an attached verandah/balcony; 3 W/m² for an associated Class 10a garage.** Illumination power density adjustment factors (Table 3.12.5.3) reward control devices — e.g. a manual dimmer controlling ≥75% of a space gives factor 0.85 (5 ÷ 0.85 = 5.9 W/m² allowance). Gas water heaters: rated **not less than 5 stars per AS 4552**.
- **Verification Method V2.6.2.2** offered a reference-building comparison route (heating/cooling loads of proposed vs reference building) as an alternative to DtS — the ancestor of NCC 2022's H6V2.
- The handbook traces stringency history: housing increased to **5-star** equivalence (BCA 2006-era), then COAG's December 2009 National Strategy on Energy Efficiency drove the increase to **6 stars in BCA 2010** — carried through to NCC 2016.

## Caveats

- **HISTORIC EDITION — superseded twice.** This handbook documents NCC 2016 Volume Two Part 3.12 (6-star regime). NCC 2019 amended these provisions, and **NCC 2022 replaced the structure entirely** (Part H6, Specification 42/44, ABCB Housing Provisions Section 13, 7 stars + Whole-of-Home). Use ONLY for buildings approved under BCA/NCC 2016-era provisions (e.g. assessing existing dwellings or alterations referencing the original approval), for historical comparison, or for training. For new work, see [[src-ncc-2022-vol-2-h6-energy-efficiency]].
- The handbook is **non-mandatory guidance** explaining intent — never a substitute for the NCC clauses themselves; jurisdictional variations applied.
- Table layouts (R-value tables, absorptance table, lighting examples) partially degraded in text extraction; verify figures against the source PDF before quoting in client work.
