# ABCB Handbook — Energy Efficiency NCC Volume One 2019 (Section J commercial) (surgical extract)

## Summary

This is the ABCB's non-mandatory guidance handbook (June 2019, version 6.0) explaining the intent and application of the **NCC 2019 Volume One Section J** energy efficiency provisions for Class 2–9 (commercial and multi-residential) buildings — the JP1 Performance Requirement, the JV1–JV4 Verification Methods (NABERS, Green Star, reference building, envelope sealing), and the Deemed-to-Satisfy Parts J0–J8 (fabric, sealing, air-conditioning/ventilation, lighting, heated water/pools, energy monitoring). Section J was significantly rewritten for NCC 2019, and this handbook was rewritten to match. It is guidance only (not regulatory), and it documents the **2019 edition** — superseded by NCC 2022 Section J for new work.

## Key claims (NCC 2019 edition)

- **JP1 is the only mandatory energy efficiency requirement in NCC 2019 Volume One.** A building including its services must facilitate efficient energy use appropriate to function/use, comfort, solar radiation (utilised for heating, controlled for cooling), energy source, and envelope sealing.
- **JP1(f) quantified targets** — hourly regulated energy consumption of a conditioned space, averaged over annual operating hours, must not exceed: **Class 6: 80 kJ/m².hr**; **Class 5, 7b, 8, 9a (non-ward) and 9b schools: 43 kJ/m².hr**; **all other classifications: 15 kJ/m².hr**. JP1(f) does NOT apply to SOUs of Class 2 buildings or Class 4 parts (no quantified targets developed).
- **Regulated energy** = energy consumed by building services (everything covered by Parts J1–J7, including lifts) less on-site-generated-and-used renewables; excludes emergency systems, cooking, plug loads, process loads, and EV charging stations.
- Energy modelling for a direct JP1 Performance Solution should comply with **ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 140 (2007)**, run hourly time-steps over a full year with representative local weather data; lift modelling per ISO 25745-2:2014.
- JP1 uses energy (kJ/m².hr) whereas JV1–JV3 shifted to **GHG emissions (kgCO₂-e)**; emissions factors in Table 3a of Specification JVb.
- **Verification Methods:** JV1 = NABERS Energy for Offices; JV2 = Green Star; JV3 = reference building comparison; **JV4 = building envelope sealing** — air permeability tested per **Method 1 of AS/NZS ISO 9972** (blower door, only possible post-construction): Class 2 and Class 4 parts: **≤10 m³/hr.m² at 50 Pa, all climate zones**; Class 5/6/8/9a(non-ward)/9b: **≤5 m³/hr.m², climate zones 1, 7, 8 only**; Class 3, 9a ward areas, 9c: **≤5 m³/hr.m², climate zones 1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8**. JV4 not applicable to Class 7. Some climate zones are excluded because air tightness there can increase energy consumption.
- **Part J0 application split (J0.1):** SOUs of Class 2 buildings / Class 4 parts use the **NatHERS energy-rating pathway (J0.2–J0.5)**; all other classifications (and Class 2 common areas) use the **elemental pathway (Parts J1 and J3)**. All buildings, including SOUs, must comply with the building services Parts (J5–J8) because house energy rating software does not assess services.
- **J0.2 star ratings (NCC 2019):** Class 2 SOUs — **average 6 stars across all SOUs, individual minimum 5 stars**; Class 4 part — **minimum 6 stars** (jurisdictional variations may apply). Ratings use NatHERS-accredited software with the load limits in the ABCB Standard for NatHERS Heating and Cooling Load Limits. The rating pathway must additionally satisfy J1.2 (thermal construction), J0.4/J0.5 (thermal breaks), J1.6(b)–(d) (floor edge insulation) and Part J3 (sealing).
- **J0.3 ceiling fans** (where part of a house energy rating solution): permanently installed with speed control; a single fan serving ≤15 m² needs blade rotation diameter **≥900 mm**; serving ≤25 m² needs **≥1,200 mm**.
- **J0.4 roof thermal breaks:** metal sheet roof on metal purlins/rafters/battens without ceiling lining (or lining fixed directly to them) requires a thermal break of **R-Value ≥ R0.2** at all contact points. **J0.5 wall thermal breaks:** lightweight-clad metal-framed walls similarly require an **R0.2** thermal break where cladding contacts the frame.
- **Part J1 (fabric) / J1.5 wall-glazing construction:** NCC 2019 introduced assessment of walls and glazing together as a **wall-glazing construction** with **Total System U-Value and solar admittance** provisions (glazing values per AFRC protocols), plus Total R-Value provisions for the wall component; applies to envelope walls including internal envelope walls. Total R-Values must be calculated per **AS/NZS 4859.2** (accounting for thermal bridging/compression). Typical masonry wall component R-values illustrated (outdoor air film 0.04, masonry 0.17, cavity/airspace 0.17, plasterboard 0.06, indoor air film 0.12).
- **Part J3 (sealing)** covers chimneys/flues, roof lights, external windows and doors, exhaust fans, ceiling/wall/floor construction and evaporative coolers.
- **Part J6 lighting:** Class 2 SOU allowance **5 W/m²** (lamp power density or illumination power density), verandah/balcony **4 W/m²**; only permanently wired lights counted; halogen and fluorescent lamps separately switched; control devices (motion sensors, dimming, time switches — Table J6.2b) earn increased allowances. Other classes (3, 5–9) use maximum illumination power density values from Table J6.2a (e.g. laboratory 6 W/m²), based on AS 1680 lighting levels, with room-aspect-ratio and control-device adjustment factors.
- Energy efficiency provisions have been in the BCA since 2006 as part of the national GHG-reduction strategy; the handbook traces this history and the climate-zone basis of the provisions.

## Caveats

- **NCC 2019 edition — superseded.** This handbook explains Section J as written in NCC 2019 (Parts J0–J8, JP1, JV1–JV4). NCC 2022 restructured and re-stringencied Volume One energy efficiency (new Section J numbering J1–J9, Class 2 SOU thermal minimum lifted to 7 stars + Whole-of-Home). Use only for buildings approved under NCC 2019, for historical context, or for training; verify any clause against the NCC edition applying to the project.
- The handbook is explicitly **non-mandatory guidance** — it explains intent and does not set out the specific NCC requirements; compliance with it does not discharge legal obligations.
- Published June 2019 (print version 6.0); commercial (Vol One) focus — Class 1 dwellings are covered by the companion Volume Two handbook.
- Table layouts (e.g. JV4 air permeability table, wall R-value figures) partially degraded in text extraction; verify figures against the source PDF.
