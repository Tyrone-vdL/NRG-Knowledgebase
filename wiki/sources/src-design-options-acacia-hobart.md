# Design For Place — Acacia House Design Options (Hobart)

## Summary

Australian Government Design For Place pack giving the building specifications that take the Acacia reference house (2 bed, 2 bath, 2 living, 123 m² single-storey with two courtyards) to 6.1, 7.2 and 8.1 NatHERS stars in the Hobart (cold) climate. The cold-climate levers differ from Brisbane: getting from 6.1 to 7.2 swaps the waffle pod slab for R1.8 under-slab insulation, reduces eaves from 1000 mm to 600 mm to admit more winter sun, and moves from thermally broken aluminium to uPVC double glazing — low-e double glazing in uPVC delivers 8.1. Companion to the Acacia Hobart Technical Drawings and the Brisbane/Melbourne versions of the same design.

## Key claims

- Star rating options modelled for Hobart: 6.1, 7.2 and 8.1. The 6.1 specs are the 'Hobart – Option 1' design; 7.2 and 8.1 are 'Hobart – Option 2', which adds cold-climate design tweaks (eave depth and shading).
- On average a 7 star home in Hobart is predicted to use 27% less heating/cooling energy than a 6 star home; an 8 star home 54% less. The Acacia 7.2 requires 32% less energy than a 6 star house; the 8.1 requires 57% less.
- Floor: 100 mm concrete slab with 300 mm waffle pod for the 6.1 option; 100 mm concrete slab on ground with R1.8 under-slab insulation for the 7.2 and 8.1 options. Finishes: burnished concrete to living areas, carpet to bedrooms, ceramic tiles to wet areas.
- Ceiling (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R5.0 batt insulation.
- Roof (all options): metal sheet roofing on timber battens with R1.5 reflective foil laminate.
- Eaves: 1000 mm overhang for the 6.1 design; reduced to 600 mm for the 7.2 and 8.1 designs to further improve solar gain in a cold climate.
- External walls (all options): lightweight cladding on battens, reflective foil membrane (vapour permeable if appropriate), timber frame, R2.5 batt insulation, 10 mm plasterboard.
- Internal walls (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R2.5 batt insulation.
- Windows: double glazed with thermally broken aluminium frame (6.1); double glazed with uPVC frame (7.2); double glazed, low emissivity glass, uPVC frame (8.1). Note even the lowest Hobart option uses double glazing — Hobart is the only city of the three where single glazing never appears.
- Draught sealing (all options): weather strips to all windows and sliding doors; seals/weather-strips to hinged external doors; sealed exhaust fans to bathroom, powder room and kitchen rangehood.
- Ceiling fans (all options): 1400 mm diameter — one in each bedroom, one in the dining area, one in the front living area.
- Approximately 150 m² of roof space is available for solar panels (vs 170 m² quoted for Brisbane/Melbourne); ceiling heights 2.7 m.
- uPVC window frames have similar insulating properties to timber frames; burnished concrete thermal mass works particularly well in cool locations but is degraded by coverings such as carpet.
