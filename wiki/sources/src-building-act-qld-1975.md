# Building Act 1975 (QLD) — alterations, s61/s81, and sustainable housing provisions (surgical extract)

## Summary

The full Queensland Building Act 1975 (reprint current as at 1 February 2024), the head legislation that defines "building work" (s5), adopts the BCA/NCC and Queensland Development Code as building assessment provisions (s12–s14, s30), and contains the sections NRG assessors cite for alterations to existing dwellings — s61 (alterations to safe existing work may be assessed under earlier building assessment provisions) and s81 (when an approval can require the existing building to be brought up to current provisions). It also contains Chapter 8A Part 2 "Provisions to support sustainable housing" (s246L–246R), which voids body-corporate and covenant restrictions on light roof colours (solar absorptance ≤ 0.55), energy-efficient windows, and solar hot water/PV. This is a surgical extract of a 657K-character Act — only the NRG-relevant sections are captured in full.

## Key claims

- **s5 — What is building work (full definition, s5(1)):** Building work is (a) "building, repairing, **altering**, underpinning (whether by vertical or lateral support), moving or demolishing a building or other structure"; or (b) excavating or filling for/incidental to (a), or that may adversely affect the stability of a building or structure on the same or adjoining land; or (c) supporting land (vertically or laterally) for (a) activities; or (d) "other work regulated under the building assessment provisions". s5(2): for (d), work includes a management procedure or other activity relating to a building even with no structural change.
- **Dictionary (Schedule 2) — "alterations":** "alterations, to an existing building or structure, **includes additions** to the building or structure." There is no narrower stand-alone definition; an extension/addition is an alteration for the Act's alteration provisions (s61, s80, s81).
- **s12 — BCA** is the document called 'National Construction Code', volume 1 and volume 2 **including the Queensland Appendixes**, as amended from time to time by the ABCB.
- **s14 — Compliance:** building work complies with the BCA or QDC only if it complies with all relevant performance requirements, achieved by (a) the deemed-to-satisfy provisions (BCA) / acceptable solutions (QDC), (b) a performance solution that complies with or is at least equivalent to the relevant requirement, or (c) a combination.

### s61 — Alterations to safe existing work may be approved on basis of earlier building assessment provisions (FULL TEXT, paraphrase-faithful)

- s61(1): The section applies to a building development application if (a) the building work is **alterations to an existing building or structure**; and (b) the building certifier is satisfied the **general safety and structural standards** of the building would **not be at risk** if the alterations were carried out under **earlier building assessment provisions**.
- s61(2): The certifier **may carry out building assessment work on the basis that the work is to be carried out under the earlier building assessment provisions** (i.e. the alteration can be assessed against the code in force when the building was approved, not the current NCC).
- s61(2A): Exception — subsection (2) does not apply to the extent a regulation under s37(5) states an amendment of a building assessment provision applies and approval must be given under the provisions **as amended** (i.e. the State can mandate that specific new provisions apply to alterations regardless of s61).
- s61(3)–(4): If the building's BCA classification changed only because of a BCA reclassification after the certificate of occupancy was given, the certifier may assess on the basis of the provisions applying to the classification shown on the certificate of occupancy.
- s61(5) definitions: "building assessment provisions" includes the former Standard Building By-laws, Standard Building Law, and the repealed Standard Building Regulation 1993. "**Earlier building assessment provisions** means the building assessment provisions as they were in force at a particular time before the application was made."

### s80 — Alterations to unsafe existing work

- Applies where the building work is alterations to an existing building and the certifier decides the building is **unsafe or structurally unsound**. The approval may include a condition that all or a stated part of the building must comply with the building assessment provisions in force when the approval was granted (or at another stated time the certifier considers will make it safe and structurally sound).

### s81 — Building development approval for particular alterations may require existing building or structure to comply with building assessment provisions (FULL TEXT, paraphrase-faithful)

- s81(1): Applies to a building development approval for alterations to an existing building or structure if **(a) the total of (i) the alterations plus (ii) any previous structural alterations approved or completed in the previous 3 years represents more than half (>50%) of the total volume of the existing building, measured over its roof and external walls**; or (b) the certifier has decided the alterations pose a risk to the safety of persons accommodated in or using the building, or of spreading fire to adjoining buildings.
- s81(2): Does not apply where the alterations are for a budget accommodation building (ch 7 pt 3) or residential care building (ch 7A pt 4) and their purpose is fire-safety-standard compliance.
- s81(3): The approval **may include a condition that all, or a stated part, of the existing building must comply with all or a stated part of the building assessment provisions as if it were a new building or structure** — this is the trigger that can drag a whole existing dwelling up to current NCC (including energy efficiency) when an alteration exceeds the 50%-of-volume threshold.
- s81(4): Does not limit chapter 5, part 3.
- **Practical pairing for assessors:** s61 is the concession (assess alteration against earlier provisions if safety not at risk); s81 is the stick (>50% volume in 3 years → existing building can be required to comply as new). Both are discretionary ("may"), exercised by the building certifier.

### Chapter 8A Part 2 — Provisions to support sustainable housing (s246L–246R)

- s246M definitions: "**energy efficient**, in relation to a window in a prescribed building, means the window reduces heat conduction and solar radiation passing through the window" (examples given: tinted glass, double-glazed, triple-glazed windows). "**Prescribed building**" = a class 1a or 2 building, or an enclosed class 10a building attached to one. "Relevant instrument" covers body corporate CMS/by-laws, building management statements, subleases, estate covenants/contracts, etc.
- s246O: A relevant instrument has **no force or effect** to the extent it would (a) prohibit a roof colour for a class 1a building (or attached enclosed 10a) where the colour achieves a **solar absorptance value for the upper roof surface of not more than 0.55**; (b) prohibit use or treatment of energy-efficient windows in a prescribed building; (c) require a minimum floor area, minimum number of bathrooms/bedrooms, or a particular building orientation where that would produce a less energy efficient building. Prohibitions on solar hot water/PV (s246O(1)(d)) survive only in limited common-property circumstances (structural integrity, insufficient shared space, piping noise).
- s246P: Restrictions (as opposed to prohibitions) on roof colour ≤ 0.55 solar absorptance and on energy-efficient windows have no force or effect where the choice minimises adverse external-appearance effects and doesn't unreasonably interfere with use/enjoyment.
- s246R: Where an instrument requires an entity's consent for roof colour or energy-efficient windows, **consent cannot be withheld** if the colour achieves solar absorptance ≤ 0.55 (or the window/treatment is energy efficient), minimises external-appearance impacts, and doesn't unreasonably interfere with use/enjoyment.
- s246N: Part 2 applies to relevant instruments even if made before 1 January 2010, with savings for prescribed matters already in instruments in force at that date.

## Caveats

- Reprint "Current as at 1 February 2024" — Queensland amends this Act frequently; verify currency before citing in formal advice.
- This is a **surgical extract** of a ~660K-character Act. Only the sections listed above were read in full; everything else is navigation-level only.
- s61 and s81 powers are **discretionary** ("may") — outcomes depend on the building certifier's satisfaction/decision; this page states the statutory tests, not certifier practice.
- The Act sets the framework only — the substantive energy efficiency requirements live in the BCA/NCC and QDC (e.g. QDC MP 4.1), not in this Act.
