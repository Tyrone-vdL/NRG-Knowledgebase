# ABCB Housing Energy Efficiency Handbook (NCC 2022, March 2023)

## Summary

The ABCB's authoritative guidance handbook (published March 2023, version 1.0) explaining the NCC 2022 housing energy efficiency provisions for Class 1 houses and Class 10a buildings — covering Performance Requirements H6P1 (thermal performance) and H6P2 (energy usage) and all five compliance options: NatHERS (7-star + WOH 60), Elemental DTS (Section 13 Housing Provisions), Verification using a reference building (H6V2), Verification of building envelope sealing (H6V3), and first-principles Performance Solutions. It is non-mandatory guidance but is the best single map of how the pieces (NCC Vol 2, Spec 42, Spec 44, ABCB Housing Provisions Section 13, ABCB load-limit and WoH standards) connect, with 10 worked examples including building fabric R-value derivations, glazing calculations, WOH energy budgets and a blower-door test walkthrough.

## Structure of the document

1. Introduction — background, scope (Class 1 + Class 10a; "house" = Class 1)
2. Performance Requirements and compliance options (H6P1, H6P2, the 5 options)
3. Compliance option 1 — NatHERS (Ch 3)
4. Compliance option 2 — Elemental (Ch 4)
5. Compliance option 3 — Verification using a reference building (Ch 5)
6. Compliance option 4 — Verification of building envelope sealing (Ch 6)
7. Compliance option 5 — Other Performance Solutions (Ch 7)
Appendices: A abbreviations; B glossary (NCC defined terms); C NCC compliance framework; D 10 worked examples; E reports and standards.

## Key claims

### Framework (Ch 1–2)

- Three main changes from NCC 2019: consistent volume structure renumbering; **quantified Performance Requirements** (numerical targets); increased fabric stringency plus regulation of the "energy value" of a house with typical fixed appliances.
- **H6P1 (was P2.6.1)**: total heating load, cooling load and thermal energy load of habitable rooms/conditioned spaces must not exceed the limits in **Specification 44**. Load limits are not the same as actual energy use (that is H6P2's domain).
- **H6P2 (was P2.6.2)**: energy value of domestic services must not exceed **70%** of a benchmark house with (a) 3-star ducted heat pump heating (2019 GEMS), (b) 3-star ducted heat pump cooling, (c) 5-star instantaneous gas water heater (2017 GEMS), (d) lighting power density 4 W/m². Domestic services exclude cooking facilities and portable/plug-in appliances.
- **No trading between H6P1 and H6P2** — both must be met in full (A2G1).
- Five compliance options (Table 2.1): NatHERS (DTS, meets both PRs); Elemental (DTS, both); VURB H6V2 (Performance Solution, H6P1 only); envelope sealing H6V3 (Performance Solution, part of H6P1 only); other Performance Solutions (both). Combinations are permitted (e.g. NatHERS for H6P1 + Elemental Part 13.6/13.7 for H6P2). All options aim to offer an equivalent level of performance.

### Option 1 — NatHERS (Ch 3)

- **Thermal rating ≥ 7 stars** (S42C2(1)(a)) using NatHERS-accredited software; stringency rose from 6 to 7 stars vs NCC 2019.
- Outdoor living area concessions, **climate zones 1 and 2 only**: ≥ 6.5 stars with a complying OLA roofed with Total R-Value ≥ R1.5; ≥ 6.0 stars if the OLA also has at least one permanently installed ceiling fan meeting S42C2(5). OLA criteria (S42C2(3)–(4)): adjoins/accessible from a general purpose living area; floor area ≥ 12.0 m²; length and width each ≥ 2.5 m; opening height above floor ≥ 2.1 m; one side permanently open + second side open or readily openable; open sides ≥ 900 mm from boundary or breeze-path obstruction.
- **Heating and cooling load limits** (separate caps) specified in the ABCB Standard for NatHERS Heating and Cooling Load Limits (2022); slightly more stringent than NCC 2019. The limits **do not apply in NSW, NT, Tas and parts of Qld and WA**.
- **WOH rating ≥ 60** (out of 100) required by S42C3 — covers fuel type/efficiency of heating, cooling and hot water, lighting allowance, pools/spas, and on-site renewables. Both the S42C3 WOH rating and Part 13.7 Services DTS must be met (S42C4(2)).
- Additional fabric DTS provisions still apply when using software (S42C4(1)): 13.2.2 insulation integrity; **13.2.3(7) and 13.2.5(5) thermal breaks (R ≥ 0.2)**; 13.2.3(5) ceiling-insulation loss compensation (except where software compensates automatically); 13.2.6(4)/(5)/(6) floor edge insulation; Part 13.4 building sealing.
- NatHERS software accounts for thermal bridging — no additional thermal bridging mitigation under this option — but the R0.2 thermal break requirements still apply. **Reflective insulation alone is not suitable as a thermal break** (needs an adjoining airspace).
- Evidence: NCC A5G9 (new for 2022) requires a **NatHERS certificate** as the evidence of suitability for this pathway.

### Option 2 — Elemental DTS (Ch 4) — Section 13 of the ABCB Housing Provisions

- Components: Part 13.2 building fabric; 13.3 external glazing; 13.4 building sealing; 13.5 ceiling fans; 13.6 WOH energy usage (new); 13.7 services. Stringency aligned to 7-star NatHERS equivalence.
- **Roofs/ceilings (13.2.3)**: minimum ceiling insulation R-values from look-up tables (13.2.3a–13.2.3r) by roof ventilation (vented vs standard), reflective insulation, solar absorptance, construction and climate zone — Total R-Value calculation no longer needed in most cases. Reflective insulation must have emittance ≤ 0.05, face downwards in CZ 3–8, and face a ≥ 20 mm airspace. Thermal bridging options for pitched roof + flat ceiling: meet Total R-Value (Table 13.2.3s), add R0.5 to required R-value, add continuous ceiling layer ≥ R0.13, or stack 2 insulation layers. **Roof solar absorptance ≤ 0.64 in climate zones 1–5** (13.2.3(10)). Edge-of-ceiling rule (13.2.3(6)): where required ceiling insulation is between R3.0 and R4.5 it may drop to R3.0 within 450 mm of the wall; above R4.5, drop to R3.0 permitted if the field insulation increases by R0.5.
- 'Vented' roof definition (2022): attic-space roof with one wind-driven ventilator per 50 m² of ceiling (with gable/eave/ridge vents), or one powered ventilator per 200 m², or a tiled roof without sarking at roof level.
- **Roof lights (13.2.4)**: area ≤ 5% of the floor area served; Total System SHGC per Table 13.2.4 (via roof light shaft index); **Total System U-Value ≤ U3.9** for all roof lights.
- **External walls (13.2.5)**: minimum insulation R-values from tables 13.2.5a–13.2.5o by wall height, construction, solar absorptance, shading overhang, storeys, climate zone. **Wall solar absorptance ≤ 0.7 in climate zones 1–5**. Thermal bridging for metal frames: Total R-Value (tables 13.2.5p–r per AS/NZS 4859.2) or prescriptive options (tables 13.2.5s–u). Thermal break R ≥ 0.2 for some metal-framed walls (13.2.5(5)). Two-storey note: for wall height ≤ 2.4 m add R0.5, > 2.4 m add R1.0 to single-storey table values (Table 13.2.5a note 5).
- **Floors (13.2.6)**: floors over unenclosed space — Table 13.2.6a; over enclosed subfloor — tables by subfloor wall height (covered up to 1800 mm; taller needs another option), reflective insulation and climate zone. CSOG with in-slab/in-screed heating or cooling: **vertical edge insulation R ≥ 1.0** (exempt if in-screed system solely in bathroom/amenity). Slab edge + underslab: **CZ 6–7 R ≥ 0.64 edge and R ≥ 0.64 underslab; CZ 8 R ≥ 1.0 edge and R ≥ 2.0 underslab**; waffle-pod slabs exempt in CZ 1–5. Edge insulation must be water resistant and continuous from ground level to ≥ 300 mm depth (or full slab edge depth).
- **Attached garages (13.2.7)**: either the garage's external fabric matches the house's thermal performance, or the garage is thermally separated from the house by construction equal to the house.
- **External glazing (13.3)**: winter performance (13.3.2, CZ 2–8) caps each piece of glazing's Cu/CSHGC ratio against Table 13.3.2a allowances; summer performance (13.3.3, CZ 1–7) caps aggregate solar heat gain at floor-area x CSHGC (Table 13.3.3a). Inputs include area, Total System U-Value, Total System SHGC (≤ 0.7 per piece), exposure/level/frame/floor factors, room type, orientation sector, P/H shading ratio. New 2022 factors: room type, number of levels, floor coverings, window open-ability (cross-ventilation), frame solar absorptance. ABCB Glazing Calculator automates it.
- **Shading (13.3.4)**: permanent projections must extend horizontally both sides of the glazing ≥ projection distance P; adjustable devices must block **≥ 80% of summer solar radiation** and be easily operable.
- **Building sealing (13.4)**: applies to all houses + conditioned garages; exemptions for evaporative-cooling-only buildings in CZ 1, 2, 3, 5 and gas-appliance ventilation openings. Chimneys/flues need closable damper; skylights and windows/doors sealed in conditioned spaces and habitable rooms in CZ 4–8 (draft protection at door bottoms; compressible/fibrous seals; windows meeting AS 2047 air infiltration exempt from edge seals); exhaust fans need self-closing damper/filter; ceilings/walls/floors sealed via close-fitting linings or caulking/foam/strips; evaporative coolers need self-closing dampers.
- **Ceiling fans (13.5)**: required in habitable rooms in **climate zones 1, 2, 3 and 5 (CZ 5 in Qld and NSW only)**, installed per Table 13.5.2 (worked example: 32 m² family/dining → 2 x 1400 mm fans; 14 m² theatre → 1 x 900 mm).
- **WOH energy usage (13.6, new for 2022)**: annual energy use = (A x EE) + EP + ES − ER must not exceed allowance = A x EF, where A = floor area x factor (Table 13.6.2a), EE from the ABCB Standard for Whole-of-Home Efficiency Factors (2022), EP/ES pool and spa pump terms, ER = rooftop PV capacity (kW, standard covers 1–10 kW only). Floor area cap **≤ 500 m²** for this DTS pathway. Set so annual net energy cost ≤ 70% of the benchmark house.
- **Services (13.7)**: central heating water piping insulation **R ≥ 0.4** (internal, all CZ); **R ≥ 0.6 (CZ 1, 2, 3, 5), R ≥ 0.9 (CZ 4, 6, 7), R ≥ 1.3 (CZ 8)** for ventilated/enclosed and external locations (cold water and relief valve piping within 500 mm of the connection). Ductwork insulation: heating-only/cooling-only **R ≥ 1.0 (CZ 1–7), R ≥ 1.5 (CZ 8)**; combined heating + refrigerated cooling **R ≥ 1.5 (CZ 1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8), R ≥ 1.0 (CZ 2, 5)**; sealed to AS 4254.1/.2 Class C; weather protection sleeve sealed with ≥ 48 mm tape. Electric resistance space heating (serving > 1 room): max **110 W/m² living areas, 150 W/m² bathrooms**, individual room switches. Artificial lighting: **≤ 5 W/m² house, ≤ 4 W/m² verandah/balcony, ≤ 3 W/m² garage**; perimeter lighting needs daylight sensor and ≥ 40 lumens/W; adjustment factors for dimmers/timers/sensors (e.g. manual dimming 0.85). Heated water: NCC Vol 3 Part B2 (STC counts updated). Heated pools: solar/reclaimed/gas/heat-pump heater; pool cover **R ≥ 0.05** if heat pump or gas heated (unless in conditioned space); time switches on heaters and all circulation pumps; spas > 680 L need pump time switch, plus cover and push-button + time switch on heater.

### Option 3 — VURB H6V2 (Ch 5)

- Performance Solution for **H6P1 only**; proposed house's loads compared to a reference house modelled to comply with Housing Provisions **Parts 13.2, 13.3 and 13.5**. Criteria: CZ 1 cooling load only; CZ 8 heating load only; CZ 2–7 both loads ≤ reference.
- Calculation method must comply with **ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 140** (2007 version per example) and must NOT be NatHERS house energy rating software (current or previously accredited, including non-regulatory modes).
- Parameters fixed across both models include floor plan, glazing size/location, orientation, soils, schedules, thermostats, occupancy, internal gains, infiltration (H6V2(3)(a)–(t)); changeable parameters include glazing type/performance, insulation amounts, construction materials, floor type, cladding colours — this enables fabric trade-offs.
- Heating/cooling availability assumed between 8 and 17 hours/day; hourly climate data for a typical year (dry-bulb, solar, wind, cloud) required.
- Additional fabric DTS still applies: 13.2.2; thermal breaks; floor edge insulation; sealing via Part 13.4 **or** H6V3.

### Option 4 — Envelope sealing H6V3 (Ch 6)

- Verifies the sealing component of H6P1 only: envelope air permeability **≤ 10 m³/hr.m² at 50 Pa**, tested to **AS/NZS ISO 9972 Method 1** (blower door). Broadly equivalent to 10 ACH at 50 Pa for homes.
- If sealed to **≤ 5 m³/hr.m² at 50 Pa**, mechanical ventilation is mandatory (new for 2022): override-able, continuous or intermittent outdoor air ≥ 25% of each 4-hour period, minimum flow per H6V3(2)(a)(iii) formula; solid-fuel appliances vented directly outside; gas appliances per AS/NZS 5601.1 clauses 6.4 and 6.4.5.
- Practical guidance: allow ~1 month between test and handover for rectification/retest; temporary sealing not acceptable; choose AS 2047-tested low-infiltration windows (WERS lists rates).

### Option 5 — First principles (Ch 7)

- Performance Solutions assessed by evidence of suitability, expert judgement, comparison with DTS, and/or Verification Methods; A2G2(4) mandates the 4-step process (brief → analysis → evaluation → final report) with a performance-based design brief (PBDB).
- H6P1's quantified Spec 44 load limits allow acceptance criteria for any climate: higher heating loads allowed in cold locations, higher cooling loads in hot/humid ones, with an area adjustment so small houses (higher surface-to-volume ratio) are not unfairly disadvantaged.
- Expert judgement alone is inappropriate for housing energy efficiency because performance is readily calculable.

### Worked examples (Appendix D) — headline numbers

- **D.1 Brisbane house (NCC CZ 2 / NatHERS CZ 10), suspended timber floor, 6.0-star OLA pathway**: load limits from ABCB Standard Table 6 — heating **29 MJ/m².annum**, cooling **42 MJ/m².annum**; modelled design 6.1 stars, heating 16.6, cooling 24.3 → complies.
- **D.2 Adelaide CSOG house (CZ 5, steel-framed, tiled roof SA 0.579, brick veneer SA 0.5, 2400 mm walls, 750 mm eaves)**: ceiling insulation R2.5 base → R3.0 (+R0.5 thermal bridging) → **R3.6** (Table 13.2.3w compensation for downlights to 1% of ceiling area); skylight ≤ 5% of room area, shaft index 2.22 → SHGC ≤ 0.33, U ≤ 3.9; wall insulation **R2.0** + R0.3 layer outside steel frame (Table 13.2.5u); slab edge **R ≥ 1.0** (in-screed heating).
- **D.3 Melbourne suspended-floor house (CZ 6, timber-framed, tile SA ≤ 0.7, 500 mm eaves)**: under-roof insulation **R1.0** + ceiling **R3.5** base → **R4.0** after downlight compensation, reducible to R3.0 within 450 mm of walls; roof space ≥ 20 mm + ventilation per 10.8.3 (CZ 6–8 condensation rule); wall insulation **R2.5** (Table 13.2.5k); suspended floor **R2.0**, no subfloor wall insulation needed at 540 mm wall height (Table 13.2.6f); subfloor perimeter edge **R ≥ 1.0** (CZ 6, 13.2.6(5)(a)).
- **D.4 Toowoomba (CZ 5 Qld) ceiling fans**: family/dining 32 m² → 2 x 1400 mm; theatre 14 m² → 1 x 900 mm.
- **D.5 WOH examples**: Canberra (CZ 7, 256 m², 2/2.5-star ducted split, heat pump HW, 7 kW PV) — allowance 5.83 kW vs usage −1.65 kW, complies even without PV (4.87 kW). Brisbane (CZ 2, 246 m², pre-2019 RC AC COP/EER 4.66 → 4.5 stars via SRI = 2 x EER − 4.5, off-peak electric storage HW, 45 kL pool with 4-star pump EP = 0.945 kW, 2 kL spa ES = 1.1 kW, 3 kW PV) — allowance 3.94 kW vs usage 3.0 kW, complies; **without PV usage is 5.9 kW → fails**, demonstrating PV is often essential with electric storage HW + pool.
- **D.6 Lighting**: 110.5 m² house allowance 553 W (5 W/m²) + garage 60 W (3 W/m²); with dimmers over 80% of living areas the illumination-power-density route raises the house allowance to 598 W (factor 0.85 → 5.9 W/m² for the dimmed 50.4 m²).
- **D.7 Perth VURB**: first iteration failed (heating 7,677 vs reference 7,365 kWh/yr despite cooling passing); improving window Total System U-Value from 6.0 to 5.6 W/m².K brought heating to 7,229 and cooling 7,265 → passes both.
- **D.8 Perth blower-door test**: full AS/NZS ISO 9972 Method 1 procedure — seal AC ducts, close (not seal) intermittent exhausts, test in 10 Pa increments to ≥ 60 Pa, average positive and negative runs, check against 10 m³/hr.m².

### Glossary highlights (Appendix B)

- R-Value = thermal resistance, m².K/W; reflective products only add R-value in conjunction with an airspace (net of any non-reflective airspace replaced). Total R-Value includes all layers, airspaces, thermal bridging and surface resistances.
- Habitable room includes bedrooms, living, kitchen, study, home theatre; excludes bathroom, laundry, WC, pantry, walk-in robe, corridors.
- Conditioned space (Vol 2): heated/cooled by domestic services, excluding non-habitable rooms with a heater ≤ 1.2 kW or 4.3 MJ/hour.
- Vapour permeance measured in µg/N.s per ASTM E96 Procedure B (23°C, 50% RH).
- NCC has 8 climate zones for thermal design; NatHERS uses 69 regional subzones.
- Thermal bridging = unintended heat-flow path (e.g. metal frame) bypassing insulation; distinct from the R0.2 thermal break rule, which addresses localised hot/cold patches where cladding contacts metal frames. Continuous insulation between lining and frame can satisfy both at once; extra in-frame insulation does not remove the need for a thermal break; metal-framed brick veneer needs no thermal break (cavity separates) but usually still needs bridging mitigation.

## Caveats

- Published March 2023 (version 1.0) against NCC 2022 as adopted; NCC 2022 Amendment 1 and state adoption dates/variations (e.g. NSW BASIX, NT/Tas deferrals, Qld/WA load-limit carve-outs) may supersede details — confirm jurisdictional position before advising.
- The handbook is explicitly **non-mandatory guidance**: it does not discharge legal obligations and must be read with the NCC, the ABCB Housing Provisions and state legislation; table values quoted in the worked examples are excerpts, not the full look-up tables.
- The deep table sets (13.2.3a–r roof, 13.2.5a–o wall, 13.2.6a–j floor, 13.3.2/13.3.3 glazing factors, WoH efficiency factors) live in the **ABCB Housing Provisions** and **ABCB Standards** — separate documents; this handbook only excerpts them. Figures/diagrams (thermal break details, slab edge, shading P/H) did not survive text extraction.
