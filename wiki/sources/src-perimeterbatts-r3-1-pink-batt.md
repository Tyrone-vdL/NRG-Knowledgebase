# Fletcher Pink Perimeter Batts R3.1 Technical Data Sheet

## Summary

Technical data sheet (Fletcher Insulation, issue date 16/09/2024) for Pink Perimeter Batts — R3.1 glasswool ceiling batts purpose-designed at 450 mm width and reduced thickness to insulate the perimeter of residential ceilings while satisfying NCC 2022 Housing Provisions clause 13.2.3(6) and preserving condensation-management clearances. Covers product data, fire hazard properties, NCC 2022 compliance clauses and installation conditions.

## Key claims

- Material R-value: R3.1 m²K/W at 110 mm nominal thickness, 450 mm width, in 1200 mm and 1160 mm lengths.
- Designed to exceed the requirements of NCC Volume 2 13.2.3(6) (roof/ceiling perimeter insulation) while keeping thickness minimal so condensation-management provisions are not impeded in most scenarios; intended for use in conjunction with Pink Ceiling batts.
- Thermal resistance complies with AS/NZS 4859.1, as referenced in NCC 2022 Volume 1 J4D3(1) and ABCB Housing Provisions section 3.12.2(1).
- Non-combustible to AS 1530.1, satisfying NCC 2022 clause C2D10(1).
- Early Fire Hazard Indices (AS/NZS 1530.3): Ignitability 0, Spread of Flame 0, Heat Evolved 0, Smoke Developed 1 — within the limits of NCC clause C2D11(1)(i) and Specification S7C7.
- BAL compliance to AS 3959: Low through to FZ (Flame Zone).
- Maximum service temperature 340°C (ASTM C411/C447); moisture absorption < 0.2% by volume after 4 days at 50°C and 95% relative humidity; passes ASTM C1338-14 fungi resistance (no growth).
- Pack data: R3.1 110×450×1200 mm, 6 batts per pack, 3.24 m² area / 3.64 m² coverage per pack (product code 20593); 1160 mm length variant 3.13 m² / 3.52 m² (code 20594). (Figures reconstructed from a compressed table row — verify against PDF.)
- Made from FBS-1 Bio-Soluble glasswool with up to 80% recycled content; Codemark certified; zero Ozone Depletion Potential.
- Installation conditions: must not be compressed (compression voids the claimed R-value), minimum 20 mm clearance to the roof/pliable building membrane, must be kept dry at all stages, not suitable for prolonged high-humidity interiors, and cannot serve as a water or vapour barrier.
