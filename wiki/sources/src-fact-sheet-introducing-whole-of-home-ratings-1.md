# NatHERS Fact Sheet — Introducing Whole of Home Ratings (Information for Assessors)

## Summary

NatHERS fact sheet for assessors (dated 23 March 2023) introducing the Whole of Home rating that accompanies the NCC 2022 7-star thermal minimum, and setting out the phased adoption timeline, software status and training pathway. Two-page summary aimed at assessors transitioning to NCC 2022 assessments.

## Key claims

- The NCC 2022 energy efficiency changes are being adopted from 1 May 2023, with a transition period to 1 October 2023 (NCC 2022).
- NCC 2022 requires a new minimum thermal performance rating of 7 stars under NatHERS, and all new homes must also meet a minimum annual energy use budget.
- The Whole of Home rating sits alongside the thermal star rating and considers energy used by heating, cooling, cooking and plug-in appliances, hot water systems, lighting, and pool and spa equipment.
- The 0–100 rating equals energy used for heating, cooling and appliances minus energy generated via solar panels, providing a snapshot of costs and greenhouse gas emissions.
- At publication, AccuRate users could model new assessments but could not produce a rating certificate until April 2023; all commercial tool providers were applying for re-accreditation.
- AccuRate Home replaces AccuRate Sustainability as the NatHERS benchmark software.
- Two online CPD modules were available: an overview module and a technical module on NatHERS Technical Note updates; the technical module is mandatory for professionals wanting to become certified Whole of Home assessors. Training ran through to August 2023 with recorded sessions available afterwards.
- New assessors receive Whole of Home training through the Certificate IV unit 'Using Accredited Software' via Registered Training Organisations.
- There are four accredited software tools, each with different user interfaces and functions.

## Concepts touched

Whole-of-Home, NatHERS, NCC 2022, 7-Star, annual energy use budget, AccuRate Home, assessor training, CPD, Certificate IV, accredited software

## Caveats

- Dated 23 March 2023 — transition deadlines and training availability described are now historical; software re-accreditation has since completed.
- High-level fact sheet; contains no technical rating methodology or numeric thresholds beyond the 7-star minimum and 0–100 scale.
