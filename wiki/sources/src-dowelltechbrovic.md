# Dowell Technical Brochure — Victorian Edition (2008)

## Summary

Victorian edition (July 2008, Boral-era) of the Dowell aluminium windows and doors technical brochure — same core technical content as the QLD edition (wind-load N-ratings, U-value/SHGC energy basics, 2008-era state star-rating regimes, acoustic STC table, AS 1288-2006 safety glazing, AS 3959-1999 bushfire, warranty, installation) but with Victorian/Tasmanian product size availability quoted in non-cyclonic N1–N5 classes only and VIC/TAS sales locations. Notable VIC-edition product differences: casement windows are not available in double glazing, and some awning options are flagged as not available in double glazing or full height.

## Key claims

- Wind performance table identical to the QLD edition: N1 permissible stress 500 Pa / ULS 700 Pa / water penetration 150 Pa through to N6/C4 3000/6000/450 Pa; the "Queensland variation — Permissible stress" note is retained even in this VIC edition.
- Energy content identical to QLD edition: U-value defined in W/m².K (lower is better); SHGC defined; 87% of heat gain and 49% of heat loss in an average home attributed to poor performing windows; 8 BCA climate zones (1–3 cooling, 4–5 mixed, 6–8 heating).
- State regimes as at 2008 (same table as QLD edition): Victoria — 5 Star construction with FirstRate the most common verification method (SEAV-developed; update to use the AccuRate engine); NSW BASIX; TAS/NT/QLD on 2005 BCA DtS (3.5 stars zones 1–3, 4 stars zones 4–8); ACT/SA and WA 5 Star on 2007 BCA NFRC values.
- Indicative acoustic STC ratings: 3mm float 25; 4mm float 26; 6mm float 28; 6.38mm laminated 31; 10.38mm laminated 33; double glazed sash 4-8-4 STC 35; double window with 100mm air gap STC 45.
- AS 1288-2006 and AS 3959-1999 content identical to the QLD edition (Grade A safety glazing locations; 1.8 mm max mesh aperture for bushfire Level 1; Level 3 toughened glass / ≥35 mm solid-core doors).
- Sliding door standard sill: 200 Pa water rating, 89 mm wide × 42 mm deep internal leg; sump sill/subsill options available.
- 10-year warranty against faulty workmanship from delivery; moving parts 3 years; install to AS 2047.
- State-specific difference — size ranges: VIC edition quotes product size ranges in N-classes only, without cyclonic C categories: sliding windows, awnings, casements, sliding doors, alfresco doors and French doors "n1–n5"; double hung "n1–n3"; bi-fold "n1–n4". Availability keys read "NOT FOR N4" / "NOT FOR N4 & N5" (vs the QLD edition's "NOT FOR N5/C3" / "NOT FOR N4/C2 & N5/C3").
- State-specific difference — double glazing: the VIC edition notes "Casement windows are not available in Double Glazing", and the awning size key adds flags "Not Available in Double Glazing" and "Not Available in Full Height" for certain options; the QLD edition does not carry these casement/awning DG restrictions.
- State-specific difference — locations: Head Office Melbourne (Bayswater) plus Albury/Wodonga, Ballarat, Bendigo, Geelong, Shepparton, Traralgon, Warrnambool, and Tasmania (Hobart, Launceston). Print code BC 02457 VIC July 08.

## Caveats

- Published July 2008 — the Victorian 5 Star/FirstRate regime described is long superseded (NCC 2022 7-Star Whole-of-Home); AS 3959 content is the superseded 1999 edition. Use for product/structural context only.
- PDF text extraction is heavily degraded in this file (intra-word spacing throughout); all figures cross-checked against the cleaner QLD edition where content is shared, but verify any standalone figure against the source PDF.
- This is the Victorian state variant; for cyclonic (C-rated) size availability use the QLD edition.
