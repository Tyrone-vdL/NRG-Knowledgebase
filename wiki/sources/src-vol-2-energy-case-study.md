# NCC Volume Two Energy Efficiency Case Study — Typical Masonry Veneer House (Climate Zone 6)

## Summary

ABCB NCC Resource Kit case study (2016) applying the elemental Deemed-to-Satisfy energy efficiency provisions of NCC Volume Two (Parts 3.12.1-3.12.5) to a three-bedroom masonry veneer house in climate zone 6, element by element: roof, roof lights, walls, floors, attached garage, glazing, sealing, air movement and services. It works through real numbers — required Total R-Values, added-insulation calculations, glazing calculator iterations from fail to pass, ductwork insulation and lighting allowances.

## Key claims

- Project: 3-bedroom masonry veneer house, climate zone 6, concrete slab-on-ground, pitched tiled roof with roof upper-surface solar absorptance more than 0.6, flat ceiling, attached garage, ducted reverse-cycle air conditioning, bathroom electric in-slab heating at 120 W/m².
- Roof: solar absorptance >0.6 in climate zone 6 requires minimum Total R-Value 5.1 upwards (Table 3.12.1.1a); a ventilated pitched tiled roof with flat ceiling has an inherent Total R-Value of 0.23 (Figure 3.12.1.1), so added insulation of minimum R4.87 (5.1 − 0.23) is needed.
- Ceiling penetrations (3 ceiling fans + 4 downlights, ~0.04 m² each assuming 200 mm clearance) need no insulation compensation because total penetrations are less than 0.5% of floor area.
- Roof light: shaft height 750 mm, serving 2.3% of the floor area of the space; shaft index ≈ 0.83 (750/900) requires Total System U-Value not more than 5.7 and Total System SHGC not more than 0.72 (Table 3.12.1.2) — met by a flat framed double-layer clear panel without ceiling diffuser using the "worst case" Explanatory Information values.
- External walls under 220 kg/m² surface density require minimum Total R-Value 2.8 (Table 3.12.1.3a); masonry veneer inherent value 0.56 means added insulation of minimum R2.24; cavity masonry walls (>220 kg/m² with slab-on-ground) only need insulation of R-Value not less than 1.0 under Table 3.12.1.3b option (c) for climate zone 6.
- Slab-on-ground floors need no insulation except with in-slab heating/cooling or in climate zone 8; the in-slab-heated bathroom requires R1.0 insulation around the external vertical edge of the slab (3.12.1.5(c)).
- Attached Class 10a garage in climate zone 6: either the garage external walls or the separating wall must match the house external wall performance (Total R-Value 2.8); the framed separating wall's inherent R0.45 allows added insulation of R2.35 (achieved with R2.5 bulk insulation if the airspace is maintained; deduct 0.17 if the airspace is filled).
- Glazing (Part 3.12.2, ABCB glazing calculator): single clear aluminium-framed glazing failed conductance by 119% over allowance; clear double glazing with timber frames still failed by 6%; reducing the north-east eave from 600 mm to 400 mm achieved a pass because the problem was winter conductance and more north-east sun improves performance.
- P and H shading values used: P = 0.6 m (450 mm eave + 150 mm gutter); H = window height + 100 mm. The garage window (G11) is excluded as the garage is not a conditioned space.
- Sealing (Part 3.12.3): in climate zone 6, roof lights serving habitable rooms must be sealed or capable of being sealed; external windows/doors serving conditioned spaces or habitable rooms must be edge-sealed; external swing door bottom edges need a draft protection device or AS 2047 compliance; the garage is exempt (vehicle accommodation, 3.12.3(b)(iii) — a carbon monoxide safety concession).
- Services (Part 3.12.5): reverse-cycle AC ductwork in climate zone 6 requires minimum material R-Value 1.5 (reducible by 0.5 in a roof space with ≥R0.5 directly beneath roofing or under an enclosed-perimeter suspended floor); fittings require minimum material R-Value 0.4; rigid duct sealed to AS 4254 Class C; duct insulation in garage/roof space needs a protective outer sleeve sealed with adhesive tape minimum 48 mm wide.
- In-slab heating piping is exempt from insulation (Table 3.12.5.1(1)(b)) as insulation would impede slab heating; the 120 W/m² power load with thermostat and time switch is under the 150 W/m² allowance for electric resistance space heating.
- Lighting allowances under Part 3.12.5.5: house 116 m² → 580 W allowance (proposed 500 W complies); garage 18 m² → 54 W allowance (proposed 60 W fails and must be reduced to not more than 54 W).

## Caveats

- 2016 publication using NCC Volume Two Part 3.12 elemental DtS numbering at 6-star stringency; NCC 2022 moved housing energy efficiency to Part H6 and the ABCB Housing Provisions (Part 13) at 7-star stringency, and the elemental glazing calculator pathway has changed — treat all clause numbers and R-values as NCC 2016-era, illustrative of method, not current minima.
- Floor plan, elevations, glazing schedule and calculator screenshots (Figures 1-6) are images not captured in text extraction; specific window-by-window results must be checked against the source PDF.
