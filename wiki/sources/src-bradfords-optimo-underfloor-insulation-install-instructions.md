# Bradford Optimo Underfloor Insulation — Installation Instructions

## Summary

CSR Bradford installation instruction flyer for Optimo underfloor insulation batts, covering retrofit installation from below an existing suspended timber floor (using straps) and new-floor installation from above (using saddles). Practical guidance only — batt widths for joist spacings, fixing centres, ventilation and electrical safety rules; no R-values are stated.

## Key claims

- Batt width selection: for floor joists at 450 mm centres use 415 mm wide batts; for 600 mm centres use 565 mm wide batts; trim with a knife if too wide.
- Retrofit (straps method): fix Optimo straps to timber joists at no greater than 500 mm spacing, swivel 90° under the batt and bend upward to support the insulation, then tighten the fixing (nails or self-tapping screws).
- Friction-fit batts tightly between joists with no gaps between insulation and the underside of the floor, joists, or abutting batt ends; if too tight, trim the edge 5 mm over the space width; if too loose, pack with a slice from another batt.
- New floor (saddles method): install from above before flooring is laid — place Optimo saddles across joists no greater than 500 mm apart, staggered joist to joist, fastened with staple, nail or screw; batts rest on the saddle feet with side edges tucked down so flooring adhesive on joists is not impeded.
- Switch off power at the fuse box before working; electrical cables completely surrounded by bulk insulation may overheat and fail — wiring must not be completely surrounded without approval from a licensed electrical contractor; partial surrounding is acceptable for wiring compliant with AS/NZS 3000 post-1984; refer to AS 3999.
- Do not block subfloor ventilation openings; store and install the insulation dry; start fitting from the furthest point from the underfloor access.
