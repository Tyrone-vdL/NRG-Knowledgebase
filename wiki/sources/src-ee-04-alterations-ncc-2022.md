# EE-04 Alterations to Existing Class 1 Buildings (NCC 2022)

## Summary

Victorian Building Authority Practice Note (Version 5.0, published 20 December 2023) covering energy efficiency compliance for alterations and extensions to existing Class 1 (and 10a) buildings under NCC 2022, the Building Act 1993, and Building Regulations 2018.

**Key principle:** Alterations and extensions don't get a free pass from NCC energy efficiency — but unlike new builds, the Relevant Building Surveyor (RBS) has **discretionary power under Regulation 233(3)** to consent to **partial compliance** where full compliance isn't reasonable. The volume of the alteration and the floor area of any extension determine what discretion is available.

NCC 2022 quantified the performance requirements (H6P1 thermal, H6P2 whole-of-home) — they now specify a value, replacing the qualitative "to the degree necessary" language of NCC 2019's P2.6.1/P2.6.2.

## Victorian regulatory framework — Regulation 233

Building Regulations 2018, **Regulation 233** is the central provision for alterations/extensions.

### Reg 233(2) — the 50% volume trigger (alterations)

If proposed alterations **combined with any alterations completed within the previous 3 years** represent **more than 50% of the volume of the original building**, the entire building must be brought into compliance with the Regulations.

For energy efficiency, that means satisfying **H6P1** (thermal) **and** H6P2 (whole-of-home) for both the alteration **and** the rest of the existing building.

### Reg 233(6) — the 25% floor area threshold (extensions)

An extension must comply with the Regulations. The RBS can consider partial compliance for the extension **only if** its floor area is not greater than the **lesser of**:

- **25% of the floor area of the existing building**, and
- **1000 m²**

If the extension exceeds these thresholds, the **extension itself must fully comply**. Partial compliance may still be granted for the existing building portion.

### Reg 233(3) — discretion to consent to partial compliance

Gives the RBS discretionary power to consent to partial compliance with sub-regs (1) or (2). When deciding, the RBS must take into account:

- Structural adequacy of the building
- Reasonable provision for amenity, safety and health of occupants
- Avoiding fire spread to/from adjoining buildings

Consent must be in the form of **Form 18** and recorded on the building permit.

### Table 1 — Application of partial compliance (verbatim from the PN)

| Alteration (% of volume) | Extension (% of floor area) | Consent to partial compliance |
|---|---|---|
| Nil (existing unchanged) | <25% | Available |
| Nil | >25% | Not available |
| <50% | Nil | Available |
| <50% | <25% | Available |
| <50% | >25% | Only available for the alteration |
| >50% | Nil | Available |
| >50% | <25% | Available |
| >50% | >25% | Only available for the alteration |

**Reading the table:** Once an extension exceeds 25% floor area, full compliance is locked in for the extension. Partial compliance still possible for any alterations to the existing dwelling.

## "Full compliance" vs "Partial compliance"

- **Full compliance** = satisfying both H6P1 (7-star NatHERS or DtS thermal equivalent) **and** H6P2 (Whole-of-Home, e.g. NatHERS WoH score ≥60 for Class 1).
- **Partial compliance** = an RBS-granted consent under Reg 233(3), accepting less than full satisfaction of H6P1 and/or H6P2 where full compliance is not reasonable. The PN repeatedly states: "**the RBS should require the highest level of compliance that can be reasonably achieved.**"
- H6P1 and H6P2 are **independent**. PV or efficient hot water systems contribute to H6P2 but **cannot offset H6P1** (thermal fabric).

## Compliance pathways for alterations

The PN sets out **three distinct compliance pathways** for demonstrating energy efficiency compliance on alterations/extensions. Two relate to thermal performance (H6P1); the third relates to whole-of-home (H6P2). They can be mixed — NCC permits independent choice of pathway for each performance requirement.

### Pathway 1 — DtS via NatHERS (whole-of-building)

Use NatHERS thermal software to rate the whole building (existing + alteration/extension) and achieve a **7-star rating** under DtS clause **H6D2(1)(a)**, plus whole-of-home rating under **H6D2(2)(a)**.

- This is a **DtS solution** under NCC 2022 only if the entire building is brought up to compliance.
- NatHERS can only assess a building as a whole — cannot rate the extension/alteration in isolation.

### Pathway 2 — DtS via Elemental Provisions (ABCB Housing Provisions Part 13.2)

Apply the elemental DtS provisions in **Part 13.2** of the ABCB Housing Provisions (building fabric: insulation R-values, glazing, sealing, etc.) directly.

- Often more practical than NatHERS for **minor alterations**.
- Part 13.2.5 sets external wall requirements by solar absorptance, eave overhang and height.
- May be unsuitable where existing dwelling has wall heights > 2.7/3 m or verandahs with overhangs > 600 mm.

### Pathway 3 — Performance Solution (clause A2G2)

Where only part of the building needs NCC compliance and NatHERS is used, it must take the form of a **Performance Solution** under clause **A2G2**, demonstrating compliance with the quantified performance requirement (H6P1 or H6P2) or at least equivalence to the DtS provisions.

- Example given: a performance solution for an extension >25% floor area could show the extended area performs equivalently to a DtS elemental form of construction.
- The PN also recommends engaging NatHERS Accredited Assessors when developing performance solutions.

### A fourth route — Consent to Partial Compliance (Reg 233(3))

Not technically a "pathway" under the NCC — it's a Victorian regulatory mechanism — but functions as the practical fourth lever when the three above can't fully be met. **Partial compliance is not a Performance Solution**; the PN explicitly states they must be documented separately.

### H6P2 (Whole-of-Home) sub-pathways

For H6P2 specifically, compliance can be demonstrated via:
- **NatHERS WoH software** — score ≥60 for Class 1 dwellings. PV (existing in working order, or new) can be included.
- **Elemental DtS WoH calculation** — Part 13.6 of the Housing Provisions + the ABCB Standard for Whole-of-Home Efficiency Factors (a WoH Calculator automates this). **Only usable where works are below the 25% area / 50% volume thresholds AND building fabric is ≥4.5 stars equivalent.** Unless PV constraints exist, a **minimum 3 kW PV system** should be installed under this route.

## Two methods for thermal partial compliance using NatHERS

Neither method is itself a DtS or Performance Solution — they may be **incorporated into a Performance Solution** if properly justified.

### Method A — The Star Rating Formula

Used where a new extension <25% floor area must meet 7-star and the existing building is unaltered (or where partial compliance is consented).

**Formula (Example 1 — extension to existing dwelling, existing has lesser rating):**

```
SRr = (Ae × Sre) + (An × SRn)
      ─────────────────────────
              (Ae + An)
```

**Formula (Example 2 — extension + compliant internal alteration):**

```
SRr = [(Ae − Ani) × Sre] + [(An + Ani) × SRn]
      ────────────────────────────────────────
                  (Ae + An)
```

Where:
- **SRr** = Overall star rating required
- **Sre** = Existing (unaltered) dwelling star rating
- **SRn** = New work dwelling star rating (typically 7.0)
- **Ae** = Existing unaltered area (includes spaces altered entirely within existing fabric)
- **An** = New work area (extension)
- **Ani** = New work area for internal alteration — spaces upgraded to DtS Part 13.2 level

Existing house rating must assume: ≥R1.5 ceiling insulation (or actual if known); closable dampers on chimneys with open fireplaces.

Formula was **revised to be area-based**, not volume-based — consistent with NatHERS tools and to avoid confusion with Reg 233's volume calculation.

### Method B — Rating Only the Renovated Home

Suited where renovation is significant and partial compliance is sought only for alterations to the existing building.

Steps:
1. Rate renovated house with enough existing-fabric improvements to hit 7 stars (or highest achievable if 7 stars not possible).
2. Generate preview certificate → give to RBS to decide on partial compliance under Reg 233.
3. Re-rate once RBS has determined which energy efficiency features will be omitted under partial compliance. Generate final NatHERS certificate for the building permit.

**A rating of the existing unaltered house is NOT required for this method.**

## Worked examples from the PN

### Example 1 (Star Rating Formula — basic)

- Ae = 203.7 m², An = 20.8 m²
- Sre = 3.2 stars, SRn = 7.0 stars
- **SRr = 3.6 stars required overall**

### Example 2 (Star Rating Formula — with internal alteration)

- Ae = 203.7 m², An = 20.8 m², Ani = 35.6 m²
- Sre = 2.0 stars, SRn = 7.0 stars
- **SRr = 3.3 stars required overall**

### Example 3 (Partial compliance — alteration triggers >50% volume)

Existing dwelling alteration exceeds 50% volume → triggers full compliance. Full compliance can't be achieved despite ceiling insulation, dampers, west-facing glazing shading, etc. Cost of dismantling external walls to add insulation justifies partial compliance.

### Example 4 (Partial compliance for WoH)

Alteration exceeds 50% volume → entire dwelling must comply. Existing has in-slab electric main space heating. Replacing the heating system is cost-prohibitive. Partial compliance proposal: install PV to get as close to H6P2 (~70% of regulated appliance energy use) as is reasonable.

## "Reasonable improvements" — DEECA 2023 technical analysis

The PN cites **non-exhaustive examples** of reasonable existing-fabric improvements (from 2023 DEECA technical analysis):

- Install/upgrade roof or ceiling insulation to flat/skillion roofs if existing insulation < **R2.5**
- Insulate external walls if external cladding or internal linings are removed as part of the renovation
- Floor insulation in living areas (rooms with heaters/ducted outlets) — subject to subfloor access and ground clearance
- Double glazing with argon fill and high solar gain low-emissivity coatings in living areas with heaters/ducted outlets

## Edge cases

### Heritage / light-coloured walls

Where heritage provisions require light-coloured walls (or other constraints prevent compliant exterior treatment), the PN suggests the RBS may accept: walls insulated to the highest R-value shown in the NCC 2022 wall insulation table for the climate zone.

### Shading constraints

Where planning or site constraints prevent external shading on existing parts of the building, reduced glazing performance can be accepted — but **only for existing parts**, not for an extension that exceeds Reg 233(6) thresholds.

### Building sealing on existing rooms

If extending an existing room and the existing part is poorly sealed (e.g. unsealed louvred glazing), full compliance with Part 13.4 may be unreasonable. Other major air leakage sources should still be addressed: chimney dampers, vent sealing, weatherstripping to doors and windows, self-closing dampers on exhaust fans.

### Lighting

May be unreasonable to change existing light fittings unless the alterations include complete re-wiring OR a safety hazard exists. If fittings ARE being replaced, they must comply (Part 13.7.6).

### Services-only work / no new appliances

If renovation doesn't involve installing/replacing fixed appliances, it may be reasonable to **not apply WoH requirements**.

### Wall height > 2.7/3 m or large eaves

Elemental DtS may not apply — use DtS NatHERS or Performance Solution instead. As a partial compliance setting: 15% of net external wall area shaded by >600 mm eave; walls >3 m insulated to highest R-value in the climate zone's table.

### Relocated homes

A re-erected, moved, or relocated dwelling is treated as an alteration **exceeding the 50% volume trigger** → RBS discretion to allow partial compliance applies. Best practice still expected: insulation to ceilings/walls/floor where accessible, sealing windows/doors, thermally efficient windows on replacement.

### Prefabricated kit homes / transportable buildings / dongas

Treated as **new dwellings** — must fully comply. Assessed via NatHERS (≥7 stars + WoH per Part 13.6) OR elemental DtS OR documented evidence. **Separate energy rating required for every new orientation/site condition** — can't reuse one rating for differently oriented placements.

### Replacement of existing elements

If replacing roof cladding, wall cladding, or wall lining: NCC 2022 fabric provisions should be achieved. Minor repair work doesn't trigger removal solely to install insulation — but where roof space access exists, fully compliant ceiling insulation should be installed.

## NCC 2019 vs NCC 2022 — key differences

| Item | NCC 2019 | NCC 2022 |
|---|---|---|
| Performance requirement language | Qualitative — "to the degree necessary" (P2.6.1, P2.6.2) | **Quantified** — Part H6, with specific values |
| Thermal target | 6-star (typical) | **7-star** under H6P1 / H6D2(1)(a) |
| Whole-of-Home | Services-only (P2.6.2) | **New WoH requirement (H6P2)** — score ≥60 for Class 1 |
| Glazing assessment | Each storey assessed separately | **Multi-storey buildings can be assessed together** |
| Glazing Calculator inputs | Basic | **New inputs:** room type (bedroom/utility/other), floor covering (concrete slab on ground), window openability, frame colour, ground floor vs upper |
| Star rating formula basis | Volume | **Area-based** (to align with NatHERS tools) |
| Cross-ventilation | Not assessed | Openable window % now feeds cross-ventilation calc — reduces summer cooling load target |

**The PN explicitly states it applies only to NCC 2022.** For NCC 2019 alterations, use the earlier Practice Note EE-04-2019.

## Documentation requirements (Table 2 in PN)

For NatHERS assessment of an existing dwelling, the assessor needs:
- Energy efficiency specifications (if separate to drawings)
- Floorplans, elevations, section drawings, site/context plans
- Electrical (incl. fans and downlights)
- List of hot water, lighting, heating/cooling, spa, pool pump appliances + star ratings
- On-site renewables details
- Drawing details, window system descriptions, window schedule (existing sizes + new), insulation details

## Building Appeals Board (BAB)

For modifications to a clause of the Regulations or NCC Volume 1/2, application can be made to the BAB. RBS advice should be sought first. Reference: www.buildingappeals.vic.gov.au

## Caveats

- **Victorian-specific.** The Reg 233 framework cited (Building Regulations 2018) is Victorian. Other states have different regulatory instruments for alterations (e.g. NSW BASIX, QLD BCA implementation). The NCC content (H6P1, H6P2, Part 13.2, Part 13.6, Housing Provisions clauses, NatHERS pathway, A2G2) is national.
- **NCC 2022 only.** NCC 2019 work uses EE-04-2019 — quantified vs qualitative performance requirements is the major shift.
- **Reg 233 is discretionary.** RBS holds the pen. Outcomes depend on the RBS's professional judgement — clients should not assume partial compliance will be granted.
- **PN Version 5.0, published 20 Dec 2023.** Superseded versions 4.0 (March 2023), 3.0 (March 2022), 2.0 (June 2021). Check for newer versions before quoting.
- **Form 18 is mandatory** for any partial compliance consent — must be recorded on the building permit.
