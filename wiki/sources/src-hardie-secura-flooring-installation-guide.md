# Hardie Secura Flooring Installation Guide (James Hardie, Sep 2025)

## Summary

James Hardie's installation guide (Australia, September 2025) for Hardie Secura Flooring — a fibre-cement structural flooring sheet for interior and exterior residential applications including tiled decks, covering joist spacings, fastener selection, loading capacities, waterproofing options and control joints. It is a structural/installation document, not a thermal one: it contains no R-values or insulation data. Relevant to NRG assessments mainly for identifying floor construction types (fibre-cement sheet flooring over timber or steel joists) and deck build-ups.

## Key claims

- Two sheet thicknesses: 19 mm (marked green on front corner edge, approx. 24.5 kg/m²) and 22 mm (marked grey, approx. 28.4 kg/m²), with tongue-and-groove long edges.
- Maximum residential floor joist centres: 450 mm for 19 mm sheets; 600 mm for 22 mm sheets (Table 1).
- Residential structural design: 1.8 kN concentrated load and 2 kPa UDL capacity per Category A1 of Table 3.1, AS/NZS 1170.1.
- Commercial (22 mm sheet) capacities: 450 mm centres without edge support = 3.6 kN point load / 5 kPa UDL; 450 mm with full edge support = 4.5 kN / 7.5 kPa; 600 mm centres = 2.7 kN / 5 kPa (Table 2).
- Suits timber framing (AS 1684) or light-gauge steel framing 0.75–1.9 mm BMT (NASH standard); minimum Class 3 fasteners recommended; joists nominally 45 mm wide minimum (narrow I-joists min. 40 mm; LVL solid joists min. 42 mm).
- Exterior decks: lay chamfer facing up; must be tiled within 3 months of installation; minimum 1:100 fall away from the building achieved by sloping joists, not packing sheets; step down from door sill of at least 50 mm (preferably 150 mm).
- Low-set decks need crossflow ventilation: minimum 50 mm clearance from bottom of joists to paved surfaces, or 150 mm to ground.
- Two exterior deck systems: water-resistant (prime with Ardex WPM 300 or equivalent and tile direct) and fully waterproof (third-party warranted system to AS 4654.1 — Ardex, Crommelin, Drizoro, Sika or Bostik listed at time of writing).
- Sheet control joints: squared layout at 2.7 m max in sheet direction; staggered layout (mortar bed system) at 4.1 m; internal tiled floors need control joints where floor dimensions exceed 5.4 m in the long-sheet direction.
- Internal orientation: chamfer up for tiles ("This side DOWN for TILES" notation), chamfer down for vinyl/carpet; 5 mm butt joints filled over a 10 mm foam backing rod with Hardie Joint Sealant.
- Fire: tested to AS/ISO 9239, critical radiant flux greater than the minimum 4.5 kW/m² of BCA Specification C1.10a, smoke development rate well below the maximum 750 percentage-minutes.
- Durability: passes AS/NZS 2908.2 moisture-resistance tests (water permeability, warm water, heat rain, soak dry) and has demonstrated termite resistance (CSIRO/Ensis testing).
- Silica safety: product contains crystalline silica — P2 (AS/NZS 1716) respirator minimum, M-class or higher vacuum extraction, never dry-sweep or use grinders.
