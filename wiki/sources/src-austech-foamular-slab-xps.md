# Austech Foamular XPS Under-Slab Insulation — Application Flyer

## Summary

One-page Austech application flyer for Foamular extruded polystyrene (XPS) used as a thermal barrier under residential on-ground slabs with floor heating systems, including slab perimeter insulation. Gives recommended FM 250 board thicknesses with R-values (30 mm R1.07, 50 mm R1.78) and basic installation guidance.

## Key claims

- Application is residential on-ground slabs with floor heating systems; Foamular acts as a thermal barrier under the slab and around the exposed slab perimeter.
- Recommended material: FM 250 at 30 mm thickness = R1.07; FM 250 at 50 mm thickness = R1.78.
- Insulating the slab minimises heat loss to the ground and external air, so less energy is required to maintain slab temperature with in-slab heating.
- Installation: Foamular is laid directly over the slab area on top of the plastic sheeting (vapour membrane), with supports and reinforcement placed over the Foamular.
- Foamular is stated as not affected by moisture, soil-borne bacteria or mould, so it can be backfilled against (e.g. for gardens) with no loss of thermal or physical properties.
- Austech distributes in NSW, QLD, VIC, WA, TAS and NZ.

## Concepts touched

insulation, flooring, slab-on-ground, XPS, under-slab insulation, slab edge insulation, hydronic floor heating, R-value

## Caveats

- Marketing/application flyer, not a formal technical datasheet — no test standard (e.g. AS/NZS 4859.1) or test conditions cited for the R-values.
- No publication date in the extracted text.
- R-values are for the FM 250 board material only, not a system Total R-value.
