# Design For Place — Telopea House Design Options (Melbourne)

## Summary

Australian Government Design For Place pack giving the building specifications that take the Telopea reference house (4 bed, 3 bath, 2 living, 182 m² two-storey, north to the side of the block) to 6.7, 7.1 and 7.2 NatHERS stars in the Melbourne climate. Uniquely in the series, the Melbourne Telopea upgrade path uses wall construction as a lever — conventional brick veneer at the lower option versus lightweight cladding on reverse brick veneer with a high performance anti-glare wall wrap (plus internal brickwork) at the higher options — alongside the usual glazing swap from low-e aluminium to uPVC double glazing; the ground floor stays on a 300 mm waffle pod slab in all options. Companion to the Telopea Melbourne Technical Drawings and the Brisbane/Hobart versions of the same design.

## Key claims

- Star rating options modelled for Melbourne: 6.7, 7.1 and 7.2. The Telopea 7.2 requires 30% less energy than a 6 star house.
- On average a 7 star home in Melbourne is predicted to use 27% less heating/cooling energy than a 6 star home; an 8 star home 53% less.
- Ground floor (all options): 100 mm concrete slab with 300 mm waffle pod — no under-slab insulation upgrade is listed for Melbourne, unlike the Hobart Telopea. Finishes: burnished concrete to living areas, carpet to bedrooms, ceramic tiles to wet areas.
- First floor (all options): timber joists with R4.0 insulation in floor framing between floors.
- Ceiling (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R5.0 batt insulation.
- Roof (all options): metal sheet roofing on timber battens with R1.5 reflective foil laminate.
- External walls, ground floor: brick veneer with R2.5 batt insulation and reflective foil membrane (vapour permeable if appropriate), 10 mm plasterboard for the lower option(s); lightweight cladding on reverse brick veneer with R2.0 batt insulation and high performance anti-glare wall wrap, 10 mm plasterboard for the higher option(s).
- External walls, first floor (all options): lightweight cladding on battens, reflective foil membrane (vapour permeable if appropriate), timber frame, R2.5 batt insulation, 10 mm plasterboard.
- Internal walls: ground floor 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R2.0 batt insulation for the lower option(s); 110 mm brickwork with R2.0 batt insulation to internal garage walls for the higher option(s); first floor 10 mm plasterboard with R2.0 batt insulation throughout.
- Windows: double glazed, low emissivity glass, aluminium frame for the lower option(s); double glazed, uPVC frame for the higher option(s).
- Eaves: 600 mm, optimised for adequate summer shading while still allowing winter solar heat gain.
- Draught sealing (all options): weather strips to all windows and sliding doors; seals/weather-strips to hinged external doors; sealed exhaust fans to bathroom, ensuite, powder room and kitchen rangehood.
- Ceiling fans (all options): 1400 mm diameter — one in each bedroom, one in the dining area, one in the front living area.
- Approximately 160 m² of roof space is available for solar panels; living area ceiling heights 2.7 m; includes a double carport.
- Reverse brick veneer (brickwork on the inside of the building) can increase thermal mass performance but is less common and can be more costly than conventional brick veneer.

## Caveats

- The specification table uses tick columns per star option; tick marks did not survive extraction — the exact split of wall/window upgrades between the 6.7, 7.1 and 7.2 columns is inferred from row ordering and narrative text. Verify against the source PDF.
- Ratings apply only to this exact design, specification and Melbourne location; site-specific assessment required.
