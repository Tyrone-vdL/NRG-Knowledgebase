# ACT Industry Information Paper — NCC 2022 Adoption and Transition (Sept 2023)

## Summary

ACT Government industry information paper (dated 15 September 2023) explaining when and how NCC 2022 and the ACT Appendix apply in the ACT, including the delayed 15 January 2024 commencement of the new energy efficiency, condensation mitigation and livable housing provisions, and summary tables showing how those provisions apply to renovations (substantial vs minor alterations, altered vs unaltered structure). This is the plain-English transition roadmap for ACT residential work moving from NCC 2019 to NCC 2022 requirements.

## Key claims

- NCC 2022 was published 1 October 2022; its main provisions took full effect in the ACT from **1 May 2023**, but the new **livable housing, energy efficiency and condensation mitigation requirements commenced in the ACT on 15 January 2024**.
- From 1 May 2023, applications for building approvals (and building work not requiring approval) must comply with BCA 2022 plus the ACT Appendix variations.
- From **1 May 2023 to 14 January 2024**, industry had to continue meeting the minimum energy efficiency and condensation requirements of **NCC 2019** (and the ACT Appendix), and had to continue using the **NCC 2019 version of NatHERS software and certificates**.
- A design meeting NCC 2022 energy efficiency/condensation requirements during the transition would still satisfy NCC 2019 minimums — no need to amend an NCC 2022-compliant design.
- From 15 January 2024, **all new residential dwellings** in the ACT must meet the new energy efficiency requirements, and **all new Class 1a and Class 2 dwellings** must meet the new livable housing requirements.
- Key ACT instruments: Building Act 2004; Building (ACT Appendix to the Building Code) Determination; Building (General) Regulation 2008; Building (Alternative requirements for unaltered parts) Determination.
- **Substantial alteration definition (ACT):** under the Building (General) Regulation 2008, a building undergoing a substantial alteration must comply with the BCA in force at the time of building approval (whole building, not just the altered area). A substantial alteration exists if, during the 3 years immediately before the building approval application, the total floor area of the proposed alteration (excluding internal alterations for Class 1) is **more than 50% of the floor area of the original building**.
- Table 1 (livable housing applied to alterations): dwelling access — No in all alteration cases; dwelling entrance — generally No unless the building work includes dwelling access and there is no other accessible entry; internal doors/corridors, toilet (entry floor), shower, and reinforced bathroom/toilet walls — Yes for altered-structure work (with exemptions, e.g. internal thresholds exempted per Part 3.2 of the ABCB Livable Housing Design Standard, and toilet/shower only if part of the approval and no other compliant one exists); unaltered structure — No.
- Table 2 (energy efficiency applied to alterations): thermal performance, ceiling/wall/floor insulation, non-isolated glazing and building sealing apply (Yes/Partial) to altered structure for extensions, minor alterations and substantial alterations; unaltered structure generally No (except substantial alterations where ceiling/wall/floor insulation, glazing and sealing become Yes and thermal performance "Partial"); isolated glazing No; the **new Annual Energy Budget (Whole-of-Home) does not apply to alterations at all** (No in every column).
- Footnote confirms "the ACT is maintaining the current policy position" on the energy efficiency rows.

## Caveats

- Dated 15 September 2023 — a transition document. The transition window it describes (1 May 2023 – 14 Jan 2024) has now passed; from 15 January 2024 the NCC 2022 provisions and the ACT Appendix Determination 2023 (No 3) Schedule 2 apply.
- Tables 1 and 2 are explicitly "summary tables of highly technical information" — the determinative documents are the Building (ACT Appendix to the Building Code) Determination 2023 and the Building (General) (Alternative requirements for unaltered parts) Determination 2023.
- Table layout degraded in PDF extraction (column headers scrambled); the Yes/No entries above were reconstructed from footnote logic — verify cell-level detail against the source PDF before client advice.
