# VBA Practice Note EE-04 — Alterations to Existing Class 1 Buildings (NCC 2019)

## Summary

Victorian Building Authority Practice Note EE-04 (Version 3.0, published 10 March 2022) on energy efficiency compliance for alterations and extensions to existing Class 1 buildings under NCC 2019, the Building Act 1993 and Building Regulations 2018 — the NCC 2019-edition predecessor of the NCC 2022 version already catalogued (see src-ee-04-alterations-ncc-2022.md). It sets out the Regulation 233 framework (50% volume / 25% floor area triggers, RBS consent to partial compliance via Form 18), volume calculation guidance with worked examples, the volume-based star rating formula against the 6-star target, elemental DtS guidance, and rules for relocated and prefabricated homes.

## Key claims

- Applies to NCC 2019: compliance means satisfying performance requirements **P2.6.1** (building fabric) and **P2.6.2** (services) — the qualitative "to the degree necessary" requirements, replaced in NCC 2022 by the quantified H6P1/H6P2.
- Regulation 233(2): where the volume of proposed alterations plus alterations completed in the previous 3 years exceeds 50% of the original building's volume, the entire building must be brought into compliance with the Regulations. Reg 233(3) gives the RBS discretionary power to consent to partial compliance; consent must be in Form 18 and recorded on the building permit. An application can also be made to the BAB under section 160 of the Act.
- Regulation 233(6): the RBS can consider partial compliance for an extension only if its floor area is not greater than the lesser of 25% of the existing building's floor area and 1000 m²; beyond that, the extension itself must fully comply, though partial compliance may still apply to the existing building.
- **Volume calculation guidance (unique to this edition):** no prescribed method exists, so the RBS applies professional judgement. Guidance: sum the building spaces above the sub-floor defined by enclosing external walls, roof space/structure, and roofed areas (verandahs etc.). Worked Example 1: alteration 3.3 × 5.7 × 3 m = 56.43 m³ against an existing volume of 7.3 × 9.3 × 3 m = 203.67 m³ → 28% alteration. For a window/door alteration, volume = only the portion of wall affected. This method does not apply to NatHERS volume figures.
- NatHERS used for the whole building (existing + alteration) achieving the **6-star** requirement is a DtS solution; where only part of the building must comply, use either a consent to partial compliance or a Performance Solution under BCA clause A2.2. Obtaining consent to partial compliance is a separate process from a Performance Solution.
- Partial compliance via NatHERS requires two ratings by the same assessor in the same software version: an initial rating of the existing dwelling as constructed, and a rating of the whole proposed design meeting the required overall star rating from the formula.
- **Star rating formula is volume-based in this edition**: SRr = (Ve × SRe + Vn × SRn) / (Ve + Vn), with an internal-alteration variant using Vni. Worked examples: Ve 203.7 m³, Vn 20.8 m³, SRe 3.2, SRn 6.0 → SRr 3.5 stars; with Vni 35.6 m³ and SRe 2.0 → SRr 3.0 stars. (The NCC 2022 edition revised this to an area-based formula with a 7.0-star SRn.)
- Volumes in the formula are NatHERS conditioned/rated space volumes — not the Reg 233 building-volume calculation.
- Glazing under NCC 2019 (BCA Part 3.12.2) is assessed on a whole-storey basis — glazing in the existing part of the storey must be included when assessing an extension; a Performance Solution may apply new-glazing performance uniformly across the storey while only requiring complying glazing in the new work. (NCC 2022 later allowed multi-storey assessment together.)
- Building sealing (BCA Part 3.12.3): if the existing part is unsealed (e.g. large unsealed louvres), there may be little benefit sealing the new part; a practical approach is sealed doors between new and existing parts. Air movement provisions (Part 3.12.4, climate zone 4) may be impossible to meet in some extensions.
- Services: BCA 3.12.5.0 does not apply to heated water supply systems in Victoria (Plumbing Regulations 2018 govern); insulation of service piping and ductwork (3.12.5.1-3.12.5.3) must always be provided. Lighting (3.12.5.5): unreasonable to replace existing fittings unless the building is fully rewired, but replacements must comply.
- Victorian variation: new Class 1 buildings must have a rainwater tank connected to sanitary flushing or a solar water heater (Plumbing Regulations 2018). A solar hot water system may be considered as a partial-compliance trade-off or Performance Solution element; a rainwater tank cannot be used as an energy efficiency Performance Solution. Schedule 2 clause 11 standards do not apply to alterations.
- Relocated homes are treated as alterations exceeding the 50% volume trigger (RBS partial-compliance discretion applies); prefabricated/kit/transportable homes (dongas, on-site cabins) are new dwellings requiring 6 stars via NatHERS or elemental DtS, plus the solar hot water/rainwater tank requirement, with a separate energy rating for every new orientation/site condition.

## Caveats

- **Superseded for current work.** Applies only to projects assessed under NCC 2019; NCC 2022 projects use Practice Note EE-04 (NCC 2022, v5.0, 20 Dec 2023) — catalogued at src-ee-04-alterations-ncc-2022.md — which moves to H6P1/H6P2, 7 stars, Whole-of-Home, and an area-based formula.
- Victorian-specific: the Reg 233 framework is from the Victorian Building Regulations 2018; other states use different instruments for alterations.
- Version 3.0 (10 March 2022), superseding Version 2.0 (28 June 2021).
