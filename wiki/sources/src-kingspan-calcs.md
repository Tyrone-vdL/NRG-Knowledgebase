# Kingspan FabCalc Thermal Computation — Concrete Wall with Kooltherm K12 (for NRG, Oct 2024)

## Summary

A single-page Kingspan Insulation FabCalc thermal performance computation (ref 3F1BAB50BD, dated 11 Oct 2024) prepared for Matthew Morelli of NRG Efficient Homes, verifying the Total R-value of a 150 mm solid concrete wall internally lined with Kooltherm K12 on 28 mm metal furring channels plus 13 mm gypsum board. Result: Total R-value 0.8 m²K/W (U-value 1.32 W/m²K) for both summer and winter heat-flow directions.

## Key claims

- Calculated per AS/NZS 4859.2:2018, with thermal bridging allowances per NZS 4214:2006, in accordance with NCC 2019 Volume One.
- Build-up (outside to inside): outdoor air film (R0.040 summer / R0.030 winter); 150.0 mm solid concrete at 2400 kg/m³ (R0.104 both seasons); 25.0 mm Kooltherm K12 (product R-value 1.09/1.09 m²K/W in/out) on 28 mm metal furring channel at 450 mm centres, 7.8% bridging fraction (layer R0.417 summer / R0.426 winter); 13.0 mm gypsum board (R0.076); indoor air film (R0.120).
- Total R-value: 0.8 m²K/W summer and 0.8 m²K/W winter; overall U-value: 1.32 W/m²K both directions.
- Boundary conditions: summer — indoor 24.0°C, outdoor 36.0°C, ΔT 12.0 K; winter — indoor 18.0°C, outdoor 12.0°C, ΔT 6.0 K.
- The metal furring channel bridging at 450 mm centres cuts the 25 mm K12 layer's effective contribution from its nominal R1.09 to roughly R0.42 in the assembly — a worked example of how steel bridging degrades rigid-board performance.
- Calculation is stated as valid only for the exact products and profile specified; produced with FabCalc 2.06z by Michael Thiel, Technical Adviser, Kingspan Insulation (1300 247 235).

## Caveats

- Calculated against NCC 2019 Volume One — figures remain physically valid but the referenced code edition predates NCC 2022.
- Project-specific computation for one wall profile only; not transferable to other build-ups, thicknesses or framing spacings.
