# TAS Building Act 2016 s 53 — Existing Buildings to be Upgraded if Altered (Partial Compliance)

## Summary

Section 53 of the Tasmanian Building Act 2016 (Division 1 — Works on existing buildings) requires that where building work is performed on more than half of a building's original volume within 3 years, the owner must bring the entire building into compliance with the Act and the applicable National Construction Code provisions — but the building surveyor may consent to *partial compliance* subject to structural adequacy, amenity/health, fire-spread and historic-building considerations, and subject to extension-size limits. ## Key claims

- Heading: "53. Existing buildings to be upgraded if altered", within "Division 1 – Works on existing buildings", Building Act 2016 (Tas). Version note on screenshot: "Version current from 1 October 2024 to date (accessed 26 March 2025 at 6:40)".
- s 53(1): the section applies to building work performed on a building if, within the previous 3 years, building work has been performed or permitted on more than half of the original volume of the building.
- s 53(1A): the section does not apply to building work performed on a building if — (a) the work is being performed to repair damage to the building (i) caused by fire, wind, flood or storm or the subsidence of land, other than a subsidence caused by a failure of the owner or occupier of the building to maintain the land, or (ii) that has not been caused by an act, or a failure to act, of the owner or an occupier of the building; and (b) the work is necessary for the building to be made suitable for habitation or safe occupation, or for use for the purposes for which it was being used before the damage; and (c) the work consists of work to restore the building to the condition it was in before the damage; and (d) the materials, components, installations and equipment used are substantially similar to those used in the part of the building to which the works relate before the damage.
- s 53(1B): the section does not apply to a building, or class of buildings, that a determination of the Director of Building Control states are exempt from the requirements of the section.
- s 53(2): if building work to which the section applies is performed on an existing building, the owner must ensure that the entire building, as part of that building work, is brought into compliance with the Act and the applicable provisions of the National Construction Code.
- s 53(3): the building surveyor engaged in respect of the building work may consent to partial compliance with the applicable provisions of the National Construction Code in respect of the work if satisfied the partial compliance is appropriate, after taking into account — (a) the structural adequacy of the existing building; (b) the requirements necessary to make reasonable provision for (i) the amenity of the building and the safety and health of the people using, or intending to use, the building, and (ii) appropriate measures to ensure that fire cannot spread to, or from, any adjoining building; and (c) whether the building is a historic building.
- s 53(4): the relevant building surveyor must NOT consent to partial compliance under subsection (3) for building work on an existing building if the work involves an extension of the existing building — (a) where the floor area of the extension exceeds (i) 25 percent of the floor area of the building before the commencement of the extension, or (ii) 1 000 square metres; or (b) that a determination of the Director of Building Control makes ineligible for partial compliance.
- s 53(5): nothing in the Act requires a building that is not the subject of work under the Act to be upgraded to comply with the National Construction Code unless the building is the subject of a notice, or an order, under Part 18.
- Practical use for energy assessors: Tasmania's analogue of the QLD half-volume rule ([[src-qld-building-act-1975-sect-81]]) — the half-original-volume/3-year trigger forces whole-building NCC compliance, with a partial-compliance escape valve capped at extensions of 25% of prior floor area or 1,000 m².
