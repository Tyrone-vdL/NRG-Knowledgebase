# ABCB Housing Provisions Standard 2022 — Section 13 Energy Efficiency + Part 10.8 Condensation (surgical extract)

## Summary

The ABCB Housing Provisions Standard 2022 (1 May 2023 print) is the Deemed-to-Satisfy detail document for Class 1/10 buildings that NCC 2022 Volume Two references into — its Section 13 contains the elemental energy-efficiency provisions (building fabric R-values per climate zone, external glazing calculations, building sealing, ceiling fans, whole-of-home energy usage, and services) that H6D2 and S42C4 of Volume Two direct compliance to. This page deep-extracts Section 13 (Parts 13.1–13.7) and Part 10.8 (condensation management: wall vapour permeance, exhaust systems, roof space ventilation). This is the document that closes the loop on every "Section 13" cross-reference in [[src-ncc-2022-vol-2-h6-energy-efficiency]].

## What the document is

- The ABCB Housing Provisions Standard 2022 replaced the "acceptable construction practices" of NCC 2019 Volume Two Part 3.x. Section 13 was formerly Part 3.12 (energy efficiency); Part 10.8 was formerly 3.8.7 (condensation). Each clause carries its 2019 mapping, quoted below as [2019: x.x.x].
- How it's invoked: **Part 13.2 (fabric) and 13.4 (sealing)** apply as directed in H6D2(1)(a) or (b); **13.3 (glazing) and 13.5 (ceiling fans)** as directed in H6D2(1)(b) (elemental pathway only); **13.6 and 13.7** as directed in H6D2(2). On the NatHERS pathway, S42C4 still pulls in 13.2.2, the thermal break and floor-edge clauses, and Parts 13.4 and 13.7.

## Document structure (navigation)

Sections: 2 Structure; 3 Site preparation; 4 Footings and slabs; 5 Masonry; 6 Framing; 7 Roof and wall cladding; 8 Glazing; 9 Fire safety; 10 Health and amenity (10.2 wet areas, 10.5 light, 10.6 ventilation, 10.7 sound, **10.8 condensation management**); 11 Safe movement and access; 12 Ancillary provisions; **13 Energy efficiency**; Schedule 1 Definitions; Schedule 2 Referenced documents; Schedules 3–11 state/territory variations (NSW, NT, TAS vary multiple Part 13 clauses; e.g. NSW deletes 13.7.9 — spa pools — "13.7.9 does not apply in NSW").

Section 13 parts: 13.1 Scope; 13.2 Building fabric; 13.3 External glazing; 13.4 Building sealing; 13.5 Ceiling fans; 13.6 Whole-of-home energy usage; 13.7 Services.

## Part 13.1 — Scope and application

13.1.1 [New for 2022]: Section 13 sets out DtS provisions for building fabric (13.2), external glazing (13.3), building sealing (13.4), ceiling fans (13.5), whole-of-home energy usage (13.6) and services (13.7). 13.1.2: subject to the Governing Requirements of NCC Volume Two and state/territory schedules.

## Part 13.2 — Building fabric

### 13.2.1 Application [2019: 3.12.1]

13.2.2–13.2.6 apply to a Class 1 building and a Class 10a building with a conditioned space; 13.2.7 applies to a Class 1 with attached Class 10a.

### 13.2.2 Building fabric thermal insulation [2019: 3.12.1.1]

- Insulation must comply with **AS/NZS 4859.1** and be installed abutting/overlapping adjoining insulation (butting at studs/joists etc.), forming a continuous barrier with ceilings/walls/bulkheads/floors, and not affecting safe operation of domestic services.
- Reflective insulation: necessary airspace to achieve required R-Value; closely fitted at penetrations/doors/windows; supported by framing; adjoining roll-membrane sheets overlapped **≥ 150 mm** or taped.
- Bulk insulation: must maintain position and thickness; in a ceiling with no bulk/reflective insulation in the wall beneath, must overlap the external wall by **≥ 50 mm**.

### 13.2.3 Roofs and ceilings [2019: 3.12.1.2]

- (1) Roof/ceiling insulation must achieve minimum R-Values from per-climate-zone tables: CZ1 Tables 13.2.3a/b (single vs two-storey) + 13.2.3j/k; CZ2 13.2.3c+l; CZ3 13.2.3d+m; CZ4 13.2.3e+n; CZ5 13.2.3f+o; CZ6 13.2.3g+p; CZ7 13.2.3h+q; CZ8 13.2.3i+r. Tables a–i are pitched roof with horizontal ceiling; j–r are flat/skillion/cathedral. Required R-Value varies with roof ventilation status (vented vs standard), presence of reflective insulation under-roof, under-roof insulation R-Value, and roof solar absorptance (SA) band (SA bands ≤0.23 / 0.23–0.32 / 0.32–0.42 / 0.42–0.53 / 0.53–0.64; CZ6–8 tables extend to 0.96). "Vented" = one wind-driven ventilator per 50 m² of ceiling area or one powered ventilator per 200 m² (with gable/eave/ridge vents), or a tiled roof without sarking; in CZ6–8 roof ventilation must comply with 10.8.3.
- Representative anchor values (flat/skillion/cathedral, ceiling insulation R-Value, reflective-under-roof Yes/No across SA bands low→high): **CZ2 (Table 13.2.3l):** Yes → 2.5 all bands; No → 3.0/3.0/3.5/4.0/4.0. **CZ3 (13.2.3m):** Yes → 2.0/2.0/2.5/2.5/2.5; No → 3.5/4.0/5.0/X/X (X = not permitted). **CZ7 (13.2.3q):** Yes → 4.5/4.5/4.5/4.0/4.0/4.0/4.0/4.0; No → 5.0/4.5/4.5/4.0/4.0/3.5/3.5/3.5. **CZ8 (13.2.3r):** Yes → 4.0/4.0/4.0/4.0/4.0/3.5/3.5/3.5; No → 4.5/4.0/4.0/3.5/3.5/3.0/3.0/3.0. (Full tables degraded in text extraction — verify against the PDF before quoting in client work.)
- (2) Reflective insulation used for (1): surface emittance **≤ 0.05**; adjacent roof space **≥ 20 mm**; in CZ3–8 must be **downward facing**.
- (3) Metal-framed roof thermal bridging — pitched/horizontal-ceiling options: (i) achieve Total R-Value in Table 13.2.3s (insulation R1.5→Total R1.38; 2.0→1.74; 2.5→2.09; 3.0→2.43; 3.5→2.63; 4.0→2.95; 4.5→3.27; 5.0→3.59; 5.5→3.91; 6.0→4.23); or (ii) increase between-frame insulation by **R0.5**; or (iii) add continuous ceiling insulation layer **≥ R0.13** above/below joists; or (iv) stack two layers with the top layer covering the joists at **≥ R0.5**. Flat/skillion/cathedral: Total R-Value per Table 13.2.3t (e.g. R3.0 → Total 3.11 down / 3.02 up) or options per Table 13.2.3u (e.g. ceiling R2.0 → increase between-frame to R3.5, or add R0.30 continuous; ≥R3.5 → only the continuous-layer option, R0.60, is available).
- Direction of heat flow (Table 13.2.3v): CZ1 down; CZ2 below 300 m altitude down, 300 m+ down and up; CZ3 down and up; CZ4–8 up.
- (4) Where 10.8.3(1) applies, continuous insulation above the primary insulation layer must have vapour permeance not less than the primary layer.
- (5) Lost ceiling insulation area (downlights, fans, flues) must be compensated per Table 13.2.3w (e.g. 0.5–1.0% area uninsulated with base R4.0 → R4.7 elsewhere; interpolation allowed; large losses with high R-Values not permitted).
- (6) Ceiling insulation R3.0–R4.5 may reduce to R3.0 within 450 mm of an external wall; above R4.5 the same reduction is allowed only if all other ceiling insulation is increased by R0.5.
- (7) Metal sheet roofing directly fixed to metal purlins/rafters/battens (no ceiling lining, or lining fixed directly to those members) requires a **thermal break ≥ R0.2** between sheeting and supports.
- (8)–(9) Insulated sandwich panel roofs are exempt from (1)–(7) but must achieve Total R-Values in Table 13.2.3x (e.g. CZ6: 3.57 across all SA bands; CZ8 at SA ≤0.23: 3.90; CZ1 single-storey at SA ≤0.23: 1.40; many high-SA cells not permitted).
- (10) **In climate zones 1–5 the solar absorptance of the upper roof surface must not be more than 0.64** (i.e. no dark roofs on the elemental pathway in CZ1–5).

### 13.2.4 Roof lights [2019: 3.12.1.3]

Roof lights serving a habitable room or interconnecting space: total area **≤ 5% of the floor area** served; **Total System U-Value ≤ U3.9**; Total System SHGC per Table 13.2.4 by roof light shaft index — shaft index <1.0: SHGC ≤0.45 (area ≤3.5%) or ≤0.29 (3.5–5%); index 1.0–2.5: ≤0.51 / ≤0.33; index ≥2.5: ≤0.76 / ≤0.49. Values are AFRC ratings.

### 13.2.5 External walls [2019: 3.12.1.4]

- (1) Wall insulation minimum R-Values from per-CZ tables 13.2.5a–13.2.5o (CZ1: 13.2.5a concrete block / 13.2.5b lightweight; CZ2: c+d; CZ3: e (f lightweight); CZ4: g+h; CZ5: i+j; CZ6: k+l; CZ7: m+n; CZ8: 13.2.5o). For CZ1 concrete block walls the required insulation depends on SA, eave overhang (0–2400 mm bands) and wall height (≤2.4 / 2.4–2.7 / 2.7–3.0 / 3.0–3.6 m) — large overhangs + low SA can reduce the requirement to reflective-only or none; some short-overhang/tall-wall combinations are not permitted.
- (2) **Lightweight wall construction**: use the designated lightweight table for CZ1/CZ3/CZ8; in CZ2, 4, 5, 6 and 7 use the standard table **with R0.3 added**.
- (3) **In climate zones 1–5 the solar absorptance of the outer wall surface must not be more than 0.7.**
- (4) Metal-framed wall thermal bridging: achieve Total R-Value per Tables 13.2.5p/q/r calculated to **AS/NZS 4859.2** (e.g. required R2.7 → Total R2.15 (one table) / R2.44 (veneer table)), or comply with options in Tables 13.2.5s/t/u — e.g. lightweight metal-framed walls with insulation >R1.5: install reflective insulation outside the frame creating a ≥20 mm reflective airspace, or add ≥R0.30 continuous insulation inside/outside the frame; reflective surfaces facing an airspace must have emittance ≤ 0.1.
- (5) Metal-framed envelope wall with no lining (or lining fixed directly to the frame) clad with weatherboards, fibre-cement or metal sheeting: **thermal break ≥ R0.2** at all cladding/frame contact points. Explanatory: EPS strips ≥12 mm or timber ≥20 mm are deemed R≥0.2. (6) Sandwich panel walls exempt.
- Explanatory: continuous insulation outside the primary insulation layer may also be subject to the vapour permeance requirements of 10.8.1(2); many perforated reflective products are not AS 4200.1 water barriers and can't serve as the water control layer.

### 13.2.6 Floors and subfloor walls [2019: 3.12.1.5]

- (1) Floor over an **unenclosed space**: minimum R-Value per Table 13.2.6a (table degraded in extraction; the legible CZ6 entry is "4.0, or 3.5 if used in conjunction with a reflective airspace" — verify other zones against the PDF).
- (2) Floor over an **enclosed subfloor**: CZ1 — subfloor wall insulation R1.5; CZ2 — Table 13.2.6b (≤600 mm wall: R0.5, rising to R1.5 at 900–1800 mm; **under-floor insulation not permitted in CZ2**); CZ3 — Table 13.2.6c (R0.5 all heights; under-floor insulation not permitted in CZ3); CZ4–8 — Tables 13.2.6d–h giving combinations of subfloor-wall R-Value + suspended-floor R-Value, varying with subfloor wall height and whether reflective insulation faces down over the subfloor (e.g. CZ8, wall ≤600 mm, no reflective: floor R2.5; with reflective: floor R1.5).
- (3) Metal-framed floor thermal bridging: Total R-Value per Table 13.2.6i (e.g. insulation R2.0 → Total R1.97 carpet / R1.83 other coverings) or options per Table 13.2.6j (e.g. R1.5 → increase between-frame to R2.5 or add R0.40 continuous; ≥R3.0 → continuous R0.60 only). Heat flow direction (Table 13.2.6k): CZ1–3 up; CZ4–8 down.
- (4) Concrete slab-on-ground with **in-slab/in-screed heating or cooling**: vertical edge insulation **R ≥ 1.0** around the perimeter.
- (5) Except for waffle-pod slabs: **CZ6 and 7** — slab edge insulation **R ≥ 0.64** plus **R ≥ 0.64 underneath the slab**; **CZ8** — edge **R ≥ 1.0** plus **R ≥ 2.0 underneath**.
- (6) Edge insulation per (4)/(5) must be **water resistant** and continuous from finished ground level to ≥ 300 mm depth or the full slab edge depth (Figure 13.2.6).
- (7) (4) does not apply to in-screed systems used solely in bathrooms/amenity areas.

### 13.2.7 Attached Class 10a buildings [2019: 3.12.1.6]

A Class 10a attached to a Class 1 must either have external fabric achieving the Class 1 thermal performance, or be separated from the Class 1 by construction achieving it (i.e. an uninsulated garage is fine if the house/garage interface is treated as envelope).

## Part 13.3 — External glazing (applies via H6D2(1)(b) elemental pathway)

- **13.3.2 External glazing — winter** [2019: 3.12.2.1]: in CZ2–8, the conductance (Cu) and solar heat gain (CSHGC) of glazing in each storey must not exceed allowances from Table 13.3.2a, calculated as area-weighted sums over every glazing element using: Total System U-Value, Total System SHGC (**capped at 0.7**), winter exposure factors E (Tables 13.3.2b–q, orientation- and P/H-dependent), bedroom conductance factor BC (13.3.2r), orientation sector conductance factor OC (13.3.2s–y per CZ), and room-type/bedroom/floor-level/frame factors (13.3.2z–ag, including separate CZ5 tables for concrete/masonry walls and a 0.85 floor factor for tiled/vinyl floors in direct ground contact). Orientation sectors per Figure 13.3.2a; shading geometry P/H per Figure 13.3.2b.
- **13.3.3 External glazing — summer**: parallel calculation against Table 13.3.3a allowances with summer exposure factors (13.3.3b–q) and summer solar heat gain factors (13.3.3r–y).
- **13.3.4 Shading** [2019: 3.12.2.2]: required shading must be (a) an external permanent projection (verandah, balcony, fixed canopy, eaves, shading hood, carport) extending horizontally on both sides of the glazing for ≥ the projection distance P (or equivalent via reveal); or (b) an external shading device (shutter, blind, screen with blades/battens/slats) **capable of restricting at least 80% of summer solar radiation**, and if adjustable, readily operable manually/mechanically/electronically by occupants. A device complying with 13.3.4(b) is considered to achieve P/H = 2.00.
- The factor tables (roughly 60 of them) are not transcribable from extracted text — use NatHERS software or the PDF directly for any actual glazing calc.

## Part 13.4 — Building sealing

- **13.4.1 Application** [2019: 3.12.3]: Class 1 + conditioned Class 10a. Exemptions: buildings in **CZ1, 2, 3 and 5 where the only air-conditioning is an evaporative cooler**; permanent ventilation openings needed for safe gas appliance operation. (Evaporatively cooled buildings in CZ4 and 6 must still be sealed.)
- **13.4.2 Chimneys/flues** [2019: 3.12.3.1]: open solid-fuel appliances need a closable damper or flap.
- **13.4.3 Roof lights** [2019: 3.12.3.2]: must be sealed/sealable when serving a conditioned space, or a habitable room in CZ4–8 — via imperforate ceiling diffuser, weatherproof seal, or occupant-operable shutter system.
- **13.4.4 External windows and doors** [2019: 3.12.3.3]: seal external doors, doors to unconditioned Class 10a, openable windows and other openings serving a conditioned space or habitable room in CZ4–8. Door bottom edge: draft protection device; other edges: foam/rubber compressible strip, fibrous seal or like. Windows meeting AS 2047 maximum air infiltration rates are deemed to comply for edges.
- **13.4.5 Exhaust fans** [2019: 3.12.3.4]: sealing device (self-closing damper, filter, or like) in the same spaces.
- **13.4.6 Ceilings, walls, floors** [2019: 3.12.3.5]: construct to minimise air leakage — close-fitting internal linings at junctions, or seal junctions/penetrations with architrave/skirting/cornice or expanding foam/compressible strip/caulking.
- **13.4.7 Evaporative coolers** [2019: 3.12.3.6]: self-closing damper or like when serving a heated space or habitable room in CZ4–8.

13.5.2 [2019: 3.12.4.3]: ceiling fans must be installed per Table 13.5.2 in **CZ1, 2 and 3**, and in **CZ5 in NSW and Queensland**. Table 13.5.2 (room size → bedroom requirement (CZ1–3) / other habitable room requirement (CZ1,2,3,5 NSW+Qld)):

## Part 13.6 — Whole-of-home energy usage [New for 2022]

- 13.6.2: the **net equivalent energy usage** (floor area factor × efficiency factor E, plus pool pump energy EP, plus spa pump energy ES, minus installed on-site PV capacity ER in kW) must not exceed the allowance (floor area factor × energy factor EF from Table 13.6.2b). E comes from the **ABCB Standard for Whole-of-Home Efficiency Factors** (separate document, not ingested).
- Floor area adjustment factor (Table 13.6.2a): <50 m² → 0.0123, sliding down to 500 m² → 0.0075 (total floor area measured inside external walls, including conditioned attached Class 10a; round up to next bracket).
- Energy factor EF (Table 13.6.2b) is per climate zone and state — e.g. Qld CZ1 2.77, Qld CZ2 1.78, Qld CZ3 2.46, Qld CZ5 2.28; NSW CZ2 1.32, NSW CZ5 1.75, NSW CZ6 2.40, NSW CZ8 3.99; Vic CZ6 1.63, Vic CZ7 1.62; WA CZ5 2.35; Tas CZ7 3.08. (Dashes = combination not applicable.)
- Pool pump factor (Table 13.6.2c, kW/1000 L·annum) varies by GEMS star rating and state — e.g. Qld: 1-star/unrated 0.046 down to 10-star 0.005. Spa pump factor (Table 13.6.2d, kW/100 L·annum): all types, e.g. Qld 0.055, NSW 0.071.

## Part 13.7 — Services

- **13.7.1 Application** [2019: 3.12.5]: Class 1, Class 10a, and Class 10b swimming pools associated with them; applied as directed in H6D2(2) (and via S42C4(2) on the NatHERS pathway).
- **13.7.2 Insulation of services** [2019: 3.12.5.1]: insulation for central heating water piping and HVAC ductwork must be weather/sunlight protected, withstand operating temperatures, and comply with AS/NZS 4859.1.
- **13.7.3 Central heating water piping** [2019: 3.12.5.2]: minimum **material** R-Values for piping outside conditioned space — internal piping (unventilated wall, between-storey floor, between ceiling insulation and ceiling, or encased in slab other than floor heating): **R0.4 all climate zones**. Piping in ventilated wall/enclosed subfloor/roof space: **CZ1,2,3,5 → R0.6; CZ4,6,7 → R0.9; CZ8 → R1.3**. Piping outside the building or in unenclosed subfloor/roof space: **CZ1,2,3,5 → R0.6; CZ4,6,7 → R1.3; CZ8 → R1.3**. (Explanatory: 9 mm closed cell polymer ≈ R0.4; 13 mm ≈ R0.6; 19 mm ≈ R0.9; 25 mm closed cell or 25 mm glasswool ≈ R1.3.)
- **13.7.4 Heating and cooling ductwork** [2019: 3.12.5.3]: ductwork + fittings must be sealed to a **Class C seal per AS 4254.1/AS 4254.2** (flexible duct: draw band + sealant/adhesive tape). Minimum material R-Values: heating-only or cooling-only (incl. evaporative): ductwork **R1.0 in CZ1–7, R1.5 in CZ8**; fittings R0.4. Combined heating + refrigerated cooling: **R1.5 in CZ1,3,4,6,7; R1.0 in CZ2 and 5; R1.5 in CZ8**; fittings R0.4; the R1.5 may reduce by 0.5 where ducts are under an enclosed-perimeter suspended floor or in a roof space with ≥R0.5 directly beneath the roofing. Exempt where ductwork is within the insulated envelope. (Explanatory: 45 mm 11 kg/m³ glasswool ≈ R1.0 flexible duct; 11 mm polyurethane ≈ R0.4 fittings.)
- **13.7.5 Electric resistance space heating** [2019: 3.12.5.4]: systems serving more than one room need separate isolating switches per room, separate temperature controller + time switch per group of rooms, and power loads **≤ 110 W/m² (living areas) / ≤ 150 W/m² (bathrooms)**.
- **13.7.6 Artificial lighting** [2019: 3.12.5.5]: lamp/illumination power density limits — **5 W/m² in a Class 1 building; 4 W/m² on verandahs/balconies attached to it; 3 W/m² in an associated Class 10a**. Allowance may be increased by dividing by control-device adjustment factors: corridor timer 0.7; motion detector 0.9 (≥75% area or <200 m² block) / 0.7 (≤6 lights per block) / 0.55 (≤2 lights); manual or programmable dimming ≥75% area 0.85; daylight-linked dynamic control adjacent to windows 0.5 (windows) / 0.6 (roof lights). Perimeter (external) lighting: daylight sensor or average efficacy ≥ 40 lumens/W. Halogen lamps must be switched separately from fluorescent.
- **13.7.7 Water heater** [2019: 3.12.5.6]: a water heater in a heated water supply system must be designed/installed per **Part B2 of NCC Volume Three** (Plumbing Code).
- **13.7.8 Swimming pool heating and pumping** [2019: 3.12.5.7]: pool heating must be solar (not boosted by electric resistance), reclaimed energy, gas, heat pump, or a combination. Gas/heat-pump-heated pools need a **cover with minimum R-Value 0.05** (unless in a conditioned space) and a heater time switch; circulation pumps need a time switch. Spa pools excluded from this clause.
- **13.7.9 Spa pool heating and pumping** [2019: 3.12.5.8]: same heating source list for spas sharing recirculation with a pool; gas/heat-pump-heated spas need a cover and a push button + time switch on the heater; circulation pump time switch where capacity ≥ 680 L. **NSW deletes this clause** (NSW 13.7.9: does not apply).

## Part 10.8 — Condensation management

- **10.8.1 External wall construction** [2019: 3.8.7.2]: pliable building membranes in external walls must comply with **AS 4200.1**, be installed per **AS 4200.2**, and sit on the **exterior side of the primary insulation layer**. Any membrane/sarking/insulation layer outside the primary insulation layer must have vapour permeance of not less than **0.143 μg/N.s in CZ4 and 5** and **1.14 μg/N.s in CZ6, 7 and 8** (explanatory: AS 4200.1 Class 3 and 4 vapour control membranes satisfy CZ4–5; Class 4 satisfies CZ6–8). Where no membrane is installed (other than single-skin masonry/concrete), the primary water control layer must be separated from water-sensitive materials by a **drained cavity**.
- **10.8.2 Exhaust systems** [2019: 3.8.7.3]: minimum flow rates **25 L/s (bathroom/sanitary compartment)** and **40 L/s (kitchen/laundry)**; exhaust must discharge directly or via shaft/duct to outdoor air (including venting clothes dryers); intermittent bathroom/sanitary exhausts in unventilated rooms must be interlocked with the light switch with a **10-minute run-on timer**; make-up air via 14,000 mm² openings to an adjacent room (≈ 20 mm undercut on a 700 mm door) or per AS 1668.2.
- **10.8.3 Ventilation of roof spaces** [2019: 3.8.7.4]: **in CZ6, 7 and 8** a roof must have a roof space ≥ **20 mm** high immediately above the primary insulation layer (or above sarking with vapour permeance ≥ 1.14 μg/N.s above that layer, or above ceiling insulation meeting 13.2.3(3)/(4)), ventilated to outdoor air through evenly distributed openings per Table 10.8.3 — roof pitch <10°: 25,000 mm²/m at each of two opposing ends; 10–15°: 25,000 mm²/m at eaves + 5,000 mm²/m at high level; 15–75°: 7,000 mm²/m at eaves + 5,000 mm²/m high level, plus an extra 18,000 mm²/m at eaves for cathedral ceilings — or located immediately under unsarked roof tiles. Exempt: concrete roofs, structural insulated panel roofs, BAL-FZ roofs (AS 3959).
