# COLORBOND Steel Core Colours — Solar Absorptance Values

## Summary

Colour chart page from the COLORBOND steel "Inspired Homes" brochure listing nominal solar absorptance (SA) values for the 22 COLORBOND core colours in Classic finish plus six colours in Matt finish — the SA inputs needed for NatHERS/NCC/BASIX roof colour assessments. SA values are nominal, based on new product, measured in accordance with ASTM E 903-96.

## Key claims

- Solar absorptance (SA) measures how much of the sun's heat a material absorbs; choosing a lower-SA colour is cooler and may help meet building regulations such as NCC or BASIX.
- SA values are nominal, based on new product, measured in accordance with ASTM E 903-96.
- Classic finish SA values (pale tones): Dover White 0.28; Surfmist 0.33; Classic Cream 0.33; Southerly 0.40; Evening Haze 0.43; Paperbark 0.43; Shale Grey 0.44; Dune 0.48.
- Classic finish SA values (mid tones): Bluegum 0.57; Pale Eucalypt 0.60; Windspray 0.60; Gully 0.64; Wallaby 0.64; Basalt 0.67; Jasper 0.67.
- Classic finish SA values (deep tones): Manor Red 0.70; Woodland Grey 0.70; Cottage Green 0.73; Monument 0.73; Ironstone 0.73; Deep Ocean 0.74; Night Sky 0.95.
- Matt finish SA values: Surfmist 0.35; Shale Grey 0.46; Dune 0.48; Bluegum 0.59; Basalt 0.71; Monument 0.79. Matt SA can differ from the same colour in Classic finish (e.g. Monument 0.73 Classic vs 0.79 Matt).
- Selected colours (e.g. Surfmist, Dune, Shale Grey, Windspray, Wallaby, Woodland Grey, Monument) are also available in COLORBOND Ultra steel for coastal and industrial environments.
- Colours apply to roofing, walling, fascias, gutters, downpipes and garage doors.

## Caveats

- Marketing brochure page, undated in the extract; COLORBOND colour ranges and nominal SA values are revised periodically — confirm current values at colorbond.com before using in a certificate or assessment.
- SA values are nominal for new product; weathered or dust-covered roofs will differ.
