# Stormwater Management Plan — 22 King Street, Urangan (Bornhorst & Ward)

## Summary

Site-based stormwater management plan (Rev B, 25 November 2024) by Bornhorst & Ward Pty Ltd for a proposed four-storey, 17-apartment social/affordable residential development at 22 King Street, Urangan (Fraser Coast, Qld), prepared for the Department of Housing, Local Government, Planning and Public Works (QBuild project #94052). It demonstrates compliance with the Fraser Coast Planning Scheme, QUDM 2017 and the Queensland State Planning Policy 2017 via Rational Method peak flow calculations and a proposed ~24m3 underground detention tank, and concludes no stormwater quality treatment is triggered because the site is under 2,500m2. Useful in the knowledge base as a worked example of a Queensland stormwater/SDA engineering report rather than as general guidance.

## Key claims

- Development: material change of use to a four-storey apartment building with 17 apartments and ground floor car parking on a 0.1012 ha site; certified by Nicholas Rozis, RPEQ 7729; Revision A 10/10/2024, Revision B 25/11/2024 (issued for approval).
- Site falls at approx. 1.2% from RL 4.29m AHD (south-east corner) to RL 3.36m AHD (north-east corner); not in a designated Fraser Coast flood hazard area, but within the medium storm-tide inundation hazard overlay with a Defined Storm-Tide Level of 4.09m AHD — which sets the minimum finished surface level (no additional freeboard component required).
- A council Coastal Futures advice note flags a proposed (not yet adopted) revised storm-tide level of 3.97m AHD with depths up to 0.62m — information only, not yet fit for building/development purposes.
- Rational Method (QUDM) inputs: 60-minute 10-year ARI rainfall intensity 71mm/hr (BoM IFD, LAT 25.286S LONG 152.903E); fraction impervious rises from 20% existing to 75% developed; runoff coefficient C10 from 0.65 to 0.83; time of concentration from 13.3 min to 5.0 min.
- Peak 1% AEP (Q100) discharge increases from 0.050 m3/s existing to 0.087 m3/s developed (+0.037 m3/s); 10% AEP increases from 0.029 to 0.049 m3/s.
- Mitigation: an underground detention tank of approximately 24m3 detains roof and driveway runoff before discharge to the existing gully pit across King Street; attenuated peak discharge up to the 1% AEP event is held at no worse than the existing 50L/s.
- Capacity check: the existing 300mm dia. RCP in King Street has ~97L/s capacity at an assumed 1% grade; with the development's 50L/s during the 1% AEP event total flow is 97L/s — "right under full capacity"; ultimate discharge point (Urangan Beach) is unchanged.
- Stormwater quality: under State Planning Policy 2017 Part E, quality requirements only trigger for urban development over 2,500m2 (with >25% impervious or 6+ dwellings, etc.); because this site is under 2,500m2, no stormwater quality treatment is required — though litter baskets will be fitted to all inlets as engineering best practice, and an erosion and sediment control plan will govern the construction phase.
- Major events beyond the 1% AEP storm continue to flow north as overland flow towards 23 Hibiscus St, as per existing conditions; addresses Fraser Coast code 9.4.6 Works, services and infrastructure.

## Caveats

- Project-specific engineering report for one Queensland site (Fraser Coast LGA) — methods and triggers (e.g. the 2,500m2 SPP quality threshold) are Queensland-specific and should not be generalised to Victorian WSUD practice.
- Rev B dated 25/11/2024; flood/storm-tide levels are those adopted by Council at issue date and may change (the report itself flags pending TLPI and Coastal Futures re-assessments).
- Appendix calculation tables (Rational Method, detention sizing) are degraded in extraction; verify figures against the source PDF.
