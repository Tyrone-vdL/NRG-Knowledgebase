# Design For Place — Telopea House Design Options (Hobart)

## Summary

Australian Government Design For Place pack giving the building specifications that take the Telopea reference house (4 bed, 3 bath, 2 living, 182 m² two-storey, north to the side of the block) to 6.9, 7.2 and 7.4 NatHERS stars in the Hobart (cold) climate — the lowest top rating of any city/model in the series, showing the two-storey design works harder to rate in cold climates. Cold-climate levers: waffle pod slab upgraded to R1.8 under-slab insulation, mid-floor insulation lifted from R4.0 to R5.0, and double-glazed low-e aluminium windows swapped for double-glazed low-e uPVC, with Option 2 adding cold-climate window-size tweaks. Companion to the Telopea Hobart Technical Drawings and the Brisbane/Melbourne versions of the same design.

## Key claims

- Star rating options modelled for Hobart: 6.9, 7.2 and 7.4. The 6.9 and 7.2 specs are for the 'Hobart – Option 1' design; 7.4 is 'Hobart – Option 2', which adds cold-climate design tweaks (window sizes).
- On average a 7 star home in Hobart is predicted to use 27% less heating/cooling energy than a 6 star home; an 8 star home 54% less. The Telopea 6.9 requires 24% less energy than a 6 star house; the 7.4 requires 40% less.
- Ground floor: 100 mm concrete slab with 300 mm waffle pod for the lower option; 100 mm concrete slab on ground with R1.8 under-slab insulation for the higher options. Finishes: burnished concrete to living areas, carpet to bedrooms, ceramic tiles to wet areas.
- First floor: timber joists with R4.0 insulation in the floor framing between floors for the lower option; R5.0 insulation between floors for the higher options.
- Ceiling (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R5.0 batt insulation (vs R4.0 for the Brisbane Telopea).
- Roof (all options): metal sheet roofing on timber battens with R1.5 reflective foil laminate.
- External walls (all options): ground floor brick veneer with R2.5 batt insulation and reflective foil membrane (vapour permeable if appropriate), 10 mm plasterboard; first floor lightweight cladding on battens, reflective foil membrane, timber frame, R2.5 batt insulation, 10 mm plasterboard.
- Internal walls (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R2.0 batt insulation.
- Windows: double glazed, low emissivity glass, aluminium frame for the lower option(s); double glazed, low emissivity glass, uPVC frame for the higher option(s). All Hobart options are double glazed with low-e glass.
- Eaves: 600 mm throughout, optimised for adequate summer shading while still allowing winter solar heat gain.
- Draught sealing (all options): weather strips to all windows and sliding doors; seals/weather-strips to hinged external doors; sealed exhaust fans to bathroom, ensuite, powder room and kitchen rangehood.
- Ceiling fans (all options): 1400 mm diameter — one in each bedroom, one in the dining area, one in the front living area.
- Approximately 150 m² of roof space is available for solar panels; living area ceiling heights 2.7 m; includes a double carport.
- uPVC window frames have similar insulating properties to timber frames; burnished concrete thermal mass works particularly well in cool locations but is degraded by coverings such as carpet.

## Caveats

- Published when 6 stars was the stated minimum standard "in most states and territories" — predates the NCC 2022 7-star requirement; treat as design guidance, not current compliance documentation (no publication date printed in the extracted text).
- The specification table uses tick columns per star option; tick marks did not survive extraction — the exact split of floor/window upgrades between the 6.9, 7.2 and 7.4 columns is inferred from row ordering and narrative text and is less certain here than in the other packs. Verify against the source PDF.
- Ratings apply only to this exact design, specification and Hobart location; site-specific assessment required.
