# Specification 44 vs NatHERS — When to Use Which Pathway

## Summary

A short one-page comparison guide explaining the difference between the NatHERS star-rating pathway and the Specification 44 load-limit pathway for demonstrating residential thermal performance compliance under NCC 2022, and listing the reasons to choose one over the other. It positions Specification 44 as a Performance Solution fallback for non-standard, innovative or hard-to-rate designs, and NatHERS as the faster, more familiar route for typical homes. For the mechanics of the Spec 44 pathway itself, see [[src-ncc-2022-first-principles-performance-solution-using-spec-44]].

## Key claims

- NatHERS (Nationwide House Energy Rating Scheme) assesses residential thermal performance with a star rating out of 10; under NCC 2022 new homes must typically achieve a 7-star minimum. Ratings are produced in accredited software based on climate zone, materials and design.
- Specification 44 is an NCC 2022 pathway setting explicit heating and cooling load limits (in MJ/m²/year) used as a Performance Solution — described as a more precise, outcome-driven alternative to NatHERS star ratings.
- NatHERS is characterised as the simplified, star-based Deemed-to-Satisfy pathway; Specification 44 verifies that the building's actual heating and cooling loads sit within specified limits.
- Reasons given to choose Specification 44 over NatHERS: design flexibility for non-standard/innovative designs; clear heating/cooling load performance targets; fallback when a building can't achieve the 7-star NatHERS rating; better alignment with Net Zero goals and carbon tracking; supports new construction methods and materials not well handled by NatHERS tools; may reduce certification time and cost for qualified projects.
- When NOT to use Specification 44: the pathway is more complex and requires detailed modelling and documentation — for typical homes, NatHERS is described as faster, more cost-effective, and more familiar to building certifiers.
