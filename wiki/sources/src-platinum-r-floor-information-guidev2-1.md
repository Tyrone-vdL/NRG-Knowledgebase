# Platinum "R" Floor Product Information & Design Guide v2.1

## Summary

Product Information & Design Guide (Version 2.1) for Platinum "R" Floor, Platinum Timber & Ply's insulated structural flooring panel — 17 mm T&G structural ply top skin and 7 mm structural ply bottom skin over a foam core — giving panel R-values of R1.2 (54 mm) to R2.5 (99 mm). This is the detailed design document: structural test results, joist requirements, wet-area set-downs, termite treatment, storage/handling and chain-of-custody.

## Key claims

- Panel R-values and weights: 54 mm panel (30 mm core + 24 mm ply) = R1.2, 40.9 kg; 64 mm (40 mm core) = R1.5, 41.9 kg; 84 mm (60 mm core) = R2.0, 43.9 kg; 99 mm (75 mm core) = R2.5, 45.3 kg. Average weight quoted as 14 kg/m² (thickness dependent).
- Construction: top skin Australian 17 mm T&G structural plywood; bottom skin Australian 7 mm structural plywood; core Australian-manufactured foam.
- Standard sizes: 2400×1200×54 mm, 2400×1200×64 mm, 2400×1180×84 mm, 2400×1180×99 mm.
- Structural performance tested by Bureau Veritas (NATA-registered laboratory); results exceeded both point load and distributed load requirements of AS/NZS 1170 (cited in the document as "AS/NZS 11701-2002") for commercial and domestic usage; independent testing showed up to four times stronger than the Australian Standard requirements.
- Recommended joist centres: 480 mm (not 450), 600 mm or 800 mm; minimum joist thickness 45 mm; suits standard steel or timber joist systems; blocking required under all load-bearing walls.
- Provides an inherent thermal break between steel joists and flooring without additional material.
- Wet areas: eliminates joist set-down/notching — a strip of fibre-cement flashing between the panel edge and the 19 mm wet area flooring gives an automatic step-down and fall to waste.
- Termite treatment: available untreated or H2 Treated; the H2 option usable throughout Australia with no geographical restriction.
- Sustainability: plantation radiata pine, Responsible Wood Chain of Custody certified (AS 4708, PEFC-recognised), 100% recyclable at end of use, Australian Made certified (AMCL).
- Storage/handling: keep dry, protect edges and corners, lay flat on a smooth level surface clear of the ground; face checks in weather-exposed ply faces are superficial and do not affect structural integrity (per CHH Ecoply guidance).

## Caveats

- Version 2.1, undated; R-values are manufacturer panel values with no AS/NZS 4859.1 test certificate cited in this guide.
- The structural standard is cited as "AS/NZS 11701-2002" — almost certainly a typo for AS/NZS 1170.1:2002; verify before quoting.
