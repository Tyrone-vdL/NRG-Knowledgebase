# ABCB Information Handbook — Energy Efficiency Provisions for BCA 2010 Volume Two

## Summary

ABCB/DEWHA information handbook (2010 edition) explaining the residential energy efficiency provisions of BCA 2010 Volume Two — the 6-star era predecessor to today's NCC Part H6 — covering Performance Requirements P2.6.1/P2.6.2, Verification Methods V2.6.2.2/V2.6.3, and the Part 3.12 Deemed-to-Satisfy provisions (fabric, glazing, sealing, air movement, services). Twelve chapters span the history of BCA energy efficiency, the performance-based code structure, clause-by-clause DtS analysis, terminology, and state/territory administration including application to alterations and additions. Useful as historical context for how the elemental and star-rating pathways evolved, not as current regulation.

## Key claims

- Stringency history: energy efficiency provisions entered BCA Volume Two on 1 January 2003; Volume One multi-residential (Class 2/3/4 parts) on 1 May 2005; all building classes by BCA 2006 (housing raised to 5-star equivalent); COAG agreed in April 2009 to increase housing to 6-star or equivalent, delivered in BCA 2010.
- Energy used in buildings accounted for almost 27% of all energy-related GHG emissions; residential energy consumption was projected to rise 56% over 1990 levels by 2020.
- Part 3.12.0 provides two DtS pathways: a star rating approach using NatHERS-accredited software (per the ABCB Protocol for House Energy Rating Software, recognising 67–69 distinct climate zones) and a more prescriptive elemental approach based on 8 climate zones. The star rating path is described as the flexible/innovative path; the elemental path as "inflexible and demanding", retained as a transition.
- Outdoor living area credit (BCA 2010, climate zones 1 and 2 only): 0.5-star credit each for (1) an outdoor living area fully covered by an impervious roof with Total R-Value at least 1.5 (downward heat flow) and (2) at least one permanently installed ceiling fan complying with 3.12.4.3 — 1 full star if both are met.
- Verification Method V2.6.2.2 (reference building) sets an annual load target by modelling a DtS-compliant reference building: cooling-only target in climate zones 1–2, heating-only in zones 7–8, separate heating and cooling targets in zones 3–6; requires two modelling runs. It can satisfy Parts 3.12.1–3.12.4 but NOT Part 3.12.5 (services), which must comply separately.
- Verification Method V2.6.3 applies only to a heater in a hot water supply system: maximum greenhouse gas pollution intensity of 100 g CO2-e/MJ, with thermal load determined per AS/NZS 4234.
- Roofs (3.12.1.2): minimum Total R-Values per Table 3.12.1.1a vary by climate zone, altitude, heat-flow direction and roof solar absorptance (lighter roofs get reduced requirements); a pitched roof with flat ceiling must have at least 50% of the insulation laid on the ceiling; an R0.5 reduction is allowed in climate zones 1–5 if all insulation is at ceiling level and the roof space is ventilated (passively or with wind-driven ventilators).
- Wall provisions distinguish external walls by surface density above/below 220 kg/m²; thermal bridging through metal elements can reduce insulation effectiveness by half, hence thermal break requirements.
- Roof lights pitched 0–70° from horizontal are treated under fabric provisions (3.12.1); steeper elements are treated as windows under glazing (3.12.2).
- Artificial lighting (3.12.5.5) used a lamp power density allowance of 5 W/m² for housing; swimming pool/spa heating and pumping provisions (3.12.5.7) applied to pools/spas of 680 L capacity or more.
- "Envelope" encloses spaces that are conditioned or likely to be conditioned (habitable rooms), and can include internal elements bounding non-conditioned spaces; "conditioned space" exempts non-habitable rooms with a heater not more than 1.2 kW.
- The Performance Requirements (P2.6.1 building, P2.6.2 domestic services) contain the phrase "to the degree necessary", which gives Building Control Authorities flexibility, particularly for Alternative Solutions on existing buildings.
- Alterations/additions guidance (Chapter 12): the BCA applies to new work only (state legislation governs extent); replacement of cladding/lining usually justifies adding insulation, but repair-only work may not; new glazing in an extension may be assessed as if applied uniformly to the whole storey while only being installed in the extension; sealing an existing room is "all-or-nothing".
- The BCA is amended every twelve months and is not applied retrospectively to existing buildings not undergoing change.
- The handbook covers national BCA content only — it explicitly excludes State and Territory variations and additions.

## Caveats

- Historic document (2010 edition, BCA 2010): the 6-star/Part 3.12/P2.6.1 framework described here was superseded — NCC 2019 restructured references and NCC 2022 replaced them with Part H6, the ABCB Housing Provisions Section 13, 7-star NatHERS minimum and Whole-of-Home (H6P2). Use only for historical context, never for current compliance values.
- National BCA only — contains no State or Territory variations, which materially alter application (e.g. Queensland).
- Large source (~270K characters); this page summarises the structure and key provisions only — chapter-level detail (e.g. full glazing worked examples in Chapter 7, sealing clause analysis in Chapter 8) is not exhaustively captured.
