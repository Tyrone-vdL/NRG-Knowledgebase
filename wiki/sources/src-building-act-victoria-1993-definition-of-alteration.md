# Building Act 1993 (Vic) — Definition of "Construct" (Alterations Extract)

## Summary

A single-page extract from Part 9 (Liability) of the Building Act 1993 (Victoria), No. 126 of 1993, capturing the statutory definition of "construct" in relation to a building — the definition that brings alterations within the scope of building work in Victoria. Useful when determining whether renovation/alteration work to an existing dwelling triggers NCC energy efficiency obligations in Victoria.

## Key claims

- "Construct" in relation to a building means: (a) build, rebuild, erect or re-erect the building; or (b) **make alterations to the building**; or (c) enlarge or extend the building; or (d) cause any other person to do anything in (a), (b) or (c); or (e) manage or arrange the doing of anything in (a), (b) or (c).
- Alterations are therefore captured within "construct" at the Act level — making alterations is legally construction work in Victoria.
- The extract also includes part of the definition of "prescribed period" for liability purposes: for a contract for sale of a building with domestic building work, 6 years and 6 months (or a longer prescribed period not exceeding 10 years) after the completion date for the construction of the building.
- The page references the completion-date trigger where no occupancy permit is issued: the date of issue under Part 4 of the certificate of final inspection of the building work.
- Section/amendment margin notes show the definitions have been amended over time (e.g. No. 52/1998, No. 11/2023, Nos 2/1996, 75/1998, 36/2008).

## Caveats

- This is a one-page extract from Part 9 (Liability) of the Act, not the full Act — the operative definition of "alteration" for building permit/NCC application purposes also interacts with the Building Regulations 2018 (Vic), which are not in this extract.
- The Act is amended frequently (margin notes show amendments up to No. 11/2023); verify currency on the Victorian legislation register before citing.
- Jurisdiction: Victoria only. Other states define alterations differently (see ACT and WA sources).
