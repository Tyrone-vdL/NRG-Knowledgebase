# Hardie Secura Flooring Brochure (2025)

## Summary

Two-page James Hardie brochure (© 2025) for Hardie Secura Flooring, an Australian-made lightweight fibre cement structural flooring board for timber or steel floor joists, marketed as able to contribute to a 7-star NatHERS rating without modifying the upper-storey flooring structure. Covers product specifications (19 mm and 22 mm boards), double-sided functionality (tile side / carpet-vinyl side), moisture protection and warranty.

## Key claims

- Hardie Secura Flooring is a durable, lightweight fibre cement structural flooring installed over timber or steel floor joists, suitable for indoor and outdoor structural flooring.
- It "can contribute to 7 stars without the need to modify the flooring structure on upper storeys" — the only thermal performance claim made; no R-value or density figures are given in this brochure.
- Double-sided functionality: one face designed for direct tile application, the other for use under carpet or vinyl; tiling is direct, without underlay (unlike plywood or particleboard), avoiding height differences between floor finishes.
- Dimensionally stable — resistant to expansion and contraction from moisture or thermal variations, making it suitable as a wet-area tiling substrate.
- Surface sealer reduces moisture penetration (water beads rather than penetrates); can be exposed to the elements for up to 3 months without warping or twisting.
- Tongue-and-groove jointed; the low-density formulation allows gun nailing without pre-drilling.
- Product specifications: 19 mm — 1800 × 900 mm (code 405591, green corner, 41.0 kg/board) and 2700 × 600 mm (code 405592, green corner, 41.0 kg/board); 22 mm — 2400 × 600 mm (code 405593, grey corner, 42.2 kg/board).
- Australian made with a 25-year warranty.
- Marketing claim: more builders said they would use Hardie fibre cement products for wet-area structural flooring in their own homes than other materials (survey of 371 builders, April 2024, commissioned by James Hardie).
