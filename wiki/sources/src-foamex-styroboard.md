# Foamex Styroboard EPS Technical Data

## Summary

Technical data sheet for Foamex Styroboard, Foamex Group's flame-retardant-modified expanded polystyrene (EPS) block/sheet product, setting out the AS 1366.3-1992 class properties (L, SL, S, M, H, VH), a thermal conductivity design table by mean temperature (0–60°C) for each class, and material characteristics including moisture resistance, fire behaviour, CFC/VOC status and the manufacturing process. This is the underlying material data for Foamex-based insulation products (e.g. EXPOL underfloor panels, waffle pods).

## Key claims

- Foamex Styroboard is closed-cell EPS modified with flame retardant additives; CFC-free, zero ozone-depleting substances in content and manufacture; zero VOC content (<0.8 g/l used in manufacture — pentane blowing agent is lost during manufacture and curing).
- AS 1366 Part 3 – 1992 class minimums (Table 1): compressive stress at 10% deformation (min): L 50, SL 70, S 85, M 105, H 135, VH 165 kPa (AS 2498.3); cross-breaking strength (min): L 95, SL 135, S 165, M 200, H 260, VH 320 kPa (AS 2498.4).
- Water vapour transmission (max, parallel to rise at 23°C): L 710, SL 630, S 580, M 520, H 460, VH 400 µg/m²s (AS 2498.5); dimensional stability of length max 1.0% (70°C dry, 7 days) all classes.
- Minimum thermal resistance at 25°C mean temperature (50 mm sample, AS 2464.5/2464.6): values per class up to 1.28 m².K/W for Class VH (the class-by-class row is degraded in extraction — upper classes read 1.13 / 1.17 / 1.20 / 1.25 / 1.28 m².K/W).
- The AS 1366.3 thermal resistance is a minimum conformance value achieved in 97.5% of cases statistically; for design purposes Foamex directs users to the average thermal conductivity design table (Table 3).
- Thermal conductivity design values (Table 3, W/mK) by mean temperature: at 25°C — Class L 0.0430, SL 0.0410, S ~0.0397, M ~0.0383, H ~0.0368, VH ~0.0352; e.g. Class SL is 0.0407 W/mK at 23°C. K value decreases at lower mean temperatures (e.g. SL ~0.0370 at 0°C), improving sub-zero performance.
- Moisture: even force-saturated to a moisture content ten times its dry weight, EPS maintains 80% of its R-value.
- Density range 11–32 kg/m³ (vs water 1000 kg/m³ — buoyancy = 1000 minus density per m³).
- Fire: EPS is combustible and must not be exposed to open flames; flame retardant inhibits ignition from small sources. AS 1530.3 early fire hazard indices — EPS: ignitability 12, spread of flame 0, heat evolved 3, smoke developed 5 (compared: Australian softboard 16/9/7/3, Oregon 13/6/5/3, bluegum 11/0/3/2). Flame propagation per AS 2122.1: median flame duration max 2.0 s all classes.
- Combustion gas toxicity reported as no greater than burning timber (CSIRO-cited research).
- Long-term stability: core specimens from freezer walls in service 20 years showed no deterioration in structural integrity or physical properties.

## Caveats

- Undated document referencing AS 1366.3-1992 and AS/NZS-era test methods; AS/NZS 4859.1 (current insulation labelling standard) is not referenced, so R-value claims here are material-property data, not labelled product R-values.
- Table 1's thermal resistance row and Table 3's column alignment are degraded in extraction (one Table 3 entry is an obvious typo, ".0149"); verify class-specific figures against the source PDF before quoting.
