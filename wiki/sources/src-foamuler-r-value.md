# Foamular XPS R-Values by Thickness and Mean Temperature

## Summary

Short technical note giving the R-values of Owens Corning Foamular extruded polystyrene (XPS) board at standard thicknesses (R0.89 at 25 mm up to R3.57 at 100 mm) plus a manufacturer's letter tabulating R-value per 25 mm and thermal conductivity across mean temperatures from +30°C to −30°C. Distributed in Australia via Austech (foamular.com.au).

## Key claims

- R-values by thickness: 25 mm R0.89; 30 mm R1.07; 40 mm R1.42; 50 mm R1.78; 50 mm with foil R2.0; 60 mm R2.14; 75 mm R2.67; 100 mm R3.57.
- R-value is cumulative when boards are stacked — e.g. 40 mm (R1.42) + 50 mm (R1.78) gives R3.2, so 100 mm is not needed to achieve R3.
- Owens Corning letter (based on actual test data), R-value for 25 mm thickness and thermal conductivity k by mean temperature: 30°C R0.87 (k 0.0288 W/m.K); 25°C R0.89 (0.0281); 20°C R0.91 (0.0275); 15°C R0.93 (0.0269); 10°C R0.95 (0.0263); 5°C R0.97 (0.0258); 0°C R0.99 (0.0253); −5°C R1.01 (0.0248); −10°C R1.03 (0.0243); −20°C R1.07 (0.0234); −30°C R1.11 (0.0225). R-values in m²·°C/W.
- Performance improves at lower mean temperatures (conductivity falls from 0.0288 to 0.0225 W/m.K across the +30°C to −30°C range).
- Letter issued by Owens Corning Hong Kong Ltd (Building Material Asia Pacific sales manager covering Oceania); Australian technical contact is Austech (sales@austech.com.au, foamular.com.au).

## Caveats

- Undated document; no reference to AS/NZS 4859.1 test/labelling conditions — figures are manufacturer data, with the temperature table stated only as "based on actual test data".
- The 25 mm R-values imply k ≈ 0.028 W/m.K, consistent through both tables, but the standard Australian rating condition (23°C mean) is not explicitly stated; interpolate between the 20°C and 25°C rows if needed.
