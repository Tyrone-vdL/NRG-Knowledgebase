# NatHERS Zoning Examples — Guide for Technical Note 1.2 (2014) [SUPERSEDED]

## Summary

An 11-page visual companion to NatHERS Technical Note 1 version 1.2 (2014), for software using Chenath engine V3.13, showing worked zoning examples on NatHERS Benchmark Study House floor plans — colour-coding rooms into the Table 2 zone types (Living, Kitchen/Living, Day-time, Bedroom, Night-time, Unconditioned, Garage). It illustrates the small-air-spaces rule, staircase zoning (open stringer/balustrade stairs join the Living zone; walled stairwells become separate zones), the at-least-one-unconditioned-zone rule, hallway/corridor zoning as Day-time, and apartment walls shared with fully glazed corridors.

## Key claims

- Demonstrates Technical Note 1.2 (2014) Table 2 "Software zone types — conditioning of spaces" applied to single-storey, double-storey and apartment versions of the NatHERS Benchmark Study House.
- Zone assignments shown: bedrooms → Bedroom; living → Living; kitchen/meals/family → Kitchen/Living; entry, pantry, linen, store, study, games → Day-time; bathroom, laundry, WC → Unconditioned; WIR and ensuite → Night-time; garage → Garage.
- Small air spaces (clause 7.6.1): small pantries (ones that cannot be walked into), built-in robes, plumbing voids, wall voids, return air ducts and other small non-habitable areas are included in the zone they are attached to or located in.
- Under-stair spaces (clause 7.6.2): storage areas, powder rooms and WCs located under a staircase — where for all practicable purposes the stairs form the ceiling — are included in the same zone as the staircase.
- Staircase zoning examples: a stairwell with exposed cut stringers and an open balustrade has no permanent boundary wall and is included in the Living zone; a stairwell with a wall defining a permanent boundary becomes a separate zone.
- Zone definition (clause 7.2): a zone is a space or group of spaces expected to be at a uniform temperature, defined by permanent boundaries — a living area cannot be artificially split into two zones by an internal wall (with or without openings).
- Unconditioned zone rule (clause 7.4.2): every dwelling must contain at least one unconditioned zone excluding the garage (normally a bathroom, laundry or WC); if none has an external window or door, the smallest must still be selected as unconditioned.
- Hallways and corridors (clause 7.4) are circulation areas modelled as Day-time zones; Class 2 common-area corridors follow Table 4 — apartment walls shared with corridors with fully glazed external walls model the external corridor as an unconditioned zone, with the corridor wall matched to the apartment wall length and neighbour walls at either end.
- Includes a double-glazed wall apartment example (double glazed wall with air gap) illustrating corridor adjacency zoning for Class 2.

## Caveats

- SUPERSEDED: companion to Technical Note 1.2 (2014), which has been replaced by the June 2019 and September 2022 Technical Notes and now the NatHERS Technical Note October 2024 (being ingested separately in batch n13). Zone names and rules have since evolved (e.g. the 2022+ notes added Glazed Common Area and Shared Basement Carpark zone types and changed bathroom/WC rules).
- Text extraction is severely degraded — the PDF uses a non-standard character encoding, so most labels surfaced as garbled glyphs; the floor-plan colour coding (the document's core content) is graphical and not captured. Verify any specific example against the source PDF.
- Marked copyright "reproduction in part or whole is strictly forbidden" — an annotated third-party training aid rather than an official NatHERS publication; treat as instructional, not authoritative.
