# TPA Support Ruling — Outdoor Living Areas Are Unconditioned and Treated as Shading in NatHERS

## Summary

A Design Matters National TPA (Thermal Performance Assessor) Support Desk email response to NRG ruling on how to zone outdoor living areas in NatHERS models: they fall under the NatHERS Practice Note Appendix 1 Zoning Table description "Outdoor Living Areas" and are "unconditioned" — treated as shading to the conditioned zones — even if capable of being enclosed, unless there is clear documentation of heating/cooling equipment being installed. The screenshot is the regulator/peak-body response only; the original question is collapsed behind "Show History".

## Key claims

- Question (implied from the response; original email not visible): how should an outdoor living area — including one capable of being enclosed — be zoned in a NatHERS assessment.
- Ruling: "Current NatHERS Practice Note covers designs like this in Appendix 1 Zoning Table under the description of 'Outdoor Living Areas'."
- Ruling: "Outdoor areas, even those that are capable of being enclosed are 'unconditioned' and treated as shading to the conditioned zones unless there is clear documentation of [mech.] heating/cooling equipment being installed." (line wrap obscures one word — see Caveats).
- The decisive exception is documentary: only "clear documentation" of heating/cooling equipment being installed changes the treatment from unconditioned/shading.
- Response issued by the Design Matters National TPA Support Desk ("Thank you for contacting the Design Matters National TPA Support Desk."), sent to NRG on Monday 20 Jul at 2:04 PM (year not shown in the screenshot).
- Issuing body identified in the footer as Building Designers Association of Victoria Inc. T/as Design Matters National, ABN 36 982 485 577, Registration no. A0008498.
