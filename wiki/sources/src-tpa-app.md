# HERA Thermal Performance Assessment Project Information Form (TPA-APP)

## Summary

A blank client-intake form (© House Energy Raters Association 2021) used to collect the project and specification details needed for a NatHERS thermal performance assessment when the project drawings lack the required information; the completed specification forms part of the certified assessment. It captures site/plan identifiers, construction details, insulation preferences, service connections, appliances, on-site generation, pools/spas, and a room-by-room schedule of downlights, exhaust fans, ceiling fans and floor coverings.

## Key claims

- The form states explicitly: "This specification forms part of the certified assessment, it is to be specified by the client when the project drawings lack the required information."
- Fields collected: lot/DP/SP numbers, address, council area, site area; external/internal wall construction, floor construction, frame, roof material; roof, external wall, internal wall and floor covering colours; glazing type, window/door details, window manufacturer, external blinds.
- Insulation preferences captured with R-values where known: floor, external wall, internal wall, ceiling, roof insulation and external wall foil.
- Service connections ticked: town water, bore water, rainwater tank (volume in kilolitres), grey water tank, reticulated alternative water supply, natural gas, LPG, solar PV, grid connection.
- Appliance details: water heating (type, capacity, fuel, STC), heating/cooling split by living and bedroom areas, cooking (wall oven, cooktop, upright stove; electricity/gas/induction), hot water recirculation/diversion, pool and spa (location, covered, shaded, heated).
- Room-by-room schedule for downlights (sealed, size, qty), exhaust fans (sealed, size, qty), ceiling fans (size, qty) and floor covering type across living, kitchen, dining, study, rumpus, WC, laundry, bedrooms, theatre, bathroom, ensuite, powder room and other rooms.
- NSW-specific warning on the form: NatHERS assessments are required at DA stage where lighting plans often don't exist yet — but if downlights and exhaust fans are added at construction and are not listed in the Universal Certificate, "the certificate is NOT VALID and a new certificate will be required" (at the certifier's discretion).

## Concepts touched

NatHERS, thermal performance assessment, ceiling penetrations, downlights, insulation, glazing, Whole-of-Home inputs, BASIX/NSW DA process, assessor documentation, HERA

## Caveats

- A blank template form, not a regulatory or technical authority — © House Energy Raters Association 2021. NRG may use or adapt it as a client data-capture checklist.
- Predates the NCC 2022 Whole-of-Home requirements; appliance fields exist but the form is not structured around the H6P2/WoH rating inputs.
