# EXPOL UnderFloor Insulation — R1.4 Rigid Polystyrene Panels

## Summary

DIY-oriented product brochure for EXPOL UnderFloor Insulation, a rigid expanded polystyrene panel (R-value 1.4) fitted snugly between timber floor joists of new or existing suspended floors, manufactured in Australia by the Foamex Group. Covers the four standard panel widths, the concertina-cut friction-fit installation method, required clearances around services, and compliance/appraisal credentials (BRANZ Appraisal No. 256, AS/NZS 4859.1:2002, AS 1366.3-1992 SL grade).

## Key claims

- R-value: 1.4 (stated as "1.4 R VALUE" for the solid underfloor insulation panel; no test conditions given in the brochure).
- Panels are rigid polystyrene, 1200 mm long × 60 mm thick, in four standard widths to suit common joist spacings: 360 mm (12/bag, covers 5.18 m²), 410 mm (11/bag, 5.41 m²), 470 mm (10/bag, 5.64 m²), 560 mm (9/bag, 6.05 m²).
- Panels are concertina-cut on both sides allowing up to 20 mm compression for a snug friction fit between joists; if too wide, slice down a concertina edge leaving ~5 mm oversize.
- Backed by a BRANZ-appraised 50-year limited product warranty (Appraisal No. 256); performance claimed not affected by ageing or moisture; will not sag.
- Complies with AS/NZS 4859.1:2002 (insulation standard); panels carry a yellow stripe confirming manufacture to AS 1366 Part 3 1992 SL grade rigid cellular polystyrene; contains flame retardant additives.
- Installation clearances: minimum 150 mm gap around metal heating flues, OA-rated recessed downlights and ceiling exhaust fans; 50 mm around brick/concrete chimneys; 100 mm around pipes/plumbing for future maintenance.
- Panel must be pushed hard against the underside of the floor leaving no air gap; secured with two non-corrosive nylon fixings (stainless nail) per panel at opposite corners.
- Electrical cables in direct contact must be separated using EXPOL Wireguard (waxed paper strip wrapped around PVC cables); do not staple.
- Suitable for retrofit under existing floors (e.g. polished-floor bungalows/villas) and new floors (pole houses, exposed timber floors); non-allergenic, recyclable.
- Manufactured by Foamex Group Ltd (Bayswater North VIC) / Foamex Polystyrene Pty Ltd (Revesby NSW).

## Caveats

- Undated brochure (ruler artwork dated 2011); references AS/NZS 4859.1:2002, since superseded by AS/NZS 4859.1:2018.
- The R1.4 figure is given without stated test conditions (material vs system, heat flow direction) — verify against the BRANZ appraisal before use in NatHERS inputs.
- BRANZ is a New Zealand appraisal body; the brochure is the Australian edition but the appraisal originates from the NZ product line.
