# BASIX Thermal Performance Protocol (June 2024)

## Summary

The NSW Department of Planning, Housing and Infrastructure's BASIX Thermal Performance Protocol (dated 07 June 2024) is the PRIMARY rulebook for conducting Simulations under the Thermal Performance Index of BASIX — it governs assessor/organisation accreditation, approved software, and the exact modelling procedures Accredited Assessors must follow for new NSW dwellings. BASIX (Building Sustainability Index) is the NSW web-based planning tool introduced 1 July 2004 under the Environmental Planning and Assessment Act 1979; its Thermal Performance Index assesses heating and cooling loads from the building fabric only (appliances, except ceiling fans, are assessed in the Energy Index). The Protocol's procedures largely defer to the current NatHERS Technical Note, with BASIX-specific overrides for zoning of conditioned/unconditioned areas, ground reflectance, window coverings, and documentation.

## Key claims

- Three pathways exist for the BASIX Thermal Performance Index: (1) the DIY method (completed within the BASIX tool, specifying minimum insulation with flexible glazing assessment), (2) the Simulation method by an Accredited Assessor using Approved Software (this Protocol governs it), and (3) the Passive House standard method using PHPP software by a Certified Passive House Designer.
- The Thermal Performance Index assesses heating and cooling loads from the dwelling fabric only. Mechanical heating/cooling appliances (air conditioners, electric resistance heating) CANNOT be considered in Simulations; ceiling fans CAN. Trombe walls and similar fabric-integrated systems may be considered via the Alternative Assessment process (section 5.2).
- Approved Software = NatHERS-accredited software using Chenath engine version 3.22 or later, approved for the Thermal Performance Index available from 1 October 2023. The Whole-of-Home / appliances component of the software (Chenath 3.22) cannot be used for BASIX Thermal Performance Simulations or to satisfy BASIX energy standards. Dwellings eligible for the BASIX Thermal Comfort Index before 1 October 2023 must use Chenath 3.21 with the 27/11/2020 BASIX Thermal Comfort Protocol and relevant NatHERS Technical Note.
- Accrediting Organisations as at June 2024: Australian Building Sustainability Association (ABSA), Design Matters National (DMN) and the House Energy Raters Association (HERA). Only organisations already accredited by NatHERS as Assessor Accrediting Organisations (AAOs) can be approved; the department will not directly accredit individual assessors.
- Software operation hierarchy (section 4.3): this Protocol's Assessment Procedures prevail over (b) the current NatHERS Technical Note, (c) software user manual/help files, (d) training material, and (e) AAO procedures, in the event of inconsistency.
- Software must not be used to demonstrate BASIX compliance for only part of a dwelling, or for alterations/additions to existing dwellings, without written department approval (section 4.4).
- BASIX conditioned floor area excludes: floor area not fully enclosed; bathrooms (but NOT ensuites) and laundries with a ventilation opening; voids, store rooms, garages and carparks. BASIX unconditioned floor area = bathrooms (not ensuites) and laundries with a ventilation opening. Ensuites must be included in a conditioned zone regardless of ventilation opening. Bathrooms/toilets/laundries WITHOUT a ventilation opening go in a conditioned zone (mechanical ventilation draws conditioned air). Outdoor living spaces (balconies, rooms with mesh/open screens) are excluded from both zone types. Note these BASIX definitions may differ from NatHERS Technical Note zone definitions.
- Fixed modelling defaults: ground reflectance 0.2 regardless of surrounds; all windows modelled with low-performance Holland blinds (no other internal coverings, regardless of what is nominated); insect screens modelled on all windows and glazed sliding doors regardless of plans; external wall colour 'medium' if unspecified; internal wall colour and window/door frame colour 'medium' if specifiable and unspecified; external shading devices only modelled if shown on plans AND of exterior-grade construction materials.
- Items deferred to the NatHERS Technical Note: postcode/climate zone selection, individual ratings for each dwelling in multi-dwelling developments, waffle pods, roof colours, loss of ceiling insulation from penetrations, thermal bridging and thermal breaks, openable proportion of windows, zoning types (including garages), walls/floors/ceilings shared with common areas, adjacent buildings, and trees/vegetation shading. Building sealing follows the NCC NSW Additions.
- Insulation must be nominated as either bulk insulation with material R-value (insulation only) stated on the Assessor Certificate and drawings, or foil insulation with total system R-value (in both directions of heat flow for ceilings/roofs) or a clear product description including foil surface emissivities and air gaps.
- Glazing documentation must include NFRC Solar Heat Gain Coefficient (SHGC) and U-value of the complete glazing unit (glass and frame combined) regardless of whether glass is single clear. The unit's U-value must not exceed the maximum on the Assessor Certificate; SHGC must be within the certificate's specified range.
- Where required information (Table 1) is missing from drawings/specifications and not received on written request, Protocol default values apply; if no default exists, worst-case values specific to the building's location apply — multiple simulations may be needed to identify worst-case defaults.
- Documentation rules: only one Assessor Certificate per DA or CDC (staged developments may have one certificate per stage matching the BASIX certificate); the Assessor Certificate is the NatHERS Certificate from the online generation system showing total heating and cooling loads and unique QR code; for Class 1a buildings on a single lot, a summary sheet from the same system must accompany the BASIX certificate; certificate information (except window frame material) must be consistent with drawings and DA/CDC details.
- Maximum heating/cooling loads are specified in Schedule 1 Part 2 of the State Environmental Planning Policy (Sustainable Buildings) 2022 — covering single dwelling maximums, maximum average loads in the BASIX multi-dwelling tool, and maximum individual loads per dwelling in multi-dwelling developments.
- Appendix notes: NatHERS climate zones 19 (Charleville), 50 (Oakey) and 66 (Ballarat) are selectable in BASIX Simulations for postcodes near the NSW border; their maximum loads equal zones 9 (Amberley), 46 (Cobar) and 24 (Canberra) respectively. There is no floor-type delineation for units. Mud brick values may be used for any dwelling with at least 50% mud brick or rammed earth walls, regardless of floor type.
- Revisions to existing simulated ratings should use the original software version (with the Protocol and NatHERS Technical Note versions applicable to that version); assessors do not need a written departmental request to access an older software version. BASIX help desk: 1300 650 908.

## Caveats

- NSW-specific instrument: applies only to the Simulation method of the BASIX Thermal Performance Index for new dwellings in NSW. Version dated 07 June 2024; supersedes the BASIX Thermal Comfort Protocol of 27/11/2020 (which still applies to pre-1-October-2023 eligible dwellings using Chenath 3.21).
- The Appendix's actual Maximum Loads table did not survive text extraction — only the explanatory notes are present. Verify specific load caps against the source PDF or Schedule 1 Part 2 of the SEPP (Sustainable Buildings).
- The Protocol repeatedly defers to "the current NatHERS Technical Note" — for current work that means the NatHERS Technical Note October 2024, not the superseded 2014/2019/2022 versions.
