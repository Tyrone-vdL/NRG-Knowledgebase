# XCEM ALPHAFLOOR Solid Concrete Floor Panel — Design Guide 2025

## Summary

XCEM's 2025 design guide for ALPHAFLOOR, a 35 mm thick, 25 MPa solid precast concrete floor panel (35 kg/m², 570 mm wide, 2700 mm long) for timber or steel joisted floors, marketed as a particle-board/AAC/fibre-cement substitute that improves NatHERS/BASIX star ratings, delivers Rw 37 acoustic performance, and is crystalline-silica free. Covers product comparison versus 75 mm AAC, particle board and FC sheet, technical specifications, fixing/cutting details, construction notes (control joints, bearing, waterproofing) and WHS handling guidance. The guide claims thermal benefit qualitatively but publishes no R-value.

## Key claims

- ALPHAFLOOR is a **35 mm solid concrete panel, 25 MPa compressive strength, 35 kg/m² dry weight**, glass-fibre reinforced, made by XCEM in the NSW Southern Highlands (ISO 9001 accredited, Australian made).
- Acoustic: **Rw 37** — claimed "highest performing acoustic product available" for this application; comparison table lists 75 mm AAC panel Rw 33, 19/22 mm particle board Rw 26, 19/22 mm FC sheet Rw 34.
- Comparison table: 75 mm AAC = 3 MPa, 52 kg/m², contains crystalline silica, requires water protection; particle board = 16 kg/m², requires water protection; FC sheet = 35–43 kg/m², contains crystalline silica. ALPHAFLOOR contains **no measurable crystalline silica** (assessed by HIBBS & Associates, report S11606-L1) and needs no protection from water/rain/traffic.
- Energy claim (qualitative): "Significant improvement to NatHERS/BASIX Star Rating" — cost saving available when substituting ALPHAFLOOR for particle board on the first floor, "driven by the high thermal performance of the product and the 7 star NatHERS/BASIX requirements". **No R-value is published in this guide.**
- Panel geometry: width 570 mm; length 2700 mm; 14 panels per pack; tolerances ±4 mm panel ends, +2.5 mm thickness; one face rebated (installed face down); tongue-and-groove edges; minimum trimmed panel width 200 mm.
- Framing: timber joists min. 40 mm wide; steel joists min. 50 mm wide; panel ends require min. 20 mm bearing on a joist; joists level and flat; load capacity values determined to AS/NZS 1170.0 Appendix B by a NATA-accredited laboratory (accreditation no. 1393).
- Fixing: specified screw types for timber (e.g. Bremick 10x65 mm Shark Tooth decking screw, Simpson Strong-Tie SSDHSD65TSA 12g x 65 mm SS316) and steel joists (e.g. Strong-Tie CBSDHG214SA 10g x 55 mm winged self-driller); construction adhesive in T&G and end joints.
- Control joints (min. 10 mm wide): max 12,000 mm grid internal (carpet/timber coverings), max 4,500 mm grid external, plus at thickness changes, structure steps, floor-system junctions and over structural beams; joints perpendicular to span need 2 joists unless single joist ≥ 63 mm wide at top; sealed with backing rod + sealant 10 mm x 5 mm.
- Durability: can be left exposed to the elements indefinitely; no waterproofing deadline; moisture will not cause lifting; cut edges need no sealing (unlike FC/AAC); suited to flood-prone areas; non-combustible; passes AS/NZS 4456.10 durability (per comparison table).
- Wet areas: waterproof to NCC Volume One 3.8.1.2 and AS 3740 / membranes to AS 4654.1 installed per AS 4654.2, with bond-breaker tape over sealant joints.
- Installation/handling: carry panels on edge (flat carrying can crack panels); typical floor installed in about one day; accepts carpet, tiles, hybrid and hardwood finishes; surface can be locally sanded (40 grit) down to not less than 32.5 mm thickness; pin holes and minor craze cracking are normal and not warrantable defects.
- Cutting: PCD blade track/circular saw; P1 respirator minimum for dust-generating work despite silica-free composition.
- Builder testimonials cite BASIX star rating uplift and concrete-slab feel on timber-frame first floors (Fowler Homes, Wincrest Group, others).

## Caveats

- Marketing-led design guide — the NatHERS/BASIX improvement claim is qualitative; **no R-value, thermal conductivity or thermal mass figure is published**. For energy modelling, obtain XCEM's engineering data or model the 35 mm concrete layer from first principles.
- Load capacity table and construction detail figures did not survive text extraction — span/load limits must be read from the source PDF or project engineer documentation.
- 2025 edition; check XCEM for current fixings/details before specification.
