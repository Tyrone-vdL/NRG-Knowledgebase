# NCC 2019 Building Code of Australia — Volume Two, Amendment 1 — Part 3.12.1.5 Floors (surgical extract)

## Summary

NCC 2019 Volume Two (Amendment 1) is the **superseded, archived** edition of the BCA for Class 1
and Class 10 buildings — the **6-star** era, predecessor to NCC 2022's 7-star + Whole-of-Home
regime. This page is a **surgical extract of the floor energy-efficiency provisions only**
(Part 3.12.1.5 Floors + Table 3.12.1.4), ingested specifically to close a live gap: the bot had
no NCC 2019 floor R-values and was answering NCC 2019 floor questions with NCC 2022 Housing
Provisions constructs (which is wrong — see Caveats). Only the floor clauses were extracted from
the 593-page PDF; the rest of Part 3.12 (roofs, walls, glazing, sealing, air movement, services)
is **not** captured here — see the NCC 2016 handbook page [[src-ncc-volume-two-energy-efficiency-provisions-2016]]
for the equivalent-era structure and NCC 2022 for current rules.

> Use for buildings approved under NCC 2019, or where a client/permit explicitly cites NCC 2019
> clause numbers. For current work use NCC 2022 ([[src-ncc2022-housing-provisions-2022]]).

## Key claims — Part 3.12.1.5 Floors

### Required minimum Total R-Value — Table 3.12.1.4 (suspended floor)

Clause **3.12.1.5(a)(i)**: a suspended floor (other than an intermediate floor in a multi-storey
building) **must achieve the Total R-Value in Table 3.12.1.4**:

| Climate zone | Direction of heat flow | Minimum Total R-Value |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Up | **1.5** |
| **2** | **Up** | **1.0** |
| 3 | Up | 1.5 |
| 4 | Down | 2.25 |
| 5 | Down | 1.0 |
| 6 | Down | 2.25 |
| 7 | Down | 2.75 |
| 8 | Down | 3.25 |

*Note to Table 3.12.1.4: for an enclosed perimeter treatment, the underfloor airspace and its
enclosure may be included in the Total R-Value calculation.* This single figure is the requirement
**regardless of whether the subfloor is enclosed or unenclosed** — enclosure only changes the
inherent *base* R-Value you start from (Tables 3.12.1.5a/b below), and hence how much insulation
must be *added* to reach the target.

### In-slab / in-screed heating or cooling (3.12.1.5(a)(ii), (c), (e))

- **Suspended floor** with an in-slab/in-screed heating or cooling system: insulate the **vertical
  edge of the perimeter to R≥1.0** *and* **underneath the slab to R≥2.0** (the under-slab insulation
  may include insulation installed to meet the Table 3.12.1.4 requirement).
- **Concrete slab-on-ground** with in-slab/in-screed heating or cooling: **perimeter edge R≥1.0**.
- **Slab-on-ground in climate zone 8** (regardless of heating): **edge R≥1.0** *and* **under-slab R≥2.0**.
- Edge insulation for the above must be **water resistant** and **continuous from finished ground
  level to ≥300 mm depth, or the full depth of the slab edge** (Figure 3.12.1.4).
- **Exemption (3.12.1.5(e))**: the in-slab edge/under-slab requirements do **not** apply to an
  in-screed system used **solely in a bathroom, amenity area or the like**.
- For calculating a floor's Total R-Value, the R-Value of an in-slab/in-screed heating/cooling
  system is **ignored**.

### Enclosed-subfloor convection barrier (3.12.1.5(a)(iii))

A suspended floor that is enclosed beneath must have a **barrier at or below floor level to prevent
convection within the wall cavity from the airspace under the floor** (e.g. an imperforate flashing).

### Inherent base Total R-Values — Tables 3.12.1.5a (timber) & 3.12.1.5b (concrete)

Clause **3.12.1.5(b)**: a floor is **deemed** to have these inherent Total R-Values (subfloor-wall
columns: cavity masonry / 190 mm concrete masonry / single-skin masonry / 9 mm fibre-cement sheet).
**Added R-Value needed = required Table 3.12.1.4 value − inherent value below.**

**Table 3.12.1.5a — typical suspended TIMBER floor**

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Enclosed ≤0.6 m, up | 1.00 | 0.93 | 0.88 | 0.77 |
| Enclosed ≤0.6 m, down | 1.11 | 1.06 | 1.01 | 0.90 |
| Enclosed >0.6–≤1.2 m, up | 0.86 | 0.81 | 0.76 | 0.65 |
| Enclosed >0.6–≤1.2 m, down | 1.00 | 0.94 | 0.89 | 0.77 |
| Enclosed >1.2–≤2.4 m, up | 0.76 | 0.72 | 0.67 | 0.57 |
| Enclosed >1.2–≤2.4 m, down | 0.89 | 0.84 | 0.79 | 0.69 |
| **Unenclosed, up** | **0.39** | 0.39 | 0.39 | 0.39 |
| **Unenclosed, down** | **0.51** | 0.51 | 0.51 | 0.51 |

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Enclosed ≤0.6 m, up | 0.93 | 0.88 | 0.83 | 0.72 |

*Membrane add-ons (explanatory note 2 to 3.12.1.5b): a non-reflective membrane between/under joists
adds R0.2. A single-sided reflective membrane under an unenclosed floor with a 90 mm airspace adds
~R0.43 (up) / R1.32 (down); double-sided reflective under an enclosed floor with a 90 mm airspace
adds ~R0.55 (up) / R1.97 (down).*

## Worked answers to the live questions this closes

- **CZ2 (QLD), Class 1, suspended floor:** required **Total R-Value = R1.0 (upward heat flow)**.
  - *Unenclosed timber* base = R0.39 → add **≈R0.6** to reach R1.0.
  - *Unenclosed concrete* base = R0.34 → add **≈R0.66**.
  - *Enclosed ≤0.6 m timber* base = R1.00 → already meets R1.0 with **no added insulation**.
- **CZ2 slab-on-ground:** **no insulation required** unless there is an in-slab/in-screed heating
  or cooling system (then perimeter edge R≥1.0). Under-slab insulation is only mandated in CZ8 (or
  with in-slab heating on a suspended floor).
- **QLD overlay:** under QDC MP4.1 A1(2), the elemental BCA pathway in **CZ1/CZ2** is applied
  *disregarding 3.12.1.5(a)(i) and (iii)* — i.e. the floor Total R-Value requirement can drop out of
  the QLD elemental path. See [[src-qdc-4-1-ncc-2019-5star-dts]].

## Caveats

- **Superseded edition.** NCC 2019 is replaced by NCC 2022 (7-star + Whole-of-Home). Use only for
  NCC 2019-approved buildings or where NCC 2019 clause numbers are explicitly required.
- **Do NOT substitute NCC 2022 constructs for NCC 2019.** NCC 2019 sets a single per-zone Total
  R-Value (Table 3.12.1.4) for suspended floors. It has **no** "subfloor-wall insulation R0.5→R1.5
  by height" schedule and **no** "under-floor insulation not permitted in CZ2" rule — those are
  **NCC 2022 Housing Provisions Table 13.2.6b** constructs and must not be quoted as NCC 2019. The
  NCC never *prohibits* adding insulation; it sets minimums.
- **Surgical extract.** Only Part 3.12.1.5 (Floors) + Table 3.12.1.4 were extracted from the
  593-page Amendment 1 PDF. Roofs (3.12.1.2), walls (3.12.1.4), glazing (3.12.2), sealing (3.12.3),
  air movement (3.12.4) and services (3.12.5) are **not** captured — extract separately if needed.
- Tables transcribed from `pdf-parse` text extraction of the archived PDF; spot-check against the
  source PDF before quoting in a permit deliverable.
