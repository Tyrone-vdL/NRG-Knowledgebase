# NCC Volume Two Energy Efficiency — Frequently Asked Questions (ABCB Resource Kit)

## Summary

ABCB NCC Resource Kit FAQ document (2016) answering 14 common questions on the NCC Volume Two energy efficiency provisions for houses: acceptable software under Verification Method V2.6.2.2, finding your climate zone, calculating required insulation, glazing calculator use, window/door sealing, ventilation openings, the history of stringency increases, and applying the provisions to existing houses. Useful for plain-language explanations of R-value arithmetic, direction of heat flow, and alterations/additions practice.

## Key claims

- Software under V2.6.2.2 must be able to model roof, walls, floor, glazing, ventilation, hours of occupation and the site's climatic/topographical conditions, and determine heating and cooling loads; acceptance of a specific program rests with the State/Territory building regulator or building official.
- Climate zone is a defined term in Part 1.1 of Volume Two, with a national map and locations table; zones generally follow local government boundaries, and State/Territory climate zone maps are on the ABCB website.
- Required added insulation = minimum Total R-Value (Part 3.12.1) minus the inherent R-Value of the construction; worked example: required R5.1 roof minus R0.41 construction = R4.69 added insulation.
- An airspace contributes to Total R-Value — if bulk insulation fills the airspace, the airspace R-Value must be subtracted from the construction's Total R-Value.
- Minimum Total R-Values for roofs and floors specify a direction of heat flow (inward for warm climates, outward for cool climates); insulation must achieve the required R-Value in the specified direction, and some insulation performs differently by direction.
- Concessions exist in Part 3.12.1, e.g. in warmer climate zones an appropriately ventilated roof with ceiling insulation gains an R0.5 reduction in required Total R-Value; in milder zones cavity masonry external walls with masonry internal walls need less insulation than veneer or framed walls.
- Insulation must comply with AS/NZS 4859.1 and be labelled with the product R-Value (two values where it varies by direction of heat flow).
- Slab-on-ground floors coupled to the ground need no insulation unless they contain in-slab heating/cooling or are in climate zone 8; all suspended floors (concrete and timber) require a minimal level of insulation.
- The ABCB glazing calculator automates the Part 3.12.2 calculations from inputs of size, Total System U-Value, Total System SHGC and shading; conservative "worst case" performance values for common glazing appear in the Explanatory Information following 3.12.2.1(b).
- The calculator's "element share of % of allowance used" columns show, per window, its contribution to the storey's conductance and solar heat gain allowances; a collective figure over 100% means the glazing fails its allowance.
- External doors and openable windows serving habitable rooms must have weather seals or comply with AS 2047 air infiltration (Part 3.12.3.3); sealing is not required in climate zones 1, 2, 3 and 5 where the only air conditioning is an evaporative cooler.
- Energy efficiency air movement provisions (Part 3.12.4, Table 3.12.4.1) and health/amenity ventilation provisions (Part 3.8.5) are separate standalone requirements, but one opening can serve both.
- Stringency history: house energy efficiency provisions entered Volume Two on 1 January 2003, increased to nominal 5 star on 1 May 2006, and to 6 star on 1 May 2010 — with State/Territory adoption dates and transitional periods varying.
- For alterations/additions: provisions apply only to new building work; dispensations may be available under State/Territory regulation; for glazing compliance calculations it is suggested all glazing (new and existing) be given the same performance values, since compliance is calculated on a whole-storey basis.
- Approval Authorities may rely on certification from an energy efficiency specialist; some States/Territories require such specialists to be registered.

## Caveats

- 2016 publication referencing NCC Volume Two Part 3.12 clause numbering at 6-star stringency; NCC 2022 restructured these provisions into Part H6 and the ABCB Housing Provisions at 7-star — the principles (R-value arithmetic, direction of heat flow, whole-storey glazing) remain instructive but clause references and stringency levels are superseded.
- The stringency history stops at 6 star (2010); it predates the NCC 2022 7-star and Whole-of-Home changes.
