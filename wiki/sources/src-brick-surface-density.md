# Midland Brick — Surface Density of Walls (Brick Veneer vs High-Mass Masonry)

## Summary

A Midland Brick (midlandbrick.com.au) "Utility Bricks and Blocks — Thermal Properties" technical page giving surface densities for common masonry wall constructions: brick veneer at approximately 187 kg/m² and five high-mass cavity masonry walls ranging 246–358 kg/m², against a high-mass threshold of >220 kg/m². Useful when an assessor needs the surface density of a wall construction for thermal mass / NCC high-mass wall classification.

## Key claims

- "Surface density of a brick veneer wall is approximately 187kg/m²."
- High-mass masonry walls are defined on the page as walls "> 220kg/m²"; the table is titled "Examples of High-Mass Masonry Walls > 220kg/m²".
- Table (Description | Surface Density): Two leaves of 110mm brick with cavity, rendered one side = 358 kg/m².
- One leaf of 110mm face brick and one leaf of 90mm Maxibrick with cavity, rendered one side = 310 kg/m².
- Two leaves of 90mm Maxibrick with cavity, rendered both sides = 286 kg/m².
- Two leaves of 90mm Maxibrick with cavity, rendered one side = 261 kg/m².
- One leaf of 90mm face brick and one leaf of 90mm Fastwall with cavity, rendered one side = 246 kg/m².
- Terminology definitions on the page: R-Value (m² K/W) — "The measure of thermal resistance. Total R-Value means the sum of the 'R' values of the individual component layers in a composite element including any air spaces and associated surface resistances."
- Thermal Transmittance or U-Value (W/m²K) — "The rate at which heat moves through a wall. Total U-Value means thermal transmittance of the composite element including any air space and associated surface transmittances. This is simply the inverse of the 'R' value."
- Thermal Conductivity (W/mK) — the rate at which heat is transmitted through a material, measured in watts per square of surface area for a temperature gradient of one Kelvin per metre thickness.
- A browser find-bar visible in the screenshot highlights the phrase "Achieve a surface density of not less than 220 kg/m²" — consistent with the BCA/NCC deemed-to-satisfy high-mass wall criterion the page is addressing.

## Caveats

- Source is a desktop (macOS Safari) screenshot of the Midland Brick website technical manual page; no publication date is visible. Browser tabs reference "BCA 2009 Volume Two" and "NCC 2019 Volume", suggesting the era of capture — the >220 kg/m² criterion should be re-verified against the NCC edition in force for the project.
- Midland Brick is a Western Australian manufacturer; product names (Maxibrick, Fastwall) and densities are brand-specific examples, not generic values.
- "Maxibrick" and "Fastwall" leaf thicknesses (90 mm) and the render assumptions are as stated in the table; mortar/render specifics behind the figures are not given on the visible portion of the page.
