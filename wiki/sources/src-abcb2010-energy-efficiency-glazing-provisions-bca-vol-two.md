# ABCB — Energy Efficiency Glazing Provisions for BCA Volume Two (BCA 2010 changes + Glazing Calculator techniques)

## Summary

ABCB guidance (© 2011) explaining the residential glazing DtS changes introduced in BCA 2010 Part 3.12.2 — the move to a nominal 6-star-equivalent elemental pathway and a new glazing calculation methodology for climate zones 2–8 — plus advanced techniques for the ABCB Glazing Calculator. It covers glazing rating basics (Total U-Value, SHGC, AFRC whole-of-window rating), winter/summer exposure factors, calculator layout changes, adjustable shading device assessment, and how to treat glazing in alterations and additions.

## Key claims

- Glazing DtS provisions have been in BCA Volume Two since 2003; BCA 2010 increased stringency as part of the move to a nominal 6-star equivalent for the elemental DtS provisions, and applied a new glazing calculation methodology to climate zones 2–8 (the climate zone 1 conductance methodology was unchanged).
- For BCA purposes, glazing means windows, glazed doors and other transparent/translucent elements (e.g. glass bricks) in the building fabric, excluding roof lights; the element includes glass, any air/gas fill and the supporting frame, and must be rated as a whole (frame, edge-of-glass and centre-of-glass) under Australian Fenestration Rating Council (AFRC) protocols.
- Total U-Value measures the glazing system's ability to conduct heat (lower = better insulator); SHGC is the fraction of incident solar radiation admitted, both directly transmitted and absorbed-then-reradiated inward (lower = less solar gain).
- The CZ 2–8 conductance ("winter performance") calculation no longer sets an allowance based on floor area: total conduction heat loss through glazing must not exceed the direct solar gains available across all glazing in that storey — so there is no arbitrary cap on glazing area, and adding suitable glazing on beneficial orientations can aid compliance.
- Winter solar gains are generally only high enough to be beneficial on North, North-east and North-west orientations; more glazing on E–SE–S–SW–W orientations increases the compensation needed on NW–N–NE.
- In cooler climate zones, higher SHGCs are better (more winter gain); shading is preferable to tinting because tinting reduces SHGC in both seasons; over-shading is heavily penalised in cooler zones.
- Exposure factors: Ew (winter, higher better — above 1 indicates a beneficial orientation/shading combination) and Es (summer, lower better); the ratio Ew/Es above 1 indicates a glazing element with significant winter benefit without excessive summer penalty.
- Calculator changes first issued with BCA 2010: floor types entered as "Direct Contact" or "Suspended" with a composite stringency where both occur on a storey; air movement entered as "Standard"/"High" (or a value between 1 and 2 for intermediate ventilation opening areas); a wall insulation concession input reflecting the Table 3.12.1.3b high-mass wall trade-off, which increases glazing stringency; multi-storey dwellings still require a separate calculator report per storey.
- Adjustable shading "devices" (3.12.2.2(b), e.g. roll-up awnings) are assessed with two calculator reports — a summer report with the device deployed (solar heat gain ≤100% of allowance) and a winter report with it retracted (conductance ≤100% of allowance) — which together evidence compliance.
- Compliance passes when the combined "% of allowance used" by all glazing is not more than 100% for both conductance and solar heat gain; element-share percentages identify which windows to change for the most impact.
- Alterations/additions where no whole-dwelling upgrade is required: it is reasonable to assess compliance as if the new glazing were uniformly applied to the whole storey, but require complying glazing only in the alteration/addition — subject to the Building Control Authority and State/Territory legislation.
- Where elemental glazing compliance is too demanding, the whole-of-house energy rating approach (star rating) may be more flexible, as it trades performance between building elements.

## Caveats

- Historic guidance for BCA 2010 (published 2011): Part 3.12.2 and the 6-star elemental glazing methodology were superseded — under NCC 2022 the equivalent provisions sit in ABCB Housing Provisions Part 13.3 within the 7-star framework. Calculator mechanics described here do not match current tools.
- National BCA only; State/Territory variations and the discretion of the Building Control Authority govern alterations/additions treatment.
