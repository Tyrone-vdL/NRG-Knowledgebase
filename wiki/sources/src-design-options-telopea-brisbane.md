# Design For Place — Telopea House Design Options (Brisbane)

## Summary

Australian Government Design For Place pack giving the building specifications that take the Telopea reference house (4 bed, 3 bath, 2 living, 182 m² two-storey, north to the side of the block) to 7.6, 8.2 and 8.4 NatHERS stars in the Brisbane climate. The Brisbane Telopea hits 7.6 with single-glazed low-e windows on a plain slab; stepping to 8.2/8.4 is purely glazing (double glazed low-e in aluminium, then timber frames) plus Option 2 sub-tropical shading (larger eaves, covered pergola, garage moved north). Companion to the Telopea Brisbane Technical Drawings and the Hobart/Melbourne versions of the same design.

## Key claims

- Star rating options modelled for Brisbane: 7.6, 8.2 and 8.4. The 7.6 and 8.2 specs are for the 'Brisbane – Option 1' design; 8.4 is 'Brisbane – Option 2', with sub-tropical tweaks: garage moved to the north, revised shading and eave depths, window types and a covered pergola.
- On average a 7 star home in Brisbane is predicted to use 21% less heating/cooling energy than a 6 star home; an 8 star home 42% less. The Telopea 7.6 requires 32% less energy than a 6 star house; the 8.4 requires 48% less.
- Ground floor (all options): 100 mm concrete slab on ground — no waffle pod or under-slab insulation specified for Brisbane. First floor: timber joists with R2.0 insulation in floor framing between floors. Finishes: burnished concrete to living areas, carpet to bedrooms, ceramic tiles to wet areas.
- Ceiling (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R4.0 batt insulation — lower than the R5.0 specified for the Hobart and Melbourne Telopea.
- Roof (all options): metal sheet roofing on timber battens with R1.5 reflective foil laminate.
- External walls (all options): ground floor brick veneer with R2.5 batt insulation and reflective foil membrane (vapour permeable if appropriate), 10 mm plasterboard; first floor lightweight cladding on battens, reflective foil membrane, timber frame, R2.5 batt insulation, 10 mm plasterboard.
- Internal walls (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R2.0 batt insulation.
- Windows: single glazed, low emissivity glass, aluminium frame (7.6); double glazed, low emissivity glass, aluminium frame (8.2); double glazed, low emissivity glass, timber frame (8.4).
- Eaves: 600 mm for the 7.6 and 8.2 designs; the 8.4 design has larger eaves, additional shading devices and a covered pergola for sub-tropical solar protection.
- Draught sealing (all options): weather strips to all windows and sliding doors; seals/weather-strips to hinged external doors; sealed exhaust fans to bathroom, ensuite, powder room and kitchen rangehood.
- Ceiling fans (all options): 1400 mm diameter — one in each bedroom, one in the dining area, one in the front living area.
- Approximately 160 m² of roof space is available for solar panels; living area ceiling heights 2.7 m; includes a double carport.
- Star ratings are estimates based on generic window performance values; any change to specifications or drawings affects the rating.
