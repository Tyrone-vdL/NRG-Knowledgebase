# CSIRO Calculation — Additional R-Value of Polystyrene Waffle Pods Under a Concrete Slab

## Summary

CSIRO report (CMIT(C)-2004-123, March 2004, by Angelo Delsante) for Foamex Victoria calculating the additional thermal resistance a polystyrene waffle pod slab system provides compared with a conventional 100 mm concrete slab-on-ground. The headline result is an additional R-value of approximately 0.9–1.0 m².K/W. This is the commonly cited technical basis for the R-value credit assessors apply to waffle pod slabs.

## Key claims

- The system analysed: 80 mm concrete top layer, 305 mm concrete edge beams, internal concrete beam grid, with 225 mm high polystyrene pods (50 mm solid top layer plus 175 mm high x 25 mm wide legs; perimeter legs 35 mm wide) forming 36 air voids per pod.
- Additional thermal resistance vs a 100 mm raft slab: Case 1 (300 mm edge beam / 110 mm internal beam) = 0.9 m².K/W; Case 2 (270/100 mm) = 1.0 m².K/W; Case 3 (110/110 mm) = 1.0 m².K/W.
- Material properties used: polystyrene thermal conductivity 0.0375 W/m.K; concrete 1.4 W/m.K; air void resistance 0.17 m².K/W (still air, mean of heat-flow-up and heat-flow-down); ground resistance 1.8 m².K/W (soil conductivity 1.4 W/m.K); carpet 0.2 m².K/W; surface air film 0.12 m².K/W.
- Concrete beams occupy 18.8–23.0% of slab surface area depending on case; polystyrene legs 24.5–25.8%; air voids 52.5–55.4%.
- Method: mean of upper and lower limits of total system resistance. Because concrete and polystyrene conductivities differ greatly, the limits diverge considerably, which reduces accuracy — results rounded to one decimal place.
- All cases modelled 153 (17 x 9) foam pods on slabs of roughly 224–236 m².

## Caveats

- Dated March 2004 and marked CONFIDENTIAL — prepared for Foamex Victoria; results are specific to the pod geometry and slab dimensions modelled, not a generic rating for all waffle pod systems.
- CSIRO explicitly notes reduced accuracy due to the wide gap between upper and lower resistance limits.
- The slab was assumed carpeted; the additional-resistance result is a comparison against a conventional raft slab, not a standalone floor R-value.
