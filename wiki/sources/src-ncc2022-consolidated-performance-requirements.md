# NCC 2022 Consolidated Performance Requirements — energy efficiency PRs (surgical extract)

## Summary

The ABCB's single-document compilation of every Performance Requirement from all three NCC 2022 volumes (1 May 2023 print), used to look up the mandatory performance level any Deemed-to-Satisfy or Performance Solution must meet — including the residential energy PRs H6P1/H6P2 and the commercial energy PR J1P1. This page is a surgical extract: the energy-efficiency Performance Requirements are captured verbatim; everything else is navigation only. Notably, this consolidated print lists only J1P1 under Volume One Part J1 — J1P2, J1P3 and J1P4 (the Class 2 SOU thermal/energy and renewable/EV PRs, new for 2022) appear in NCC 2022 Volume One itself but not in this compilation.

## What the document is

- Publisher: Australian Building Codes Board (ABCB). Print date: 1 May 2023. Licence: CC BY-ND 4.0.
- Contains the Governing Requirements (Parts A1–A7) plus the Performance Requirements only — no Verification Methods and no Deemed-to-Satisfy Provisions. DtS detail lives in the individual volumes and the ABCB Housing Provisions.
- Useful as the quick-reference "what must be achieved" layer; pair with [[src-ncc-2022-vol-2-h6-energy-efficiency]] (Vol Two DtS/VM detail) and the Housing Provisions Section 13 extract ([[src-ncc2022-housing-provisions-2022]]) for "how to comply".

## Volume Two — Part H6 Energy efficiency (Class 1/10a) — verbatim

### H6P1 — Thermal performance [2019: P2.6.1]

(1) The total heating load of the habitable rooms and conditioned spaces in a building must not exceed the heating load limit in Specification 44.
(2) The total cooling load of the habitable rooms and conditioned spaces in a building must not exceed the cooling load limit in Specification 44.
(3) The total thermal energy load of the habitable rooms and conditioned spaces in a building must not exceed the thermal energy load limit in Specification 44.

### H6P2 — Energy usage [2019: P2.6.2]

(1) The energy value of a building's domestic services must not exceed 70% of the energy value with—
(a) a 3-star ducted heat pump, rated under the 2019 GEMS determination, heating all spaces that are provided with heating; and
(b) a 3-star ducted heat pump, rated under the 2019 GEMS determination, cooling all spaces that are provided with cooling; and
(c) a 5-star instantaneous gas water heater, rated under the 2017 GEMS determination, providing all domestic hot water; and
(d) a lighting power density of 4 W/m² serving all internal spaces that are provided with artificial lighting.

(2) Domestic services, including any associated distribution system and components must, to the degree necessary, have features that facilitate the efficient use of energy appropriate to (a) the domestic service and its usage; (b) the geographic location of the building; (c) the location of the domestic service; (d) the energy source.

## Volume One — Part J1 Energy use (Class 2–9) — verbatim

### J1P1 — Energy use [2019: JP1]

A building, other than a sole-occupancy unit of a Class 2 building or a Class 4 part of a building, including its services, must have features that facilitate the efficient use of energy appropriate to—
(a) the function and use of the building; and
(b) the level of human comfort required for the building use; and
(c) solar radiation being— (i) utilised for heating; and (ii) controlled to minimise energy for cooling; and
(d) the energy source of the services; and
(e) the sealing of the building envelope against air leakage; and
(f) for a conditioned space, achieving an hourly regulated energy consumption, averaged over the annual hours of operation, of not more than—
  (i) for a Class 6 building, 80 kJ/m².hr; and
  (ii) for a Class 5, 7b, 8 or 9a building other than a ward area, or a Class 9b school, 43 kJ/m².hr; and
  (iii) for all other building classifications, 15 kJ/m².hr.

### J1P2–J1P4 — NOT in this consolidated print

This 1 May 2023 consolidated compilation lists only J1P1 under Part J1 (both in its table of contents and body). The Class 2 SOU energy PRs — **J1P2 Thermal performance of a sole-occupancy unit of a Class 2 building or a Class 4 part of a building**, **J1P3 Energy usage of a sole-occupancy unit of a Class 2 building or a Class 4 part of a building**, and **J1P4 Renewable energy and electric vehicle charging** (all "New for 2022") — are published in NCC 2022 Volume One itself. For their verbatim text see [[src-ncc2022-volume-one]]. When citing J1P2–J1P4, cite Volume One, not this compilation.

## State/territory variations relevant to energy (from the schedules)

- **Queensland** — schedule references the **Queensland Development Code MP 4.1 – Sustainable buildings** as varying Section J (Vol One), Part H6 (Vol Two) and Section 13 (Housing Provisions). See [[src-qdc-mp4-1-sustainable-buildings]].
- **Tasmania** — schedule references **BCA 2019 Amendment 1** for "TAS Section J / TAS Part H6 / TAS Section 13", i.e. Tasmania retains the NCC 2019 energy provisions in place of the 2022 ones.

- **NSW** — in this compilation NSW varies H2P3 (rising damp) and H7P1 (swimming pool access) in Volume Two; no NSW H6 variation appears here (NSW's BASIX-driven energy variations operate through the Volume One/Two schedules and BASIX — see [[src-ncc2022-volume-one]] for NSW J1P1/J1P4 and BASIX transition notes).

## Useful glossary entries captured (Schedule 1)

- **Reference building:** for Volume One, a hypothetical building used to calculate maximum allowable annual greenhouse gas emissions (common area of Class 2 / Class 3–9) or heating load, cooling load and energy value (Class 2 SOU / Class 4 part); for Volume Two, a hypothetical building used to determine the maximum allowable heating load and cooling load for the proposed building.
- **Reflective insulation:** a building membrane with a reflective surface such as a reflective foil laminate, reflective barrier, foil batt or the like capable of reducing radiant heat flow. Explanatory note: emittance + reflectance = 1 for a given surface; typical added R-Values are given in Section 13 of the ABCB Housing Provisions.
- **Glazing** has separate definitions for Section J generally vs a Class 2 SOU / Class 4 part.
- Referenced documents table maps **AS/NZS ISO 9972:2015** (air permeability fan-pressurisation test) and **AS/NZS 5601 Part 1:2013** (gas installations) to J1V4 / H6V3, and **ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 140:2007** to J1V1, J1V2, J1V3, J1V5 / H6V2.
