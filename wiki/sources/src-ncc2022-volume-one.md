# NCC 2022 Volume One — Section J Energy Efficiency for Class 2-9 buildings (surgical extract)

## Summary

NCC 2022 Volume One (Building Code of Australia, 1 May 2023 print) is the commercial/multi-residential code for Class 2–9 buildings, and its Section J contains the energy efficiency provisions NRG touches when a job involves Class 2 apartments — including the new-for-2022 Part J3 elemental provisions for sole-occupancy units (the Class 2 mirror of Housing Provisions Section 13) and the J1P2/J1P3 performance requirements that bring NatHERS 7-star average ratings and whole-of-home into apartments. This page extracts the Section J structure (Parts J1–J9 and what each covers) plus the key Class 2 SOU fabric, glazing and energy-usage requirements; the rest of the volume is navigation only.

## Volume structure (navigation)

Sections: A Governing requirements; B Structure; C Fire resistance; D Access and egress; E Services and equipment; F Health and amenity (F8 = condensation management); G Ancillary provisions (G6 occupiable outdoor areas, G7 livable housing); I Special use buildings; **J Energy efficiency**. Schedules: 1 Definitions; 2 Referenced documents; 3+ Commonwealth and state/territory variations (large NSW schedule). There is no Section H in Volume One.

## Part J1 — Performance Requirements (verbatim)

### J1P1 — Energy use [2019: JP1]

A building, other than a sole-occupancy unit of a Class 2 building or a Class 4 part of a building, including its services, must have features that facilitate the efficient use of energy appropriate to: (a) the function and use of the building; (b) the level of human comfort required; (c) solar radiation being (i) utilised for heating and (ii) controlled to minimise energy for cooling; (d) the energy source of the services; (e) the sealing of the building envelope against air leakage; and (f) for a conditioned space, achieving an hourly regulated energy consumption, averaged over annual hours of operation, of not more than — **Class 6: 80 kJ/m².hr; Class 5, 7b, 8, 9a (other than ward area) or Class 9b school: 43 kJ/m².hr; all other classifications: 15 kJ/m².hr**.

### J1P2 — Thermal performance of a SOU of a Class 2 building or a Class 4 part [New for 2022]

(1) The total heating load of the habitable rooms and conditioned spaces in a sole-occupancy unit of a Class 2 building or a Class 4 part of a building must not exceed the heating load limit in Specification 44.
(2) The total cooling load … must not exceed the cooling load limit in Specification 44.
(3) The total thermal energy load … must not exceed the thermal energy load limit in Specification 44.

### J1P3 — Energy usage of a SOU of a Class 2 building or a Class 4 part [New for 2022]

(1) The energy value of the domestic services of the SOU must not exceed the energy value with: (a) a 3-star ducted heat pump (2019 GEMS) heating all heated spaces; (b) a 3-star ducted heat pump (2019 GEMS) cooling all cooled spaces; (c) a 5-star instantaneous gas water heater (2017 GEMS) providing all domestic hot water; and (d) a lighting power density of 4 W/m² serving all internally lit spaces. (2) Domestic services must, to the degree necessary, have features facilitating efficient energy use appropriate to the service and its usage, geographic location, service location, and energy source.

Note: unlike H6P2 for Class 1 (which is capped at **70%** of the reference energy value), J1P2/J1P3 for apartments is capped at 100% of the reference — the apartment WoH benchmark is softer than the house benchmark.

### J1P4 — Renewable energy and electric vehicle charging [New for 2022]

A building must have features that facilitate the future installation of on-site renewable energy generation and storage and electric vehicle charging equipment.

## Part J1 — Verification Methods

- **J1V1 NABERS Energy** [2019: JV1]: Class 5 — minimum 5.5-star NABERS Energy base building Commitment Agreement with emissions ≤ 67% of the 5.5-star level (excluding tenant supplementary HVAC, external lighting, carpark services) and PMV −1 to +1 across ≥95% of occupied floor area for ≥98% of annual hours; Class 2 (other than SOUs) — minimum 4-star NABERS Energy for Apartment Buildings, common-area air-conditioning ≥18 h/day, services emissions <90% of the 5-star level; Class 3 — minimum 4-star NABERS for Hotels; Class 6 shopping centres — also covered; all plus Specification 33.
- **J1V2 Green Star**: verification via a registered Green Star – Design & As-Built or Green Star Buildings rating plus Spec 33.
- **J1V3 Verification using a reference building** [2019: JV3]: for Class 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 or Class 2 common areas, with modelling per Specs 34/35.
- **J1V4 Verification of building envelope sealing**: air permeability tested per AS/NZS ISO 9972 Method 1 of not more than — **Class 2 / Class 4 part: 10 m³/hr.m² at 50 Pa**; Class 5, 6, 8, 9a/9b (non-ward) in CZ1, 7, 8: 5 m³/hr.m²; Class 3, 9c, or 9a ward areas in CZ1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8: 5 m³/hr.m². Where a Class 2 SOU achieves ≤ 5 m³/hr.m², a manually-overridable mechanical ventilation system is required (continuous, or intermittent ≥25% of each 4-hour segment), with flow rate per formula based on floor area and bedrooms, plus combustion-appliance ventilation per AS/NZS 5601.1 cl 6.4/6.4.5 — mirrors H6V3 in Volume Two.
- **J1V5 Verification using a reference building for a Class 2 sole-occupancy unit** [New for 2022]: J1P2 verified when each SOU has, in CZ3–8, a heating load ≤ the reference building's and ≤ **120% of J1P2(1)**; and in CZ1–5 a cooling load ≤ the reference building's and ≤ **120% of J1P2(2)**; plus Specs 33 and 45. J1P3 verified by comparing domestic services energy value (including centralised infrastructure) to the reference building. Calculation method must comply with **ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 140** and must **not** be house energy rating software.

## Part J2 — Application of Section J (J2D2)

- **(1) Class 2–9 other than a Class 2 SOU / Class 4 part** — J1P1 is satisfied by: Part J4 (fabric), Part J5 (sealing), Part J6 (AC and ventilation), Part J7 (lighting and power), Part J8 (heated water + pool/spa), and J9D3 (energy monitoring).
- **(2) Class 2 SOU / Class 4 part** — J1P2 satisfied by **either** J3D3 (house energy rating software, i.e. NatHERS) **or** the elemental set: J3D4 (ceiling fans); J3D5, J3D6, J4D3, J4D7(3), J4D7(4) and Part J5 (general thermal construction); J3D7 (roofs); J3D8 + J3D11–J3D13 or J3D9 (walls and glazing); J3D10 (floors).
- **(3) Class 2 SOU / Class 4 part** — J1P3 satisfied by J3D14 (net equivalent energy usage, total floor area ≤ 500 m²) or J3D15 (house energy rating software), plus Part J6 and Part J7.
- **(4) Class 2–9** — J1P4 satisfied by J9D4 (EV charging) and J9D5 (solar PV + battery).

## Part J3 — Class 2 SOU elemental provisions (key content)

- **J3D3 — NatHERS pathway** [2019: J0.2]: the SOUs of a Class 2 building / Class 4 part must **collectively achieve an average energy rating ≥ 7 stars** and **individually ≥ 6 stars**, each including the separate heating and cooling load limits from the ABCB Standard for NatHERS Heating and Cooling Load Limits; plus thermal breaks (J3D5/J3D6), ceiling-insulation-loss compensation (Table J3D7w), general thermal construction (J4D3), floor edge insulation (J3D10(3),(5),(6)) and building sealing (Part J5).
- **J3D4 — Ceiling fans** [2019: J0.3]: required per Table J3D4 in CZ1, 2, 3 and CZ5 in NSW/Qld; fans must be permanently installed with a speed controller. Table identical to Housing Provisions Table 13.5.2 (<15 m²: 1×900 mm … ≥50 m²: 2×1400 bedroom / 3×1400 other habitable room).
- **J3D5 / J3D6 — Roof and wall thermal breaks** [2019: J0.4/J0.5]: **R ≥ 0.2** thermal break for metal sheet roofing direct-fixed to metal supports without (or with direct-fixed) ceiling lining, and at all cladding/frame contact points of metal-framed envelope walls clad with weatherboards/fibre-cement/metal sheeting; sandwich panels exempt. Mirrors Housing Provisions 13.2.3(7)/13.2.5(5).
- **J3D7 — Roofs and ceilings** [New for 2022]: per-CZ minimum R-Value tables (J3D7a–r) split by construction type — flat concrete roof, timber-framed pitched with horizontal ceiling, flat/skillion/cathedral, etc. — keyed to solar absorptance bands and reflective insulation; e.g. CZ1 flat concrete roof: with reflective insulation 1.0/1.5/2.0/2.5/3.0 across SA bands ≤0.23→0.64, without 1.5/2.0/2.5/3.0/4.0; **CZ6: R3.5, or R3.0 if the roof contains reflective insulation**. Reflective insulation: emittance ≤ 0.05, ≥ 20 mm roof space, downward facing in CZ3–8. Metal-frame thermal bridging options mirror Housing Provisions 13.2.3(3) (Total R-Value tables J3D7s/t, +R0.5 between frames, R0.13 continuous layer, or stacked layers with R0.5 top layer). Ceiling edge reductions to R3.0 within 450 mm of external walls as per the housing rules. **CZ1–5: roof upper surface solar absorptance ≤ 0.64.** Sandwich panel roofs per Table J3D7x.
- **J3D8–J3D10 — Walls, wall-glazing, floors**: external wall R-values per CZ (J3D8), wall-glazing construction option (J3D9), floors including edge insulation (J3D10 — referenced by J3D3 for slab edges on the NatHERS pathway).
- **J3D11–J3D13 — External winter/summer glazing and shading**: Cu/CSHGC allowance calculations and shading rules paralleling Housing Provisions 13.3.2–13.3.4.
- **J3D14 — Net equivalent energy usage** [New for 2022]: same whole-of-home ledger as Housing Provisions 13.6.2 — floor area factor (Table J3D14a, 0.0123 at <50 m² down to 0.0077 at 450–459 m²; identical values to Table 13.6.2a) × efficiency factor from the ABCB Standard for Whole-of-Home Efficiency Factors, + pool/spa pump energy (using **Tables 13.6.2c and 13.6.2d of the ABCB Housing Provisions**), − apportioned on-site PV (kW), compared against floor area factor × energy factor (Table J3D14b).
- **J3D15 — WoH via software** [New for 2022]: a SOU of a Class 2 building / Class 4 part must achieve a **whole-of-home rating of not less than 50** using house energy rating software. (Class 1 equivalent under Vol Two S42C3 is **60** — apartments get a lower WoH bar.)

## Part J4 — Building fabric (non-SOU: Class 2 common areas, Class 3–9)

- **J4D3 Thermal construction — general** [2019: J1.2]: AS/NZS 4859.1 insulation installation rules (reflective insulation overlap ≥ 50 mm or taped here, vs 150 mm in the Housing Provisions); deemed material properties per Spec 36; Total R-Value/U-Value calculations per AS/NZS 4859.2 (roof/floor), Spec 37 (wall-glazing), Spec 39 or CIBSE Guide A 3.5 (sub-floor/soil).
- **J4D4 Roof and ceiling** [2019: J1.3]: roof/ceiling Total R-Value ≥ **R3.7 (CZ1–5, downward heat flow); R3.2 (CZ6, down); R3.7 (CZ7, up); R4.8 (CZ8, up)**. **Roof solar absorptance ≤ 0.45 in CZ1–7** (stricter than the 0.64 residential limit).
- **J4D5 Roof lights** [2019: J1.4]: ≤ 5% of floor area served; Total system U-Value ≤ U3.9; SHGC per Table J4D5 (same shaft-index matrix as Housing Provisions 13.2.4: 0.45/0.29, 0.51/0.33, 0.76/0.49).
- **J4D6 Walls and glazing** [2019: J1.5]: **Total System U-Value of wall-glazing construction ≤ U2.0** for Class 2 common areas, Class 5/6/7/8/9b and Class 9a non-ward; for Class 3/9c/9a ward areas: **U1.1 in CZ1,3,4,6,7; U2.0 in CZ2,5; U0.9 in CZ8**. Display glazing U ≤ 5.8; Total system SHGC of display glazing ≤ 0.81 ÷ shading factor (S37C7). Wall components: Total R-Value ≥ R1.0 where wall < 80% of the wall-glazing area, else per Table J4D6a (e.g. CZ1: R2.4 commercial / R3.3 Class 3-type; CZ2–7: R1.4 commercial; CZ8: R1.4 / R3.8). Solar admittance limits per aspect per Tables J4D6b/c (e.g. commercial CZ1: 0.12 all aspects; CZ8: 0.2 E/N, 0.42 S, 0.36 W). Calculations per Spec 37.
- **J4D7 Floors** [2019: J1.6]: floor Total R-Value per Table J4D7 — without in-slab heating/cooling: **R2.0 (CZ1–7) / R3.5 (CZ8)**; with in-slab heating/cooling: **R3.25 / R4.75 (CZ8)**. A slab-on-ground without in-slab conditioning is deemed R2.0 (except CZ8, and certain CZ7 Class 3/9a-ward/9b cases). Slab edge insulation **R ≥ 1.0** (water resistant, ≥ 300 mm depth or full slab edge) for slab-on-ground in CZ8 or any floor with in-slab/in-screed conditioning (bathroom-only systems exempt).

## Parts J5–J9 (coverage summary)

- **J5 Building sealing**: J5D3 chimneys/flues dampers; J5D4 roof lights; J5D5 windows and doors; J5D6 exhaust fans; J5D7 ceilings/walls/floors construction sealing; J5D8 evaporative coolers. Applies to Class 2 SOUs too (pulled in by J2D2(2) and J3D3).
- **J6 Air-conditioning and ventilation**: system and mech-vent controls, fan/duct efficiency, ductwork insulation and sealing, pump systems, pipework insulation, space heating (incl. gas availability rules in Table J6D10), refrigerant chillers, unitary AC equipment, heat rejection equipment. Applies to SOU domestic services via J2D2(3)(b).
- **J7 Artificial lighting and power**: J7D3 lighting power density limits (large table by space type), J7D4 interior lighting/power control, J7D5 decorative/display lighting, J7D6 exterior lighting, J7D7 boiling/chilled water units, J7D8 lifts, J7D9 escalators. Applies to SOUs via J2D2(3)(c).
- **J8 Heated water supply and pool/spa plant**: J8D2 heated water per NCC Volume Three Part B2; J8D3/J8D4 swimming pool and spa heating and pumping.
- **J9 Energy monitoring and on-site distributed energy resources** [New for 2022]: J9D3 energy monitoring facilities (sub-metering); **J9D4 facilities for electric vehicle charging equipment** (carpark distribution-board provisioning per Table J9D4; stand-alone Class 7a exempt); **J9D5 facilities for solar PV and battery systems**.

## Transition and state notes (energy)

- **Staged commencement:** from 1 May 2023 to 30 September 2023, Section J of NCC 2019 Volume One Amendment 1 could be used instead of NCC 2022 Section J; **from 1 October 2023 NCC 2022 Section J applies**.
- **NSW/BASIX:** for a Class 2 building or Class 4 part — BASIX Single/Multi Dwelling Certificate issued under Version 3.0 or earlier → NSW Section J of NCC 2019 Vol One Amd 1 applies; **Version 4.0 or later → Section J of NCC 2022 applies**; BASIX Alterations and Additions Certificate → NSW Section J of NCC 2019 Amd 1 applies. NSW also substitutes its own NSW J1P1/NSW J1V1–J1V4 and NSW J4D–J7D clause set in the NSW schedule.
- **Tasmania:** for a Class 2 building and Class 4 part, Section J is replaced with Section J of **BCA 2019 Amendment 1**.
