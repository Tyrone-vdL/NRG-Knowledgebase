# ABCB Guidance Document — Development of Performance Solutions (Version 2.1)

## Summary

Short ABCB guidance document (Version 2.1, circa 2016) setting out the standard four-step collaborative process for developing Performance Solutions that comply with the Performance Requirements of NCC Volumes One, Two and Three: prepare a performance-based design brief (PBDB), carry out analysis/modelling/testing, collate and evaluate results, and prepare a final report. It is the process framework referenced by the ABCB's energy efficiency case studies and applies to both simple and complex Performance Solutions.

## Key claims

- Compliance with the NCC is only achieved by satisfying the Performance Requirements; this document facilitates Performance Solutions across all three NCC volumes, for both simple and complex proposals.
- The four-step process: (1) prepare a PBDB, (2) carry out analysis, modelling or testing, (3) collate and evaluate results, (4) prepare a final report.
- The PBDB is developed collaboratively with key stakeholders and, once finalised, becomes the platform for the design — recording the agreed methodology, acceptance criteria and approval pathway so the design can proceed with high confidence of approval if the PBDB requirements are achieved.
- Key stakeholders typically include the building owner/representative, builder/project manager, design practitioners (architects, engineers, ESD/HVAC specialists, building designers, trades), the appropriate approval authority (building surveyors), and other relevant agencies (health, environment, fire safety, infrastructure). To avoid conflicts of interest, the approval authority may limit its involvement to the PBDB stage only.
- A PBDB should typically include: a summary of the proposal (building type/function, effective height, location), description of the proposed solution, nominated applicable Performance Requirement(s), agreed analytical assessment processes, agreed acceptance criteria, required scope of supporting evidence, format/content of the final report, and acknowledgement of participants.
- Analysis must use one or more of the NCC Assessment Methods; example evaluation tools include comparative or absolute analysis, qualitative or quantitative analysis, deterministic or probabilistic analysis, empirical calculations, in-situ or laboratory testing, and computer-aided modelling. If results do not meet the agreed acceptance criteria, the analytical method is re-evaluated and re-run.
- Evaluation must compare results against the PBDB's agreed acceptance criteria, accounting for uncertainties and sensitivities; further analysis/testing is required if outcomes are inconsistent with the criteria.
- The final report should demonstrate compliance with the agreed Performance Requirements and typically include: PBDB overview (scope, stakeholders, requirements, methods, assumptions, acceptance criteria and safety factors), the analysis outline (methods, calculations, sensitivity/redundancy/uncertainty studies, results), evaluation (comparison with criteria, expert judgement applied and its justification), and conclusions (accepted final design specifications, Performance Requirements met, and all design limitations and conditions of use).
- The document is non-mandatory: using it does not guarantee acceptance or accreditation of a design, nor does it itself establish NCC compliance.

## Concepts touched

NCC, Performance Solutions, performance-based design brief, assessment methods, training

## Caveats

- Version 2.1 (circa 2016): the ABCB has since published an expanded Performance Solution process handbook, and NCC 2019 Amendment 1 introduced a mandatory Performance Solution documentation process (A2.2(4), now A2G2(4) in NCC 2022) that formalises essentially these steps — check the current NCC Governing Requirements before relying on this version.
- Process guidance only; contains no technical or energy efficiency values.
