# Changes to the 2022 NatHERS Certificates and Non-Accredited Reports

## Summary

Official NatHERS guide (Version 5, September 2022) walking through every change on the 2022 NatHERS Certificate formats — the house (Class 1) and apartment (Class 2/4 unit) certificate, the Class 2 unit-block summary certificate, and the non-accredited report. Key additions: the Whole of Home performance rating out of 100, heating/cooling load limits from ABCB Standard 2022.1 displayed on page 1, new schedules (appliances, thermal bridging for steel frames, onsite renewables, batteries), an expanded design-vs-built checklist, and a verification QR code. Essential reference for reading and certifying against the post-NCC 2022 certificate layout.

## Key claims

- The 2022 certificate retains the thermal performance star rating to demonstrate compliance with the thermal performance requirement in NCC 2022; it adds a separate Whole of Home (WoH) performance rating out of 100.
- WoH rating: 100 = a net zero (energy value) home; ratings above 100 are possible where a home generates more energy than it uses. It covers heating and cooling, hot water, lighting, pool/spa equipment, cooking and plug-in appliances, minus onsite generation (solar/batteries).
- NCC compliance pathways stated on the certificate: houses (Class 1) — Specification 42 of NCC 2022 Volume Two; apartments (Class 2 SOUs and Class 4 parts) — clauses J3D3 and J3D15 of NCC 2022 Volume One. State/territory variations may apply and are now flagged on the certificate (BCA Volume + variation Yes/No).
- New thermal performance section shows modelled heating and cooling loads (MJ/m²) against load limits taken from ABCB Standard 2022.1, plus the features determining the load limits: floor type of lowest conditioned area (CSOG / SF / NA), NCC climate zone 1 or 2 (Y/N/NA), outdoor living area (Y/N/NA), outdoor living area ceiling fan (Y/N/NA).
- A flat assumption of 5 W/m² is used for lighting, so lighting is not included in the appliance schedule.
- New thermal bridging schedule for steel frame elements records steel section dimensions, frame spacing, BMT steel thickness and thermal break (yes/no). Thermal breaks are defined as materials with R-value ≥ 0.2 separating metal frame from cladding (e.g. timber battens ≥ 20 mm thick, polystyrene sheeting, plastic strips or furring channels).
- Expanded (non-mandatory) certificate checklist allows assessor, consent authority/surveyor and builder to verify the home is designed and then built per the assessment inputs, at approval and construction stages, including a genuine-certificate check via QR code/web link.
- Class 2 summary certificate: thermal star rating shown is the average of all units; the WoH rating shown is the lowest individual unit rating — there is no average WoH requirement.
- Non-accredited reports (produced by unaccredited "raters" using NatHERS accredited software) are printed black and white with no NatHERS logos, show star and WoH ratings as text rather than graphics, and carry a reliance-at-your-own-risk disclaimer; raters need no formal qualifications, insurance, CPD or quality assurance.
- Glossary additions include: energy value (net cost to society as defined in the ABCB Housing Provisions Standard), net zero home (net zero energy value), provisional values (e.g. unspecified wall colour must be modelled as 'medium'), and exposure categories (exposed/open/suburban/protected).
- Predicted onsite renewable energy section reports the WoH rating excluding generation, annual GHG emissions with and without solar (kg CO2e), predicted annual electricity use (kWh) and percentage exported vs used.

## Caveats

- Document is Version 5, September 2022 — certificate formats and the verification URL (a dev Azure site in the sample) may have been updated since.
- Source is a heavily annotated sample certificate; much of the extracted text is placeholder boilerplate ([address], 0000.0 etc.), and table layout degraded in extraction — verify field positions against the source PDF.
