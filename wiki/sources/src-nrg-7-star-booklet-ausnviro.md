# Ausnviro 7 Star Information Booklet (1 June 2023, NRG Co-branded Variant)

## Summary

The Ausnviro-branded edition (dated 1 June 2023) of the 7 Star client information booklet that NRG later reissued under its own brand as [[src-nrg-7-star-booklet-010224]] — same core content introducing the NCC 2022 7-star NatHERS requirement, Whole of Home ratings, the preliminary assessment offer and the WoH information checklist, but with a compilation of appended government fact sheets. The appendices reproduce official NatHERS and Victorian Government materials, including the 8-page "Changes to the 2022 NatHERS Certificates" guide (Version 5, September 2022).

## Key claims

- Core booklet content is substantively identical to the NRG 010224 edition: NCC 2022 raises the building shell minimum from 6 to 7 NatHERS stars; introduces a Whole-of-Home annual energy use budget for fixed appliances with rooftop-solar offset; removes the Victorian solar water heater/rainwater tank variation; and requires apartments be designed for solar and EV-charging retrofit.
- Timing as stated in this edition: the 7-star requirement was "being made mandatory as of the 1st of October 2023" (no extension mentioned — this dates the document before the deferrals reflected in the 2024 NRG edition).
- Preliminary assessments "will be quoted per dwelling" — no price range given (the later NRG edition specifies $250-$400 + GST per dwelling).
- Repeats the claim that the majority of dwellings assessed would not achieve 7 stars even with major insulation and glazing upgrades, described as industry consensus.
- The WoH information checklist matches the NRG edition: heating/cooling appliance types, efficiencies and conditioned-zone mark-up; hot water type and efficiency; lighting power density (W/m2) per Section 13.7.6 of the ABCB Housing Provisions; pool dimensions, pump type and star rating; PV array sizes (kW), azimuth/inclination, inverter size, phases and export limits. Missing information triggers software defaults that could affect compliance.
- Appended government materials reproduced in the booklet: the "NatHERS is Expanding" infographic; "Using NatHERS to pass Energy Efficiency Requirements in the NCC" infographic; Figure 1 (page 5) of the Whole of Home Guidance Note (v20220927); "Top tips for building for 7 Stars"; and the Victorian DEECA homebuyer fact sheet — each summarised separately in this wiki.
- The appended "Changes to the 2022 NatHERS Certificates" guide (Version 5, September 2022, 8 pages) details the new certificate features: Whole of Home performance rating out of 100 (ratings above 100 possible where a home generates more energy than it uses); heating/cooling load limits from ABCB Standard 2022.1 with features determining load limits (floor type CSOG/SF, NCC climate zone 1 or 2, outdoor living area, outdoor living area ceiling fan); expanded certificate checklist; new appliance, onsite renewable energy, battery and steel-frame thermal bridging schedules; predicted annual impact by appliance (energy, emissions, cost); and a Whole of Home rating excluding onsite renewables.
- The certificate guide cites NCC 2022 references: houses under Specification 42 of NCC Volume Two; apartments under clauses J3D3 and J3D15 of NCC Volume One. A flat assumption of 5 W/m2 is used for lighting in the appliance schedule.
- The guide's glossary defines key terms with values: thermal breaks are materials with an R-value >= 0.2 separating metal frame from cladding (e.g. timber battens >= 20 mm); U-value is the rate of heat transfer through a window (lower is better); SHGC is the fraction of incident solar radiation admitted through a window, 0-1 (lower transmits less solar heat); a net zero home achieves net zero energy value.
- Class 2 summary certificates show the average thermal star rating across all units but the lowest Whole of Home rating (no average WoH requirement); non-accredited reports are produced in black and white without NatHERS logos, with star ratings written as text.

## Caveats

- Dated 1 June 2023 and superseded by the NRG-branded 1 February 2024 edition ([[src-nrg-7-star-booklet-010224]]) for the introductory pages; its "mandatory 1 October 2023" statement predates the deferrals noted in the later edition.
- Branding/relationship: published under "© Ausnviro" — the NRG library treats this as the co-branded predecessor of NRG's booklet; client-facing service claims should be quoted from the current NRG edition.
- Large portions are reproductions of third-party government documents; certificate-sample text extraction is heavily scrambled (placeholder addresses, ligature corruption) — verify certificate details against the source PDF or the original NatHERS publications.
