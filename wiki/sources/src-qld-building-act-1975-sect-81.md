# QLD Building Act 1975 s 81 — When Alterations Trigger Whole-Building Compliance

## Summary

Section 81 of the Queensland Building Act 1975 sets the trigger at which alterations to an existing building can force the *whole* existing building or structure to be brought into compliance with current building assessment provisions: when the alterations (plus structural alterations approved or completed in the previous 3 years) exceed half the total volume of the existing building, or when the certifier decides the alterations pose a safety or fire-spread risk. This is the counterweight to the s 61 concession — exceed the half-volume threshold and the approval may condition the existing building to comply as if it were new. ## Key claims

- Section heading: "Building development approval for particular alterations may require existing building or structure to comply with building assessment provisions" (Building Act 1975 (Qld), s 81).
- s 81(1): the section applies to a building development approval for alterations to an existing building or structure if — (a) the total of the following represents more than half the total volume of the existing building or structure, measured over its roof and external walls: (i) the alterations; (ii) any previous structural alterations to it approved or completed in the previous 3 years; or (b) the building certifier has decided the alterations pose a risk — (i) to the safety of persons accommodated in or using the building or structure; or (ii) of spreading fire to adjoining buildings or structures.
- The volume test is measured "over its roof and external walls" and aggregates the current alterations with previous structural alterations approved or completed in the previous 3 years.
- s 81(2): the section does not apply if — (a) the alterations are for (i) a budget accommodation building to which chapter 7, part 3 applies, or (ii) a residential care building to which chapter 7A, part 4 applies; and (b) the purpose of the alterations is to ensure the building complies with — (i) for a budget accommodation building, the fire safety standard; or (ii) for a residential care building, the fire safety standard (RCB).
- s 81(3): the building development approval may include a condition that all, or a stated part, of the existing building or structure must comply with all or a stated part of the building assessment provisions as if it were a new building or structure.
- s 81(4): this section does not limit chapter 5, part 3.
- Practical use for energy assessors: if an addition/renovation tips past half the existing dwelling's volume (counting the last 3 years of structural alterations), the certifier can require the existing dwelling to meet current NCC energy-efficiency provisions, not just the new work — see [[src-qld-building-act-s81-form-pinnacle]] for a live certifier example, and [[src-qld-building-act-1975-sect-61]] for the earlier-provisions concession.
