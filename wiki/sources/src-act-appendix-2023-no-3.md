# Building (ACT Appendix to the Building Code) Determination 2023 (No 3) — Full Instrument

## Summary

The complete disallowable instrument DI2023-304 (made 13 December 2023 under s 136 of the Building Act 2004) containing both ACT Appendices to the BCA: Schedule 1 (NCC 2019-based, applying to building approvals 1 May 2023 – 14 January 2024) and Schedule 2 (NCC 2022-based, applying from 15 January 2024). It is the legal source of the ACT's energy efficiency alterations regime in both NCC eras — ACT 7.1.x concessions under NCC 2019 (Schedule 1) and ACT H6D10–H6D14 under NCC 2022 (Schedule 2).

## Key claims

- Instrument name: Building (ACT Appendix to the Building Code) Determination 2023 (No 3), DI2023-304, made under Building Act 2004 s 136; commences the day after notification; **revokes Determination 2023 (No 2) (DI2023-236)**. Signed by Minister Rebecca Vassarotti MLA, 13 December 2023.
- **Application split:** Schedule 1 applies to building approvals determined 1 May 2023 to 14 January 2024 (and unapproved work started in that window); Schedule 2 applies to approvals determined on or after 15 January 2024.
- **Schedule 1 is NCC 2019-based** for energy efficiency and condensation: Part F6 of NCC 2019 Vol One Amd 1 applies instead of NCC 2022 Part F8; Section J of NCC 2019 Vol One Amd 1 applies instead of NCC 2022 Section J; Part 2.6/Part 3.12 of NCC 2019 Vol Two apply for housing.
- **Schedule 1 ACT 7.1 (alterations under NCC 2019)** provides extra compliance options for house extensions against P2.6.1/P2.6.2: (a) ACT 7.1.2 allows house energy rating software to rate a "house-like" addition/extension alone — only if it has a contiguous floor area ≥100 m² (which may include up to 50 m² of existing floor), contains at least 1 kitchen in the rated area, and is isolated from the rest of the building by a draft-proof barrier complying with 3.12.3; the separate heating/cooling load limits of 3.12.0.1(a) are not required.
- ACT 7.1.2(d) anti-backsliding rule: the concession cannot be used if it would reduce the building's energy efficiency below the statutory minimum that applied when constructed/altered, or below the building's current energy efficiency (worked example: shading a 2008 extension's northerly glazing with a new extension requires offsetting the detriment).
- ACT 7.1.2(e): ACT dispensations cannot be applied inside an energy rating — all relevant existing elements must be assessed at actual performance.
- Other Schedule 1 options mirror the later NCC 2022 regime: elemental provisions for the extension (ACT 7.1.3), window-treatment credits for existing windows (ACT 7.1.4(a)), solar-film and isolated-window exclusions under Building (General) Regulation 2008 s 29 (ACT 7.1.4(d)), glazing calculator relief where northerly glazing is impractical (ACT 7.1.4(c)), and services re-use concessions (ACT 7.1.6).
- **Schedule 1 ACT 6 (building services):** since 2020 ACT electricity is renewable/offset, so certain electric options are permitted (corresponding water heater changes at 3.12.5.6 and ACTB2.2 of the ACT Appendix to the Plumbing Code); electric resistance space heating provisions at 3.12.5.4 continue to apply.
- Schedule 1 NCC 2019 Section J variations replace JV1 NABERS with an ACT version requiring base building energy use (less on-site renewables) to comply with JP1(f) and thermal comfort PMV −1 to +1 across ≥95% of floor area for ≥98% of annual operating hours, calculated per ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 140.
- **Schedule 2 is the NCC 2022-based appendix applying from 15 January 2024** — its content (ACT H6D10–H6D14 alterations pathways, Table ACT H6D12(a) window-treatment U-values, ACT H8 livable housing alterations rules, energy-metrics-instead-of-emissions substitutions) is captured in detail in [[src-ncc2022-pages-from-act-appendix-2023-no-3]].
- Non-energy ACT additions in both schedules: fire-stair notices (letters ≥20 mm/3 mm), litter control and waste management parts (F9/F10, H4 additions), building over drains (testing to AS/NZS 3500.2 s 15), swimming pool recirculation/egress rules (pools >10 m³), and lift/toilet concessions mirroring the Disability (Access to Premises — Buildings) Standards 2010.
- Referenced documents added include ISO 10077-1:2017 (window/shutter thermal transmittance, for ACT H6D12), the ABCB Livable Housing Design Standard 2022 and ABCB Housing Provisions Standard 2022.

## Caveats

- Version as at December 2023. The instrument applies "as amended from time to time" — later determinations may have replaced No 3; check the ACT Legislation Register before citing.
- Schedule 1's NCC 2019-based regime is now historical (its application window closed 14 January 2024) but remains relevant for approvals lodged in that window.
- Jurisdiction: ACT only. Flow charts and figures referenced in ACT 7.1 did not survive text extraction.
