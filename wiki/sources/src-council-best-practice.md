# Council's Best Practice Standards — CASBE/MAV Sustainable Design (Nov 2024)

## Summary

"Council's Best Practice Standards" (Version 1, last updated 21 Nov 2024) is a CASBE/Municipal Association of Victoria consolidation of the sustainable-design best-practice standards Victorian councils apply to development applications, grouped under Operational Energy, Transport, Integrated Water Management, Urban Ecology, Indoor Environment Quality, and Waste & Resource Recovery. Its headline energy standard — achieve at least 10% above minimum NCC energy compliance — plus daylight, ventilation and stormwater (STORM 100%) benchmarks are what ESD assessments for Victorian planning permits are measured against; developments varying from the standards must demonstrate how each objective is otherwise satisfied.

## Key claims

- Operational Energy: achieve an energy efficiency standard at least 10% above minimum NCC compliance; lighting at least 10% better than NCC lighting power density; daylight and occupancy sensors for external lighting; on-site renewable energy generation; external clothes drying; hot water/heating/cooling systems within one energy star rating of best available; basement carparks fully naturally ventilated or CO-monitored fan control; preliminary building energy ratings (e.g. NatHERS, JV3); individual utility meters per dwelling/non-residential area plus sub-metering of major common services; for large developments, ongoing fine-tuning commitments and ESD professionals engaged from preliminary design through construction.
- Transport: at least 1 secure bicycle space per dwelling and 1 visitor space per 4 dwellings (residential); secure bicycle parking for 10% of occupants plus end-of-trip facilities (non-residential); Green Travel Plan for large developments; EV charging infrastructure; 5% or at least 5 spaces for motorbikes/small vehicles.
- Integrated Water Management: WELS-rated fittings within one star of best available; rainwater tanks sized to capture the vast majority of otherwise-discharged rainwater, plumbed to consistent year-round use such as toilet flushing (retention tanks are in addition to any detention requirement); separate water meters per dwelling/non-residential area; low-water landscaping; fire-system test water reduction measures; exceed Victoria's best practice stormwater targets in the Urban Stormwater Best Practice Environmental Management Guidelines (BPEMG) — demonstrated by a STORM rating of at least 100% or equivalent modelling (MUSIC, Urban Developer); a stormwater pollution reduction strategy for construction works.
- Urban Ecology: protect existing on-site vegetation; additional amenity/performance vegetation; social interaction areas; indigenous and/or productive gardens; tap and drainage on balconies and courtyards.
- Daylight: single-aspect apartment habitable rooms limited to 8 m depth from a window; minimum daylight factor of 1% for 90% of floor area in living areas/kitchens and 0.5% for 90% of floor area in bedrooms; non-residential: daylight factor ≥2.0% for ≥30% of regularly occupied primary spaces; ≥70% of apartments to receive minimum 3 hours direct sunlight to living areas/private open space between 9am-3pm mid-winter; internal bedrooms capped at 10% of total apartments.
- Daylight modelling parameters: model adjacent buildings at likely developed form per current zoning (mirrored); standard reflectance values — ground 0.1, external walls/obstructions 0.4, floor 0.3, wall 0.7, ceiling 0.8; grid points at maximum 0.5 m spacing; uniform design sky of 10,000 lux; include surrounding buildings, balconies, screening and large trees.
- Ventilation: all dwellings effectively naturally ventilated; at least 60% of dwellings cross-ventilated; single-sided ventilation needs room depth ≤5 m with separated openings (each 5% of floor area); cross-ventilation needs a breeze path <15 m between openings in opposite/adjacent external walls (or wall + operable skylight), opening size >2% of floor area or 1 m² (whichever greater — e.g. a 55 m² room needs ≥1.1 m² clear open area), no more than 1 doorway between openings, and courtyard reliance requires ≥3 m depth or ≥9 m² size; mechanically assisted natural ventilation: fresh air rates of 2.5-5 L/s/m² supported by calculations.
- Other IEQ: external noise sources located away from openable windows; external shading designed per facade orientation for summer sun; openable external windows to circulation corridors and lift lobbies; avoid light courts for habitable-room daylight.
- Waste & Resource Recovery: at least 70% recycling target for all demolition and construction waste; recycling facilities at least as convenient as general waste; on-site food and garden waste management; Building Users Guide / Building Operations Manual for large developments.
- Each category closes with: developments seeking to vary from the standards must demonstrate how the objective (energy efficiency, sustainable transport, integrated water management, ecological value, IEQ, waste) is satisfactorily achieved.

## Caveats

- Victorian local-government guidance (CASBE fact sheets maintained on behalf of member councils, supported by the MAV; originally produced by the Cities of Yarra, Port Phillip, Melbourne, Stonnington and Maribyrnong) — these are planning-permit best-practice benchmarks, not NCC or NatHERS regulatory requirements, and individual councils may apply them differently.
- Version 1, updated 21 Nov 2024; sustainable design categories were renamed since the original PDF fact sheet suite, so older fact sheets may use different category names.
- Carries an explicit MAV disclaimer: general information only, no warranty of suitability.
