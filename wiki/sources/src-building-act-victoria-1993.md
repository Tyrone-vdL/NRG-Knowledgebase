# Building Act 1993 (VIC) — building work and alteration definitions, regulation-making framework (surgical extract)

## Summary

The full Victorian Building Act 1993 (Authorised Version No. 139, incorporating amendments as at 28 February 2024) — the head legislation establishing Victoria's building permit system, building surveyors, and the power to make the Building Regulations that adopt the NCC/BCA. For NRG the load-bearing content is in Part 1 (s3 definitions: "building work" and "construct", which is how alterations are captured) and Part 2 (s7 building regulations may apply to existing buildings; s9 incorporation of the BCA by reference; s10 transitional rule for new regulations vs existing permits). The Act itself contains **no energy-efficiency technical requirements and no partial-compliance provision for alterations** — those sit in the Building Regulations 2018 and the NCC adopted under them; the Act only supplies the heads of power. This is a surgical extract of a 1,162K-character Act.

- **Part 2 — Building standards**: s7 building regulations; s8 local laws; s9 incorporation by reference (BCA); s10 application of new building regulations to building work; s11 effect of planning schemes; s14 accreditation of building products.
- **Part 3 — Building permits**: s16 offences (building work requires a permit); s17–25 applications/decisions; s24 refusal of building permit.
- **Part 4 — Inspection of building work**; **Part 5 — Occupation of buildings** (occupancy permits); **Part 6 — Private building surveyors**; **Part 7 — Protection of adjoining property**; **Part 8 — Enforcement of safety and building standards** (building notices/orders).
- **Part 9 — Liability** (10-year limitation; s137B owner-builder provisions — contains its own "construct" definition); **Part 10 — Building Appeals Board**; **Part 11 — Registration of building practitioners**; **Part 11A — Licensing of building employees**; **Part 12 — Building administration** (VBA; s188A Minister's guidelines incl. energy efficiency); **Part 12A — Plumbing work**; **Part 13 — General enforcement**; **Part 14 — General**; **Schedule 1 — subject matter for building regulations**.

## Key claims

- **s3(1) — "building work" definition (exact):** "building work means work for or in connection with the **construction, demolition or removal** of a building". The word "alteration" does not appear in this definition — it enters via "construct".
- **s3(1) — "construct" definition (exact):** "construct, in relation to a building, includes — (a) build, re-build, erect or re-erect the building; and (b) **repair** the building; and (c) **make alterations** to the building; and (d) **enlarge or extend** the building; and (e) place or relocate the building on land". So an alteration, repair, or extension is "construction", and work for or in connection with it is "building work" requiring a building permit (s16) unless exempted by the regulations.
- A second, narrower "construct" definition exists in s137B(7) (owner-builder liability context): build/rebuild, make alterations, enlarge or extend, or cause/manage/arrange any of those — the library's separate one-page extract "BUILDING ACT VICTORIA 1993 - DEFINITION OF ALTERATION.pdf" is a clip of this s137B definition, not the s3 one.
- **s4(1)(f) — objectives:** an express objective of the Act is "to facilitate the construction of **environmentally and energy efficient buildings**" (inserted by amendment; s4 substituted by No. 54/2007).
- **s7 — building regulations power:** regulations may prescribe standards "expressed in terms of performance, types of material, methods of construction or in other terms" (s7(3)), may deem particular materials/designs/methods to satisfy prescribed standards (s7(4)), and **"may apply to existing buildings, whether or not building work is being or is proposed to be carried out on those buildings"** (s7(5)) and may "require the carrying out of building work and maintenance on existing buildings" (s7(2)(c)).
- **s9 — incorporation by reference:** the building regulations may apply, adopt or incorporate, wholly or partly and with or without modification, "any matter contained in the **Building Code of Australia** or any other document", as in force at a particular time or from time to time. This is the mechanism by which the NCC (including Part H6 / 7-star energy efficiency) binds in Victoria.
- **s10 — application of new regulations to existing permits/designs:** (1) a new or amended building regulation (or binding determination) does **not apply to building work carried out under a building permit existing immediately before commencement**; (2) it also does not apply if the relevant building surveyor certifies in writing that **substantial progress was made on the design of the building before commencement**; (3) such work is carried out under the provisions in force at the material times; (4) the surveyor and owner may agree that the new regulation applies. (This is the Victorian transition rule used when an NCC energy-efficiency uplift, e.g. 7-star, commences mid-project.)
- **Schedule 1, item 30:** the regulations may regulate "the manner of **alteration** and demolition of and additions to buildings, including the connecting or dividing of buildings" — the head of power under which the Building Regulations 2018 deal with alterations (including any partial-compliance treatment); the detail is in the Regulations, not this Act.
- **s188A:** the Minister may issue guidelines for single dwellings on listed matters, including "(i) matters relating to the **energy efficiency** of buildings".
- s11: where a planning scheme regulates siting, consistent building regulations must be complied with in addition; the Act manages planning/building overlap.

## Caveats

- Authorised Version No. 139, as at 28 February 2024. Victoria has since continued amending this Act (e.g. building reform legislation); verify currency before formal advice.
- **The substantive energy-efficiency and alterations-compliance rules for Victoria are NOT in this Act** — they are in the Building Regulations 2018 (which adopt the NCC under s9). Any question about partial compliance for alterations to existing dwellings in Victoria must be answered from the Building Regulations/NCC, not from this Act; the Act contains no "partial compliance" provision (term searched — zero hits).
- Two different "construct" definitions exist (s3(1) general; s137B(7) liability-specific). Cite the right one for the context.
