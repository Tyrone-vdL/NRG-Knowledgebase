# Building Act 2004 (ACT) — building work/alteration definitions, substantial alterations (s29), energy efficiency certificates (s139C) (surgical extract)

## Summary

The full Australian Capital Territory Building Act 2004 (Republication No 51, effective 6 March 2025) — the ACT's head building legislation, notable for NRG because it is the only one of the three ingested state/territory Building Acts that deals with energy efficiency procedurally on its face: s26(2)(h) requires building approval applications to be accompanied by an **energy efficiency certificate** where an energy efficiency provision requires one, and Division 8.3 (s139C–139D) governs those certificates and who may prepare them (building assessors). It also contains the **substantial alteration** rule (s29(1)(a) and s29(2)): plans for a substantial alteration must show the building **as altered** will comply with the Act and building code, but a regulation may declare what is/is not a substantial alteration and that **unaltered parts need not comply** — the ACT's statutory partial-compliance mechanism for existing dwellings. This is a surgical extract of a 472K-character Act.

## Key claims

- **s6(1) — "building work" definition (exact):** building work means "(a) work in relation to the **erection, alteration or demolition** of a building, and includes disposal of waste materials generated (i) by the alteration of a building other than a building excluded under the regulations; or (ii) by the demolition of a building (but not part of the building); or (b) work in relation to **repairs of a structural nature** to a building." s6(2): regulations may exempt kinds of work from, or include kinds of work in, the definition.
- **Dictionary — "alteration" (exact):** "alteration, of a building, includes — (a) **demolition of part** of the building; and (b) **an addition** to the building."
- **s7 — "building"** includes structures, additions, fixtures and parts of buildings, but excludes (among others) fittings **other than** fittings included to make the building comply with the building code or that cause non-compliance (s7(2)(h)).
- **s7 Example 1 (energy efficiency — quoted in substance):** where insulation batts are added to an extension to comply with the building code's energy efficiency provisions and loose batts are also laid on the existing house's ceiling, the batts are part of the extension (regulated for the extension) but **not part of the existing house** because they are not fixtures and are not regulated for the existing house — "unless they are needed in the existing house to make the extension comply with the building code". This example is direct statutory guidance on the new-work/existing-fabric boundary for energy efficiency in alterations.
- **s26(2)(h):** an application for building approval must "if required under an energy efficiency provision — be accompanied by an **energy efficiency certificate**". (The provision cross-refers to the definition in s139C.)
- **s29(1)(a) — substantial alteration:** an approval requirement for plans is that "if the plans are for the **substantial alteration** of a building — the building **as altered** will comply with this Act and the building code" — i.e. a substantial alteration can pull the whole altered building into current-code compliance.
- **s29(1)(b):** for any (non-substantial) erection or alteration, the requirement is only that the building, if erected or altered in accordance with the plans, will comply with this Act.
- **s29(2) — the partial-compliance valve (exact):** "A regulation may declare that — (a) an alteration of a building is or is not a substantial alteration; or (b) a part of a building (the **unaltered part**) that has not been altered **need not comply with the building code** despite subsection (1)(a)." The thresholds for "substantial alteration" therefore live in the Building (General) Regulation, not this Act.
- **s29(3)(b):** a building product, construction method, design, component or system is taken to comply with the building code if it complies with a **recognised standard**.
- **s139C — energy efficiency certificates:** where a person is required to provide one, it "must be prepared in accordance with the requirements prescribed by regulation". Definition (as printed in s139C): "**energy efficiency provision** means a provision of this Act or the building code, prescribed by regulation, that requires building work or proposed building work, or parts of building work or proposed building work, to comply with stated energy efficiency requirements."
- **s139D — conflict of interest:** a **building assessor** must not prepare an energy efficiency certificate for building work if the assessor has a legal/equitable or financial interest in the building or is the certifier for the work; limited exceptions with a declared conflict for proposed work or unoccupied work (s139D(2)).
- **s12 — exempt buildings:** "exempt building means a building prescribed under the regulations"; exemption narrows the application of Parts 3, 5 and 6. Part 2A exemption assessment B notices certify whether particular building work is exempt.

## Caveats

- Republication No 51, effective 6 March 2025 (last amendment A2023-55). ACT republishes frequently; check the ACT legislation register for currency.
- The s26(2)(h) note in the Act cross-references the "energy efficiency provision" definition as "section 139C (4)", while the extracted text of s139C shows the definition under "(3) In this section". Extraction or internal-numbering discrepancy — verify subsection numbering against the source PDF before quoting a pinpoint reference.
- The substantive thresholds (what counts as a substantial alteration; when unaltered parts are excused; which provisions require energy efficiency certificates and what those certificates contain) are **prescribed by regulation** (Building (General) Regulation 2008) — not in this Act and not in the wiki yet.
