# ABCB Performance Solution Example — Single Storey House Energy Efficiency (V2.6.2.2)

## Summary

ABCB worked example (PBDS 2-002-C) showing how a Performance Solution using Verification Method V2.6.2.2 in NCC Volume Two lets a Perth single-storey house use cheaper, less energy-efficient glazing that fails the DtS Provisions, compensated by other low-cost energy efficiency improvements. It walks through the performance-based design process: objectives, stakeholders, the reference-building acceptance criterion, evidence requirements and the final submission package.

## Key claims

- Scenario: a single-storey house in Perth, WA initially met the DtS glazing requirements, but the client wants cheaper non-DtS-compliant glazing and is willing to upgrade other low-cost energy efficiency elements to compensate.
- Basis of the Performance Solution is Verification Method V2.6.2.2 in NCC Volume Two; the acceptance criterion is that the revised design uses less heating and cooling energy than a "reference building" that satisfies the minimum relevant DtS Provisions.
- The applicable DtS Provisions for dwelling thermal performance are Parts 3.12.1, 3.12.2, 3.12.3 and 3.12.4; the primary Performance Requirement is P2.6.1 — Building (Part 2.6 Energy Efficiency).
- Assessment Methods are listed in clause 1.0.5; any Assessment Method or combination may be used — here the Verification Method V2.6.2.2 is the Assessment Method.
- Energy rating software used must meet the calculation method requirements in V2.6.2.2 and provide separate heating and cooling energy use outputs.
- The energy assessor's report must include: separate heating and cooling loads for reference building and revised design; which elements were varied; which software was used and how it meets calculation requirements; which climate data was used; and demonstration that the reference building meets the minimum DtS Provisions of Parts 3.12.1-3.12.4.
- Stakeholders to consult: building designer, client, builder, energy assessor and the regulatory approval authority.
- The final submission comprised the energy assessor's report plus a covering letter from the building designer summarising scope, stakeholders, the Performance Requirement assessed, modelling overview, comparison against the V2.6.2.2 acceptance criteria, and acceptance that compliance is demonstrated.
- A Performance Solution was previously known as an "Alternative Solution"; other Performance Requirements may also be impacted and must be considered in accordance with clause 1.0.7.

## Caveats

- Uses pre-NCC 2022 Volume Two clause numbering (P2.6.1, V2.6.2.2, Parts 3.12.1-3.12.4); under NCC 2022 the equivalents are H6P1 and the H6 verification methods, and stringency has since risen to 7-star/Whole-of-Home — the process is illustrative, the references are dated.
- ABCB disclaimer: a simplified example intended only for learning how to use the NCC, not an appropriate solution in all situations or jurisdictions.
- Document is undated in the extracted text; the terminology ("previously known as an Alternative Solution") places it in the NCC 2016-2019 era.
