# Design For Place — Acacia House Design Options (Melbourne)

## Summary

Australian Government Design For Place pack giving the building specifications that take the Acacia reference house (2 bed, 2 bath, 2 living, 123 m² single-storey with two courtyards) from 6.1 to 7.1 NatHERS stars in the Melbourne climate — the only city of the three Acacia packs with just two options. The 6.1-to-7.1 jump comes from three changes: waffle pod slab replaced with R1.8 under-slab insulation, eaves reduced from 1000 mm to 600 mm for more winter solar gain, and double-glazed thermally broken aluminium windows swapped for double-glazed uPVC. Companion to the Acacia Melbourne Technical Drawings and the Brisbane/Hobart versions of the same design.

## Key claims

- Star rating options modelled for Melbourne: 6.1 and 7.1 only (no 8-star option is specified, unlike Brisbane and Hobart).
- On average a 7 star home in Melbourne is predicted to use 27% less heating/cooling energy than a 6 star home; an 8 star home 53% less. The Acacia 7.1 requires 28% less energy than a 6 star house.
- Floor: 100 mm concrete slab with 300 mm waffle pod for the 6.1 option; 100 mm concrete slab on ground with R1.8 under-slab insulation for the 7.1 option. Finishes: burnished concrete to living areas, carpet to bedrooms, ceramic tiles to wet areas.
- Ceiling (both options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R5.0 batt insulation.
- Roof (both options): metal sheet roofing on timber battens with R1.5 reflective foil laminate.
- Eaves: 1000 mm overhang for the 6.1 design; the 7.1 design has the eaves reduced to 600 mm, optimised for adequate summer shading while still allowing winter solar heat gain.
- External walls (both options): lightweight cladding on battens, reflective foil membrane (vapour permeable if appropriate), timber frame, R2.5 batt insulation, 10 mm plasterboard.
- Internal walls (both options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R2.5 batt insulation.
- Windows: double glazed with thermally broken aluminium frame (6.1); double glazed with uPVC frame (7.1).
- Draught sealing (both options): weather strips to all windows and sliding doors; seals/weather-strips to hinged external doors; sealed exhaust fans to bathroom, powder room and kitchen rangehood.
- Ceiling fans (both options): 1400 mm diameter — one in each bedroom, one in the dining area, one in the front living area.
- Approximately 170 m² of roof space is available for solar panels; ceiling heights 2.7 m.
- uPVC window frames have similar insulating properties to timber frames; burnished concrete thermal mass works particularly well in cool locations but is degraded by coverings such as carpet.
