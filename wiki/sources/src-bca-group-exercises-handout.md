# NCC Resource Kit — BCA Volumes One and Two Group Exercise Handout (2016)

## Summary

A 2016 ABCB NCC Resource Kit training handout containing eight group exercises (questions only, no answers) for classroom delivery of an introduction to BCA Volumes One and Two. The exercises mirror the companion FAQs document and test understanding of building regulation purpose, the NCC's structure and legal status, Performance Solutions, classifications, compliance structure and defined terms.

## Key claims

- Contains 8 exercises: (1) building regulations — why Australia has them, what the NCC is, its legal status, who produces it; (2) the NCC as a performance-based code — what a Performance Solution is and why use one; (3) NCC application — what work must comply and how state/council regulations, the NCC and Australian Standards fit together; (4) classifications; (5) compliance structure — mandatory parts and Volume Two "Acceptable Construction Practices"; (6) defined terms (italics); (7) a Volume One worked scenario; (8) whether a Performance Solution requires a specialist practitioner.
- Exercise 7 presents a proposed office building and car park scenario asking students to determine: classification, rise in storeys, type of construction, maximum fire compartment size, and the number of sanitary facilities required for 200 employees split evenly male/female.
- The handout provides questions only — answers are expected to come from the accompanying FAQs/presentation and the NCC itself.

## Concepts touched

NCC, building classifications, Performance Solutions, training

## Caveats

- 2016 training material aligned to the pre-2019 BCA structure; purely pedagogical — no regulatory values. The worked scenario relies on diagrams that did not survive text extraction.
