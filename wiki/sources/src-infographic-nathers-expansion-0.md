# NatHERS is Expanding — Infographic on the New Two-Rating Certificate

## Summary

Official NatHERS infographic announcing the expansion of NatHERS from a thermal-shell-only rating to include a Whole of Home performance rating, with a draft representative sample of the new two-rating certificate. It explains that new certificates carry both a star rating out of 10 (thermal shell) and a 0-100 Whole of Home performance rating.

## Key claims

- NatHERS is expanding to rate the energy performance of fixed appliances — heating, cooling, hot water, solar panels and batteries — in addition to the thermal shell rating.
- New NatHERS certificates have two ratings: a thermal star rating out of 10 and a 0-100 Whole of Home performance rating; higher scores mean a more comfortable, cheaper-to-run home.
- The sample certificate shows a 7.0 star thermal rating with 107.9 MJ/m2 predicted annual energy load for heating and cooling (standard occupancy assumptions), and a Whole of Home performance rating of 60 out of 100 on a 0-100 scale ending at "Net zero home".
- The certificate records modelled heating and cooling loads against load limits taken from an ABCB Standard, plus features determining load limits: floor type (lowest conditioned area), NCC climate zone 1 or 2 (Y/N/NA), outdoor living area (Y/N/NA), outdoor living area ceiling fan (Y/N/NA).
- The sample's NCC notes: the NCC allows NatHERS accredited software to demonstrate energy efficiency compliance for houses (Class 1) and apartments (Class 2 sole-occupancy units and Class 4 parts); house requirements are in Specification 42 of NCC Volume Two, apartment requirements in clauses J2D2(2)(a) and (3) of NCC Volume One (NCC 2022).
- An earlier draft panel references the NCC 2019 clauses: houses 3.12.0(a)(i) and 3.12.5 of NCC Volume Two; apartments J0.2 and J5 to J8 of NCC Volume One, with minimum star ratings and separate heating/cooling load limits.
- NCC 2022 includes enhanced thermal performance requirements plus a new whole-of-home annual energy use budget applying to the major equipment in the home.
- Certificates carry a QR/web verification link; state and territory variations to the NCC may apply.

## Concepts touched

NatHERS, Whole of Home, 7-Star, NCC 2019, NCC 2022, Specification 42, climate zones, certificates, load limits, solar PV, batteries

## Caveats

- The sample certificate is marked "DRAFT - REPRESENTATIVE ONLY" — all figures (7.0 stars, 107.9 MJ/m2, 60/100) are placeholder illustrations, not real assessment values.
- Text extraction of the certificate sample is heavily scrambled (repeated placeholder address strings); verify certificate layout against the source PDF.
