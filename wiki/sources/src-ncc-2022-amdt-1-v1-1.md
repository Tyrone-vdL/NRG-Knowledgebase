# NCC 2022 Amendment 1 (v1.1, adopted 1 May 2025) — Consolidated Amendment Text

## Summary

The official ABCB amendment instrument for NCC 2022 Amendment 1 (Version 1.1, release date 8 April 2025, date of effect 1 May 2025, adopted by all states and territories on 1 May 2025), listing every change made to NCC 2022 Volumes One, Two, Three and the ABCB Housing Provisions. Its substantive content is dominated by new Western Australian variations — notably the WA energy efficiency carve-out letting alterations/extensions to existing Class 1 buildings use NCC 2019 Part 2.6/3.12.5 instead of NCC 2022 Part H6, and the WA option to treat climate zone 6 as climate zone 5 for fabric and glazing.

## Key claims

- **Adoption:** NCC 2022 Amendment 1 was adopted on **1 May 2025** in every jurisdiction (Cwlth, ACT, NSW, NT, QLD, SA, TAS, VIC, WA). Purpose: update copyright/licence (now CC BY 4.0), amend WA variations and additions, amend the Tasmanian variation regarding Part H8, and correct errors.
- **WA definition of "existing building" (new, Schedule 11):** for the purposes of Part H6, an existing Class 1 building or existing attached Class 10 part **that was not required to comply with the energy efficiency requirements of NCC 2022 or any later edition**.
- **WA Part H6 note (Volume Two):** for existing buildings undergoing alterations or an extension (or when installing an associated swimming pool), **Part 2.6 and Part 3.12.5 of NCC 2019 Volume Two Amendment 1 may apply instead of Part H6 of NCC 2022** — i.e. WA alterations can keep using the NCC 2019 energy efficiency regime. The explanatory information endorses Performance Solutions such as the industry-developed WA Alterations & Additions Protocol, noting NCC 2019 Part 2.6's "to the degree necessary" wording allows reasonable allowance for the existing building's performance.
- **WA Housing Provisions 13.2.1(4) and 13.3.1(3) (new):** for Parts 13.2 (building fabric) and 13.3 (external glazing) only, references to **climate zone 6 may be considered as climate zone 5** in WA. Condensation management and services provisions are unaffected by this substitution.
- **WA 13.2.7 (replaced):** attached Class 10a buildings must either have external fabric achieving the Class 1 thermal performance or be separated by construction achieving it; where a Class 1 floor provides that separation, it must be insulated with **reflective insulation facing down, minimum R2.0 in climate zone 4 and R2.5 in climate zone 5**; the same R-values apply to upper-storey floors with a conditioned space above that project beyond the ground-floor external walls.
- **WA 8.2.2 window installation:** the required minimum 10 mm gap between the top of a window assembly and loadbearing framing **does not apply to windows in masonry wall elements** in WA.
- **WA swimming pools (WA H9D3, replaced):** an outdoor private pool or spa associated with a Class 1 building must be supplied with a cover/blanket designed to reduce evaporation and complying with **AS 5348:2022** (newly referenced).
- **WA eaves gutters (new WA Part F9 Vol One / WA Part H10 Vol Two / WA 7.4 Housing Provisions):** clause 3.5.1 of AS/NZS 3500.3:2021 is replaced so eaves gutter overflow measures may follow Part 7.4 of the ABCB Housing Provisions; AS/NZS 3500.3 Table 3.5.2 is replaced with a WA version permitting 95 mm × 45 mm rectangular downpipes; both AS/NZS 3500.3 and Part 7.4 design eaves gutters for a 5-minute rainfall intensity at 5% AEP, with Part 7.4 overflow provisions at 1% AEP; gutters installed with fall <1:500 must follow AS/NZS 3500.3.
- **Tasmania Part H8 (Volume Two):** new note — for Tasmania, refer to the **Director's Determination regarding the application of Part H8 (Livable housing provisions)**, available at cbos.tas.gov.au (i.e. Tasmania's livable housing application is governed outside the NCC text).
- **Housing Provisions corrections:** Table 3.2.1 stable rock (Class A) site cut ratio corrected to 8:1 (compacted fill 2:3); 6.3.9 transition note deleted; **13.3.2(1)(b) external glazing winter formula replaced** (corrected aggregate conductance/SHGC formula for the glazing calculator method).
- Volume One changes are minor: fire safety door sign wording for self-closing doors ("FIRE SAFETY DOOR / DO NOT OBSTRUCT / DO NOT KEEP OPEN"), deletion of various notes, and AS/NZS 3500.3:2021 added to referenced documents.

## Caveats

- This is the amendment instrument only (the delta) — it must be read against the consolidated NCC 2022 text; clause numbers cited are as amended.
- Date of effect 1 May 2025; the WA energy-efficiency carve-out and climate-zone-6-as-5 substitution apply **in Western Australia only**; the Part H8 note applies in Tasmania only.
- The replaced 13.3.2 winter glazing formula rendered as garbled unicode in extraction — verify the formula against the source PDF or the consolidated NCC before use.
