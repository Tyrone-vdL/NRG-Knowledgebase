# Dowell Technical Brochure — Queensland Edition (2008)

## Summary

Queensland edition (October 2008, Boral-era) of the Dowell aluminium windows and doors technical brochure, covering wind-load/water-penetration performance criteria (N1–N6 and cyclonic C1–C4), energy efficiency basics (U-value, SHGC, BCA climate zones, state-by-state star-rating regimes circa 2008), acoustic STC ratings by glazing option, AS 1288-2006 safety glazing rules, AS 3959-1999 bushfire requirements, the full product range, warranty, installation and maintenance guidance. The QLD-specific content is the cyclonic C-rating size availability on products and Queensland sales locations.

## Key claims

- Wind performance table (AS 4055/AS 2047): N1 permissible stress 500 Pa / ULS 700 Pa / water penetration 150 Pa; N2 700/1000/150; N3/C1 1000/1500 (C1 ULS 2000)/150; N4/C2 1500/2200 (C2 3000)/200; N5/C3 2200/3300 (C3 4400)/300; N6/C4 3000/4400 (C4 6000)/450 Pa. The table carries a "Queensland variation — Permissible stress" note.
- Window energy performance criteria defined: U-value as thermal transmittance in W/m².K (lower is better) and SHGC as the fraction of solar heat passing through the unit; cooling climates need low SHGC, heating climates may need high SHGC on north-facing windows.
- In an average home an estimated 87% of heat gain and 49% of heat loss is attributed to poor performing windows.
- BCA divides Australia into 8 climate zones: zones 1–3 predominantly cooling, 4–5 mixed, 6–8 predominantly heating.
- State verification regimes as at 2008: VIC 5 Star with FirstRate; NSW BASIX (deemed-to-satisfy or NatHERS, NFRC window data); TAS/NT/QLD on 2005 BCA DtS (3.5 stars zones 1–3, 4 stars zones 4–8, ANAC window data) with a note that Queensland was to fall in line with the 5-star states on release of the May 2008 BCA; ACT/SA and WA 5 Star on 2007 BCA provisions using NFRC values. Rating tools listed: NatHERS, AccuRate, FirstRate, BERS (BERS mainly used in Queensland).
- All Dowell aluminium products classified as "aluminium improved"; Dowell is an AWA member and WERS participant (data at wers.net).
- Indicative acoustic ratings: 3mm float STC 25; 4mm float 26; 6mm float 28; 6.38mm laminated 31; 10.38mm laminated 33; double glazed sash 4-8-4 STC 35; double window with 100mm air gap STC 45. A 10 dB reduction halves perceived noise; 3 dB is the minimum detectable change (CSIRO).
- AS 1288-2006 highlights: 3mm glass limited to 0.85 m²; Grade A safety glazing in all doors; side panels within 300 mm of a door and ≤1200 mm above floor; glazing mistakable for a doorway (sight line ≥500 mm wide, ≥1000 mm high, or ≤500 mm above floor); bathrooms up to 2000 mm; schools/childcare up to 1000 mm; aged care up to 1500 mm; stairways within 2000 mm of the stair base. Maximum safety glass areas per Table 5.1 (e.g. 5mm toughened 3.0 m², 6mm laminated 3.0 m²).
- AS 3959-1999 (April 2006 bulletin): Level 1 — openable windows screened with corrosion-resistant steel/bronze/aluminium mesh, max aperture 1.8 mm; Level 2 — no aluminium mesh; Level 3 — toughened glass (intent assumes 5mm toughened) where not shuttered, and solid-core doors ≥35 mm thickness.
- Sliding door standard sill has a 200 Pa water rating, 89 mm wide × 42 mm deep internal leg; sump sill and subsill options for higher ratings.
- 10-year warranty on workmanship from delivery to site; moving parts 3 years; AS 2047 installation required.
- QLD product size availability is quoted against combined wind classes — e.g. sliding windows, casements and sliding doors N2, N3/C1, N4/C2, N5/C3; double hung N2, N3/C1; bi-fold N2, N3/C1, N4/C2 — i.e. cyclonic categories are carried throughout the QLD edition.
- QLD edition locations: Head Office Geebung (Brisbane), Gold Coast, Townsville; sales via Boral Window Systems (print code 02457 QLD Oct08).

## Caveats

- Published October 2008 — energy provisions described (3.5/4/5-star regimes, 2005/2007 BCA) are long superseded by NCC 2019/2022 7-Star Whole-of-Home requirements; AS 3959 content is the superseded 1999 edition. Use for product/structural context only, not current compliance.
- This is the Queensland state variant; size ranges include cyclonic C1–C4 categories that the Victorian edition omits.
- Several tables (wind pressures, brick charts, size grids) have degraded layout in extraction; verify figures against the source PDF.
