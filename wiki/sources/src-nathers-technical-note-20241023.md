# NatHERS Technical Note Version 20241023 (Thermal and Whole of Home Assessment Requirements)

## Summary

The current primary NatHERS Technical Note (Version 20241023, published October 2024 by DCCEEW) setting out the mandatory requirements every assessor must follow when conducting NatHERS thermal and Whole of Home assessments in regulation mode for the NCC 2022 deemed-to-satisfy NatHERS pathway, using Chenath engines 3.22 and 3.23. It covers climate zone selection, zoning, floors, walls, windows, ceilings and roofs, shading, thermal bridging, Whole of Home appliance modelling, and certificate/stamping requirements, with default values for every unspecified input. The Technical Note prevails over the Assessor Handbook, AAO guidance, software manuals and trainer advice; only state/territory regulatory requirements (e.g. BASIX in NSW) override it. The 20241023 update added a centralised heat pump hot water proxy for Class 2 buildings (clause 12.10), changed the default downlight density to 1 per 5 m², added NCC Class 1b modelling guidance (Table 1), and added a bathroom-zoning flowchart (Appendix 2).

## Key claims

- **Status and scope:** Mandatory for all NatHERS assessments (accredited and non-accredited assessors) using Chenath 3.22/3.23, designed for NCC 2022. Forms part of the software End User Licence Agreement. Applies to NCC Class 1a, 1b, 2 and Class 4 parts of buildings; attached Class 10a buildings must also be modelled. Misuse consequences include suspension/cancellation of accreditation, regulator notification and voided professional indemnity insurance (clause 1.13).
- **Class 1b (Table 1):** ≥4 single dwellings on one allotment each with kitchen/kitchenette → model each as Class 1a; boarding/guest house/hostel <300 m² with shared kitchen/living/bathroom → model as a single Class 1a dwelling; ≥4 dwellings with separate communal kitchen building → cannot be modelled in NatHERS.
- **Climate and site:** Use the postcode's principal NatHERS climate zone unless an allocated alternative zone better represents the site (justify in 'additional notes'); never use a zone not allocated to the postcode (4.1). Ground reflectance must always be 0.2 (4.3). Orientation is true north, not magnetic (4.4). Data retention minimum is 7 years (3.10).
- **Zoning minimums (5.2-5.3):** Every dwelling must contain one main kitchen/living zone, a minimum of three zones excluding garage, and at least one unconditioned zone. Studios/bedsits: kitchen/living ≥30% of floor area, bedroom ≥20%, separated by an artificial plasterboard-on-stud wall of no less than 40% wall area between zones. Zones may only be combined within garages or where a dwelling exceeds 50 zones (same orientation, zone/conditioning type and connecting zone).
- **Bathrooms (5.5-5.7, Appendix 2):** Zone per the new flowchart — broadly: parent zone if unventilated with no closable door or with in-floor heating; unconditioned if ventilated (external openable window/door) and closable from the dwelling; night-time if associated with a bedroom and not for general use. Every dwelling needs at least one general-use bathroom/WC.
- **Floors:** Waffle pods default to 175 mm thickness and 85 mm concrete cover if unspecified (6.2-6.3). Non-insulating void formers are modelled as conventional slab-on-ground; if a reflective membrane is underneath, include an unventilated reflective horizontal air gap >66 mm (E = 0.20) (6.4). Default floor coverings: garages concrete; wet areas/butler's pantries/kitchens ceramic tiles; all other areas carpet with rubber underlay; default covering colour medium (solar absorptance 0.5) (6.5-6.6).
- **Colour defaults:** Where unspecified, walls, roof, ceiling, floor covering and window frames all default to medium = solar absorptance 0.5. Defined values: light = 0.3, medium = 0.5, dark = 0.85. Solar absorptance = 1 − solar reflectance.
- **Windows (8.3-8.13):** Use AFRC custom window codes matching the documentation; if unavailable, substitute a custom window only with identical opening type, a Uw equal to or greater than the specified window, and SHGCw within ±5% (itemise substitutions in 'additional notes'). Default opening percentages (Table 5): fixed 0%, double hung 45%, sliding 45%, awning 90%, casement/tilt-and-turn 90%, louvre 90%; windows with restricted-opening safety requirements and no complying security screen default to 10%. A partially glazed door may be modelled as a solid door if glazing is <25% of the door. WERSlink default/custom window libraries arrive with Chenath 3.23.
- **Ceiling penetrations (9.4-9.7, Table 6):** All downlights, vents, flues, chimneys, fireplaces and exhaust fans must be modelled. Default where no lighting specification: sealed downlights at 1 per 5 m² (or part thereof), 90 mm diameter, 50 mm insulation clearance. Exhaust fans default 250 mm diameter, sealed in conditioned zones, unsealed in unconditioned zones. Unknown flue diameter: allow 300 mm total insulation clearance. Downlights must be modelled regardless of adjoining zone. Default edge batts: R3.0, 450 mm width (9.9).
- **Shading (10.4-10.11):** Model all single-storey neighbours within 10 m and two-storey-or-higher neighbours within 20 m. South of the Tropic of Capricorn, ignore shade features between SSE and SSW (168°45' and 191°15'); north of it, model all features. Unknown neighbours in new developments default to mirror the design being modelled (same footprint, height, setback) with 1.8 m side/rear fences. Only trees with a preservation order or heritage protection may be modelled as shade — no other vegetation.
- **Thermal bridging (11.1-11.9):** Applies only to repeating steel frame elements interrupting insulation, per Table 7 (Class 1 external walls; apartment external walls adjacent to non-neighbour public areas; internal walls to unconditioned garages/roof space/subfloor; ceilings below roof space or directly attached to roof; suspended floors above outside air/unconditioned garage/non-neighbour areas). Default steel framing in Table 8 (e.g. wall studs 90 × 40 mm at 600 mm spacing, 0.75 mm base metal; rafters for flat/skillion/cathedral roofs 200 × 75 mm at 900 mm). Thermal breaks require R-value ≥R0.2 separating frame from cladding and are modelled only after bridging is applied; Table 9 sets default thermal-break/air-gap combinations, including an additional R0.16 air gap for cavity walls and ceilings below attics. Roof blankets do not currently need thermal bridging modelling.
- **Whole of Home appliance defaults (Table 10):** Default heating/cooling is a room reverse-cycle air conditioner — heating HSPF 2.5 (1.0 star) in cold zones, 3.5 (2.0 stars) in mixed, 4.0 (2.5 stars) in hot/humid; cooling TCSPF 3.5 (2.0 stars) cold/mixed, 4.0 (2.5 stars) hot/humid (climate-zone groupings listed in the table footnotes). Default hot water: 4.0-star gas storage if reticulated gas is available, otherwise off-peak electric storage. Default lighting 5 W/m². Wood heater 60% (slow combustion). Pool pump defaults: single speed 1 star (dual 3 stars, multi-speed 5 stars). PV and pools cannot be included unless specified; default PV inverter capacity = system size × 0.75.
- **Heating/cooling modelling (12.2-12.5):** Where multiple appliances serve a zone, model the one with the highest energy consumption (excluding wood heaters unless sole heater). Ducted systems are modelled as ducted across all serviced zones; multi-splits as non-ducted across all serviced zones. For unrated systems, AEER/ACOP must come from the GEMS energy rating registry — manufacturer-specified values are not acceptable.
- **Centralised heat pump hot water proxy (12.10-12.14, new in this version):** For Class 2 buildings of four storeys or more with a centralised heat pump hot water system on a continuous tariff, closed-loop (circulatory) piping, NCC-compliant pipe insulation, and located in CER heat pump zones 3, 4 or 5 — model a decentralised heat pump in each SOU as a proxy: 25 STC for CER zone 3 (incl. Adelaide, Brisbane, Perth), 27 STC for zones 4 and 5 (incl. Melbourne, Canberra). Mandatory disclosure text must go in 'additional notes'. All other centralised hot water technologies are currently excluded.
- **STCs as efficiency proxy (12.9):** Use the 10-year STC value from the Clean Energy Regulator register (solar water heaters <700 L; air-source heat pumps ≤425 L) — not the CER online calculator value.
- **Re-rating triggers (12.26):** A Whole of Home assessment must be revised if appliance technology/efficiency/fuel type changes, the thermal assessment is updated, the hot water system's STC value moves outside the certificate's substitution range, or pool volume increases. A NatHERS Certificate is invalid if design documentation changes in a way that impacts the rating (3.3.1).
- **Certificates and stamping (13):** The NatHERS QR code stamp must be applied electronically to all design documentation relevant to the assessment. Class 2: each unit gets an individual certificate plus a building summary certificate showing the average thermal rating and the lowest individual Whole of Home rating. Final certificates are only issued on For Construction documentation.

## Caveats

- Version 20241023 (October 2024) is the version held in the library and applies to Chenath engines 3.22/3.23 under NCC 2022; the Technical Note is updated periodically (prior versions 20240501, 20231023, 20230913, 20220901) — check nathers.gov.au for newer releases before quoting.
- State or territory regulatory requirements (e.g. BASIX in NSW) prevail over this Technical Note where inconsistent.
- Several tables (Appendix 1 zoning matrix, Table 3 adjacency matrix, Table 9) have degraded layout in text extraction; the substance above is verified but verify cell-level detail against the source PDF.
