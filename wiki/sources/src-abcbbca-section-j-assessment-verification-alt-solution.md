# ABCB Handbook — BCA Section J Assessment and Verification of an Alternative Solution (2010, JV3)

## Summary

Non-mandatory ABCB handbook (first published 2010) on assessing and verifying energy efficiency Alternative Solutions against BCA Volume One Section J for commercial-type buildings, centred on Verification Method JV3 (reference building, annual energy consumption comparison). It explains the four BCA Assessment Methods, the use and limitations of energy analysis software (including the ABCB Protocol for Building Energy Analysis Software), and gives a clause-by-clause walkthrough of JV3's fixed parameters for the reference building, both buildings, and the proposed building. Relevant to NRG as background on the commercial (Class 3, 5–9) reference-building method — the Volume One analogue of the residential V2.6.2.2/H6V2 pathway.

## Key claims

- Energy efficiency entered the BCA in stages: 2003 for Class 1 and 10 (Volume Two), 2005 for Class 2/3 and Class 4 parts (Volume One), 2006 for Classes 5–9 (completing coverage, with Vol Two stringency also raised), and a further stringency increase in both volumes in 2010.
- Compliance with the mandatory Performance Requirements is achieved by DTS Provisions, an Alternative Solution, or a combination (A0.5); an Alternative Solution must be assessed by at least one of four Assessment Methods (A0.9): (a) evidence of suitability (A2.2), (b) Verification Methods, (c) comparison with the DTS Provisions, (d) expert judgement.
- JV3 applies to Class 3, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9 buildings only — not Class 2 buildings or Class 4 parts (which used the house energy rating star-rating approach). JV1 was absorbed into Part J0 DTS and JV2 (a "stated value" approach) was withdrawn at industry request.
- JV3 verifies JP1 when the annual energy consumption (MJ/m².annum or kWh/m².annum) of the proposed building is not more than that of a reference building (the same building designed to DTS Provisions). Three modelling runs are required: (1) reference building, (2) proposed building with proposed services, (3) proposed building with reference-building services.
- The third run exists to stop designers "trading away" envelope/fabric thermal performance for over-performing services; the trade is permitted only in the other direction (over-performing fabric can offset under-performing services), because fabric performance persists for the building's life while services degrade or change.
- Fixed reference building parameters (JV3(d)(i)) include: DTS Parts J1–J7 with only the minimum Part F4 mechanical ventilation; solar absorptance 0.6 for external walls and 0.7 for roofs; maximum illumination power density with no control-device adjustment factor; conditioned space temperature within 18°C DB to 26°C DB for 98% of plant operation time; Specification JV occupancy/operating profiles (or actual profiles if operating hours are not less than 2,500 per year); infiltration of 1.0 air change per hour for a perimeter zone (depth equal to floor-to-ceiling height) when pressurising plant operates and 1.5 ACH whole-building when it does not.
- JV3(d)(ii) lists 26 parameters (A)–(Z) that must be identical in all runs, including the calculation method/software, location and climate file, adjacent structures, orientation, building form (roof geometry, floor plan, storeys, glazing size and location — though glazing SHGC/U-Value/shading may vary), testing standards, surface density of envelope walls over 220 kg/m², internal loads and profiles, air-conditioning system configuration and zones, and infiltration values (unless specific sealing provisions/pressure testing are proposed).
- JV3(d)(iii): the proposed building must be modelled with a roof and wall solar absorptance 0.05 higher than proposed, to allow for weathering of lighter colours.
- JV3(b): energy from on-site renewables (solar, geothermal, wind) or reclaimed energy from another process may be deducted from the proposed building's annual energy consumption; purchased GreenPower does not qualify.
- JV3(g)(ii) still requires compliance with certain DTS provisions: J1.2 (general thermal construction), J1.3(c) (compensation for loss of ceiling insulation), J1.6(a)(ii)/(c)/(d) (floor edge insulation), and testing standards BS 7190 (water heaters), AS/NZS 3823.1.2 at test condition T1 (packaged air-conditioning) and ARI 550/590 (refrigeration chillers).
- Hot water supply and lifts/escalators may be omitted from modelling if identical in both buildings (no Vol One DTS provisions existed for them at publication); lift energy in mixed-class buildings may be apportioned by number of storeys served.
- Software for JV3 must comply with the ABCB Protocol for Building Energy Analysis Software (commercially available, hourly climate data based on TMY/WYEC/TRY files, 3D geometric modelling, results comparable per ASHRAE Standard 140-2001 BESTEST). Suppliers had claimed compliance for: BEAVER, the DOE suite (VisualDOE, eQUEST, EnergyPlus), E20-II HAP, IDA ICE v3.0, IES Apache, TAS and TRACE 700 — acceptance rests with State/Territory administrations.
- Industry advice cited: different energy analysis software can give results differing by up to 20% — the comparative (same software, same operator, all runs) structure of JV3 neutralises much of this.
- Key Section J definitions noted: "conditioned space" excludes Class 6/7/8/9b spaces where air-conditioning input energy is not more than 15 W/m²; "envelope" includes internal boundaries between conditioned and non-conditioned spaces (e.g. floor below a rooftop plant room), not just the outer shell.

## Caveats

- Historic (2010, BCA 2010 era): Section J was comprehensively overhauled in NCC 2019 (new JV verification methods, NABERS/Green Star pathways) and renumbered in NCC 2022 (e.g. J1V3); the term "Alternative Solution" itself was replaced by "Performance Solution". Clause references and software lists here are obsolete.
- Commercial focus (Volume One, Class 3 and 5–9) — does not apply to Class 1 dwellings; residential reference-building method is the separate V2.6.2.2 (now H6V2).
- State/Territory administration contact details (Appendix 1, dated 1 May 2010) are obsolete.
