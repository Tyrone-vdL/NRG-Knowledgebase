# Pinnacle Certifiers Email — Applying QLD s 61 vs s 81 to an Alterations Project (13 Third Av, Coolum Beach)

## Summary

An email from Zac of Pinnacle Certifiers to Matthew Morelli (cc Liam Stephens, Tye Lincoln), dated 26 November 2025, giving a certifier's view on how Building Act 1975 (Qld) ss 61 and 81 interact for an alterations project at 13 Third Av, Coolum Beach: s 61 can be applied because the work is alterations to an existing building, but s 81 becomes the concern if alterations exceed half the existing building's total volume. The email pastes the s 81(1) text with the half-volume trigger highlighted — a worked example of how a QLD certifier frames the threshold for energy/assessment provisions on renovations.

## Key claims

- Certifier's position: "s61 can be applied as this is alterations to an existing building, my concern would be is we have to apply s81 of the Act."
- Certifier's summary of the trigger: "Under s81 of the Act, a building needs to comply with existing assessment provisions if alterations form more than half the total volume of the existing building."
- The email reproduces s 81 heading: "81 Building development approval for particular alterations may require existing building or structure to comply with building assessment provisions".
- Pasted s 81(1) text (with (1), (a) and (i) highlighted by the certifier): the section applies to a building development approval for alterations to an existing building or structure if (a) the total of the following represents more than half the total volume of the existing building or structure, measured over its roof and external walls — (i) the alterations; (ii) any previous structural alterations to it approved or completed in the previous 3 years; or (b) the building certifier has decided the alterations pose a risk (i) to the safety of persons accommodated in or using the building or structure, or (ii) of spreading fire to adjoining buildings or structures.
- The certifier's highlighting of (1)(a)(i) signals the live issue on this project was the volume of the proposed alterations themselves, not prior works or fire/safety risk.
