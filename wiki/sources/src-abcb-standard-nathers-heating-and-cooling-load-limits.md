# ABCB Standard — NatHERS Heating and Cooling Load Limits (2019.1)

## Summary

The ABCB Standard "NatHERS heating and cooling load limits" (version 2019.1, published February 2019) sets the separate maximum annual heating and cooling loads (MJ/m².annum) that dwellings must not exceed — in addition to achieving the minimum star rating — when using the NatHERS compliance pathway under NCC 2019 (clause J0.2 of Volume One; clause 3.12.0.1 of Volume Two). It tabulates load limits per NatHERS climate zone for Class 1 buildings (split by floor type: concrete slab-on-ground vs suspended floor, at 6, 5.5 and 5 star targets) and for Class 2 sole-occupancy units and Class 4 parts (at 6 and 5 stars). NSW, NT, Tas and parts of Qld and WA are excluded (shown N/A) because their climates are heating- or cooling-dominated, or in NSW's case because BASIX already imposes its own caps.

## Key claims

- The Standard is NOT stand-alone: load limits are an additional requirement on top of the existing star ratings for Class 1 buildings, Class 2 SOUs and Class 4 parts when using the NatHERS deemed-to-satisfy pathway (NCC 2019 J0.2 Vol One / 3.12.0.1 Vol Two).
- Units are MJ/m².annum. Heating/cooling load = the heat energy calculated by NatHERS-accredited software that must be added to/removed from a space to maintain temperature in an acceptable range.
- Under NCC 2019, the NatHERS pathway requires Class 1 dwellings to achieve 6 stars (5.5 or 5 stars permitted with an outdoor living area and/or ceiling fan in NCC climate zones 1 and 2); Class 2 SOUs and Class 4 parts must achieve 6 stars on average with a minimum 5 stars per SOU. Load limits were developed for each of these target star ratings.
- Load limits do not apply in NSW, NT, Tas and parts of Qld and WA (shown as N/A). NSW already has separate heating and cooling caps via BASIX Thermal Comfort requirements; NT/Tas and some Qld/WA zones are dominated by hot or cold weather.
- For Class 1 buildings, the applicable table depends on the floor type of the LOWEST living area: Tables 1, 3, 5 apply for Concrete Slab-on-Ground (CSOG); Tables 2, 4, 6 apply for Suspended Floor (timber or concrete) or a mixture of CSOG and SF. (E.g. for a two-storey house this is typically the ground floor, but could be the first floor if the ground floor is a garage.)
- For Class 2 SOUs / Class 4 parts (regardless of floor type): the average of all SOUs' loads must not exceed the 6-star limits (Table 7); the worst-performing SOU must not exceed the 5-star limits (Table 8).
- Sample Class 1 CSOG 6-star limits (heating / cooling, MJ/m².annum): zone 6 (Qld) 57/91; zone 13 (WA) 57/39; zone 14 (Qld) 121/26; zone 16 (SA) 67/52; zone 20 (Vic) 114/47; zone 21 (Vic, Melbourne) 96/45; zone 22 (Vic) 123/27; zone 24 (ACT/Vic, Canberra) 154/38; zone 27 (Vic/SA) 82/57; zone 59 (SA) 226/14; zone 60 (Vic) 126/31; zone 66 (Vic, Ballarat) 189/26.
- Sample Class 1 SF 6-star limits: zone 21 (Vic) 88/47; zone 24 (ACT/Vic) 143/47; zone 27 (Vic/SA) 78/68; zone 66 (Vic) 181/48.
- 5.5-star and 5-star Class 1 limits exist only for Qld zones 7, 9 and 10 (all other zones N/A) — e.g. zone 9 CSOG at 5.5 stars: 43 heating / 61 cooling; zone 10 CSOG at 5 stars: 38/42.
- Sample Class 2 SOU / Class 4 6-star limits (Table 7): zone 21 (Vic) 88/36; zone 24 (ACT/Vic) 144/31; zone 27 (Vic/SA) 81/65; zone 66 (Vic) 178/28. Sample 5-star limits (Table 8): zone 21 120/62; zone 24 194/47; zone 66 233/50.
- A small number of zones have load limits only in the Class 2 5-star table (Table 8), e.g. zones 35, 36, 39 (Qld) and 40 (WA) — zone 39 (Qld): 52 heating / 205 cooling.
- The methodology behind the limits is in the ABCB report "NCC 2019 Energy efficiency residential buildings NatHERS heating and cooling load limits" (2018).

## Caveats

- This is edition 2019.1 (Feb 2019), keyed to NCC 2019 clause references (J0.2 Vol One, 3.12.0.1 Vol Two) and 6-star targets. NCC 2022's 7-star NatHERS pathway (H6, Specification 42 clause S42C2) references the ABCB Standard for NatHERS heating and cooling load limits in its updated form — verify the current edition before applying these figures to NCC 2022 work.
- Full tables span all 69 NatHERS climate zones; only representative values are quoted above. Verify any specific zone's limit against the source PDF tables (extraction preserved table rows but always double-check the zone row).
- Load limits never applied in NSW (BASIX governs), NT or Tas under this Standard.
