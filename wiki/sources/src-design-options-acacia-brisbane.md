# Design For Place — Acacia House Design Options (Brisbane)

## Summary

Australian Government Design For Place pack giving the building specifications that take the Acacia reference house (2 bed, 2 bath, 2 living, 123 m² single-storey with two courtyards) to 7.3, 8.3 and 8.9 NatHERS stars in the Brisbane climate. In Brisbane the design reaches 7+ stars on a plain 100 mm slab with 1000 mm eaves and single-glazed low-e windows — the glazing upgrade to double glazing is what lifts it to 8.3+, plus an operable courtyard pergola for 8.9. Companion to the Acacia Brisbane Technical Drawings and the Hobart/Melbourne versions of the same design.

## Key claims

- Star rating options modelled for Brisbane: 7.3, 8.3 and 8.9. The 7.3 and 8.3 specs are for the 'Brisbane – Option 1' design; 8.9 is 'Brisbane – Option 2', which adds minor sub-tropical design tweaks (shading, window types).
- On average a 7 star home in Brisbane is predicted to use 21% less heating/cooling energy than a 6 star home; an 8 star home 42% less. The Acacia 7.3 requires 25% less energy than a 6 star house; the 8.9 requires 59% less.
- Floor (all options): 100 mm concrete slab on ground — no waffle pod or under-slab insulation specified for Brisbane. Finishes: burnished concrete to living areas, cork to bedrooms, ceramic tiles to wet areas.
- Ceiling (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R5.0 batt insulation.
- Roof (all options): metal sheet roofing on timber battens with R1.5 reflective foil laminate; 1000 mm eave overhang (optimised for summer shading while allowing winter solar gain).
- External walls (all options): lightweight cladding on battens, reflective foil membrane (vapour permeable if appropriate), timber frame, R2.5 batt insulation, 10 mm plasterboard.
- Internal walls (all options): 10 mm plasterboard on timber framing with R2.5 batt insulation.
- Windows: single glazed, low emissivity glass, aluminium frame for the 7.3 option; double glazed, low emissivity glass, thermally broken aluminium frame for the 8.3 and 8.9 options.
- The 8.9 (Option 2) design adds an operable pergola over the courtyards.
- Draught sealing (all options): weather strips to all windows and sliding doors; seals/weather-strips to hinged external doors; sealed exhaust fans to bathroom, powder room and kitchen rangehood.
- Ceiling fans (all options): 1400 mm diameter — one in each bedroom, one in the dining area, one in the front living area.
- Up to 87% of a home's heat can be gained, and up to 40% lost, through windows and glass doors.
- Approximately 170 m² of roof space is available for solar panels; ceiling heights 2.7 m.
- Star ratings are estimates based on generic window performance values; any change to specifications or drawings affects the rating and the home must be rated by an assessor for its exact location.
