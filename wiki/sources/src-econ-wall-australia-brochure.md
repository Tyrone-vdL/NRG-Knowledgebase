# Econ Wall Insulated Concrete Thermal Mass Wall System Brochure

## Summary

Product brochure for ECON WALL, an Australian stay-in-place insulated concrete formwork (ICF) wall system that places rigid insulation (EPS, XPS, PIR or phenolic) on the outside of a poured reinforced-concrete core, with fibre cement on the inside, to create an externally insulated thermal mass wall. Gives estimated insulation-layer R-values by material and thickness (R2.7 to R4.4), panel/wall dimensions, bushfire (BAL) capability with render systems, and system components including a built-in services cavity variant (ECON WALL SC).

## Key claims

- System build-up: external rigid insulation layer + in-situ reinforced concrete thermal mass core + internal fibre cement (or MgO) drywall layer, connected by a non-conductive recycled-polymer vertical stud that acts as a thermal break and rebar support. Optional hybrid polymer-steel studs for fire-rated walls.
- Insulation on the outside leaves the concrete free to absorb, store and release thermal energy (passive solar design and optional hydronic/active heating embedded in the concrete).
- Estimated insulation R-values (insulation layer only, "final R Value would depend upon grade and brand of material selected"): XPS 75 mm R2.7, 100 mm R3.6; PIR 75 mm R3.3, 100 mm R4.4; EPS (H Grade) 100 mm R2.8, 125 mm R3.5, 150 mm R4.2; Phenolic 80 mm R4.2.
- Total wall thicknesses range 185–350 mm depending on insulation and concrete core (100/150/200 mm cores); add 40 mm for the ECON WALL SC built-in services cavity. Concrete usage 0.10–0.20 m³/m².
- Panel widths 1200/600/450/300/150 mm (custom available); heights 2500/2800/3100 mm (custom available).
- EPS used is manufactured to AS 1366.3-1992 Class H with flame retardant additive; optional Perform Guard termite-resistant additive (exclusive to RMAX); BASF Neopor graphite EPS available for enhanced performance.
- Bushfire capability with a suitable external render system: EPS and XPS can conform to BAL 29; PIR and phenolic foam can conform to BAL 40 (described as extreme fire resistance).
- Internal drywall layer is 9 mm or 10 mm fibre cement (or MgO sheet for superior fire/water/mould/vermin resistance) and is thermally conductive so heat can flow to the concrete core.
- External finishes: acrylic render systems (EIFS) with fibreglass mesh; or external cladding on built-in furring channels creating a drainage/ventilation cavity that can also accommodate radiant barriers; thin stone veneer/tiles direct-adhered.
- Concrete guide specification: 7–10 mm aggregate, 20–30 MPa, slump 100–120, water/cement ratio < 0.60, with plasticizers for flow.
- 15 modular wall configurations including uninsulated, double-insulated, basement (waterproof vinyl outer) and carpark variants.

## Caveats

- Undated brochure; no NCC/NatHERS compliance claims made — R-values are explicitly estimates for the insulation layer only, not system Total R-values, and exclude the concrete core and films.
- BAL claims are conditional on a "suitable external render system" and are stated as capability ("can confirm to"), not certification.
- Specification tables have degraded column alignment in extraction; verify model-number figures against the source PDF.
