# Brimbank ESD Guidance — Water Sensitive Urban Design (v2, 2024)

## Summary

Brimbank City Council's 19-page ESD guidance document (Version 2, 2024) setting out the council's preferred Water Sensitive Urban Design treatment hierarchy, encouraged and non-supported treatment methods, STORM/MUSIC modelling submission requirements, and preferred WSUD strategies for specific development types (dwelling-behind-existing, duplexes, townhouses, industrial sites) in Melbourne's west. It is the reference for what WSUD evidence Brimbank expects with planning applications under Clauses 22.02 and 53.18 of the Brimbank Planning Scheme.

## Key claims

- Brimbank WSUD Hierarchy (in order): 1) minimise paving extent, 2) permeable paving in pedestrian areas only, 3) capture rainwater for reuse, 4) treat with bio-filtration, 5) proprietary products only once bio-filtration is exhausted, 6) off-site drainage schemes only once all on-site options are exhausted.
- Both Clause 22.02 and Clause 53.18 of the Brimbank Planning Scheme call for best practice stormwater management; WSUD best practice is assessed by City Planning, drainage design and On-site Detention (OSD) by Engineering Services (fees apply for OSD assessment and Legal Point of Discharge applications).
- Modelling submission requirements: STORM must show a separate line for treated and non-treated portions of each catchment and demonstrate a minimum 100% rating; MUSIC reports must demonstrate best-practice minimum indicators and include the electronic .sqr file.
- WSUD plans must show all new/altered catchments (treated and untreated areas matching the modelling), located treatment devices with annotations (buffer-strip area, raingarden area and detention depth, tank capacity and reuse connections), plus typical device details, construction-phase erosion/sediment controls and operational maintenance commitments (inspection frequency, cleanout procedures, remediation).
- Encouraged treatments, in order of preference: rainwater tanks (connect to toilets/laundry; target 80% tank reliability in STORM; claim an extra "bedroom" per dwelling in STORM when connecting washing-machine cold taps; use Table D2D18 in NCC 2022 Volume 1 for unknown commercial occupancy; allow 1.5 occupants per car bay for industrial), buffer strips (minimum 0.6m runoff travel across vegetation, connected surface drain), vegetated swales, raingardens, sediment basins (large developments/treatment trains).
- Raingardens are explicitly to be avoided where possible; when unavoidable, minimise number/area, line in-ground raingardens to protect structures from reactive clay heave, consider a submerged zone for dry-spell plant survival, and expect periodic full filter media replacement; raingardens are generally up to 90cm deep.
- Non-supported methods: infiltration trenches (Brimbank's clay basalt-plain soils don't infiltrate), tyre-track split driveways (no exceptions generally), proprietary devices (only after preferred methods are demonstrated exhausted, with third-party performance evidence, local parts supply and a committed maintenance regime), permeable paving in trafficked areas (pedestrian areas encouraged; limited exceptions e.g. nose of car bays, arborist-required zones), and off-site drainage schemes (only via a recognised scheme with evidenced contribution payment).
- "Treat-in-lieu" method for a new dwelling behind an existing one: adding a tank to the existing house lets the treated existing-roof area be claimed in lieu of treating an equivalent area of new driveway, entered in STORM as "untreated driveway" and "treated driveway (existing roof in lieu)".
- Strategy guidance by genre: duplexes/townhouses — maximise tank reuse then buffer-strips, raingardens only when unavoidable (visible locations, shared catchments under owners' corporations); industrial — distribute raingardens to limit runoff travel, set back from boundaries by a distance equal to their depth, pair with (not under) canopy trees, and use vegetated swales for passive irrigation first.
- Pollution-control guidance for paved/industrial areas: roof fuelling areas with differential paving falls draining to fuel/oil interceptors, bunding for liquid contaminant handling, pre-screening with gross pollutant traps where litter is excessive, and pre-filtering sediment-laden runoff before raingardens.
- Positioning rules repeated across strategies: locate WSUD devices away from side/rear boundaries and away from buildings so overflow in heavy rain doesn't cross boundaries or pool against walls/footings (devices are sized for light rainfall and will overflow).

## Caveats

- Brimbank City Council (Victoria) guidance, Version 2, 2024 — council-specific preferences for Melbourne's west (clay basalt soils, lower rainfall); other Victorian councils and other states differ, and the document states content is subject to change without notice.
- Guidance for planning applications, not a mandatory standard — it interprets Brimbank Planning Scheme Clauses 22.02 and 53.18 rather than creating obligations itself.
