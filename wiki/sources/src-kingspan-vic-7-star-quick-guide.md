# Kingspan VIC 7 Star NatHERS Quick Guide (Class 1a, NCC 2022)

## Summary

Kingspan Insulation's Victoria quick guide (first issue 08/2024, doc KIAU0195 VIC) showing insulation specifications that achieve a 7 Star NatHERS rating under NCC 2022 for example single- and double-storey Class 1a homes in NatHERS Climate Zone 21 (Melbourne), in timber and steel frame, across floor types (concrete slab, wafflepod, slab + under-slab insulation). It is the VIC state variant — unlike the NSW guide (Zones 28 and 56, BASIX-linked), this one covers a single colder zone and is notable for requiring triple-glazed Low-E windows in its uninsulated-slab single-storey solutions. Solutions centre on Kooltherm K12 Framing Board (walls), Kooltherm K3 Floorboard (under slab) and GreenGuard GG350 (slab edge).

## Key claims

- VIC-specific scope: NatHERS Climate Zone 21 (Melbourne) only; no BASIX content (BASIX is NSW-only — the NSW variant covers Zones 28/56 and the BASIX 7 Star Total Load link).
- Distinctive VIC finding: timber- and steel-frame single-storey homes on a concrete slab on ground need Triple Glazed Low-E windows and sliding doors to reach 7 Stars in Zone 21; switching to a wafflepod or an insulated slab brings the requirement back to double glazing. The NSW guide never specifies triple glazing (its hardest cases use double glazed Low-E thermally broken).
- Zone 21 solutions also lean harder on ceilings than NSW Zone 28: most single-storey rows require R7.0 ceiling insulation, and wafflepod single-storey rows require R7.0 garage ceiling insulation as well.
- NCC 2022 raised the minimum NatHERS rating from 6 to 7 stars in some regions and updated climate files (69 climate zones, weather data 1990–2015 replacing 1970–2005); buildings cause around 18% of Australia's carbon emissions. NCC 2022 stated as in use in NSW and Victoria.
- Example homes identical to the NSW guide: single-storey 235.17 m² total (184.43 m² ground floor, 25.72 m² garage, 4.68 m² porch, 10.34 m² alfresco); double-storey 384.70 m² total (168.06 m² ground, 176.12 m² first, 36.59 m² garage, 3.93 m² porch). Medium colours, west-facing front, north-facing solar panels, IC4 downlights, ceiling perimeter R2.5 per AS 3999:2015.
- Timber single-storey slab-on-ground example: triple glazed Low-E + R2.7 wall batts with 25 mm Kooltherm K12 (R1.1) + R7.0 ceiling + R2.7 laundry perimeter walls; with 50 mm K3 Floorboard + 40 mm GG350 under/around the slab, double glazing + R2.7 walls + R6.0 ceiling suffices.
- Steel single-storey slab-on-ground example: triple glazed Low-E + R2.2 batts with 25 mm K12 + R7.0 ceiling + R7.0 garage ceiling + R1.3 roof blanket + R2.2 laundry perimeter walls.
- Double-storey solutions use double glazed Low-E (no triple glazing required) — e.g. uninsulated slab, timber frame: R2.5 + 25 mm K12 both storeys, R6.0 ceiling, R2.5 laundry/bathroom/W/C perimeter walls.
- Wall build-ups: 90 mm stud + 90 mm glass-fibre batt (R2.7) + 25 mm Kooltherm K12 (R1.1) inside or outside; 76 mm steel stud + 75 mm batt (R2.2) + 25 mm K12 (R1.1); or full-fill 80 mm Kooltherm K12 between 90 mm timber studs giving R3.6. (NSW lists the same build-ups but also flags R2.5/R2.0 batt options.)
- Floor build-up: 50 mm Kooltherm K3 Floorboard under slab = R2.3, plus 40 mm GreenGuard GG350 slab-edge insulation = R1.0.
- Pack sizes identical to the NSW guide: K12 25 mm × 2400 × 1200 mm (12/pack), 80 mm × 1200 mm in 403/413/553/563 mm widths (6/pack); K3 25 mm (12/pack) and 50 mm × 2400 × 1200 mm (6/pack); GG350 40 mm × 2400 × 300 mm (5/pack); Thermakraft Watergate Plus rolls 45–90 m² nominal.

## Caveats

- First issue 08/2024; solutions are modelled on two specific example house designs — guidance starting points, not deemed-to-satisfy specifications; actual ratings require a NatHERS assessment of the real design.
- Applies to Victoria, NatHERS Climate Zone 21 (Melbourne) only; do not transfer rows to other zones or states — see the NSW variant for Zones 28 and 56.
- The guide's double-storey section description contains a typo ("four bedroom single-storey home" with 384.70 m²); area figures match the NSW guide's double-storey model.
- The compliance tables are heavily layout-degraded in extraction; verify any row used for client advice against the source PDF.
