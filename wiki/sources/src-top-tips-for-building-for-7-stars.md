# Top Tips for Building for 7 Stars (NatHERS Fact Sheet)

## Summary

Official NatHERS fact sheet listing eight practical design tips for getting a dwelling to the NCC 2022 minimum 7-star thermal rating: early assessor involvement, orientation, glazing, zoning, insulation, ceiling fans, colours and lighting/ceiling penetrations. It also summarises what changed under NCC 2022, including the 6/6.5-star outdoor living area allowances in NCC climate zones 1 and 2.

## Key claims

- NCC 2022 requires all new Australian houses and apartments to meet a minimum 7-star NatHERS energy efficiency rating; equivalent increases apply to the other compliance pathways (Deemed-to-Satisfy elemental provisions and the Reference Building Verification Method).
- Allowances exist for 6 and 6.5 star houses with a compliant outdoor living area in NCC climate zones 1 and 2 — see Section S42C2 of NCC Volume Two.
- Tip 1 — involve a NatHERS accredited assessor early in the design process; assessors are trained to improve ratings and provide options to reach 7 stars and beyond.
- Tip 2 — orientation: build for the local climate (tropical/temperate/cool); repositioning a house plan on the block can make a large difference to performance.
- Tip 3 — glazing: double and triple glazing costs have fallen considerably; choosing NatHERS default windows usually results in a lower rating, so custom windows with known specifications give greater flexibility; window sizing, placement and shading should suit the climate.
- Tip 4 — increase zoning: more internal doors (end of corridors, bottom of stairs, occasionally used rooms like media/rumpus) let mechanical heating/cooling estimates be applied more accurately.
- Tip 5 — insulate more: increase the R value of wall, floor and ceiling insulation or extend insulation to new areas (slab edge, under slab, garage wall, internal walls to unconditioned zones like laundries and bathrooms); care must be taken not to increase condensation risk.
- Tip 6 — plan for fans: specifying ceiling fans in all rooms improves the NatHERS rating and comfort.
- Tip 7 — check colours: adjust roof and external wall colours (move away from darker roofs in warmer climates); some software can adjust window frame colours.
- Tip 8 — look at lighting: NatHERS rewards fewer ceiling penetrations; consider deleting downlights that aren't IC4 rated, roof windows and skylights, replacing them with options that allow continuous ceiling insulation.
- Building for 7 stars doesn't have to be costly or complicated — simple design features and material choices make a big difference; YourHome.gov.au provides free 7-star house plans and case studies.

## Caveats

- Tips are qualitative design guidance, not prescriptive requirements; no specific R-values or U-values are mandated in this document.
- References NCC 2022 at introduction; state/territory adoption timing varies.
