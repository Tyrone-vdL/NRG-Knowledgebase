# ABCB Advisory Note 2014-1 — Slip Resistance for Stairways, Landings and Ramps

## Summary

ABCB Advisory Note 2014-1 (August 2014) explains the BCA 2014 Deemed-to-Satisfy slip-resistance classifications for stairway treads, nosings, ramps and landings, tested to AS 4586-2013. It sets out the P-class (wet pendulum) and R-class (oil-wet inclining platform) classifications required for dry and wet surface conditions, transitional arrangements from AS/NZS 4586-2004, and compliance pathways including in-situ testing and Alternative Solutions.

## Key claims

- The mandatory Performance Requirements for slip resistance are DP2 (BCA Volume One) and P2.5.1 (BCA Volume Two): stairways and ramps must have slip-resistant walking surfaces on ramps and on stairway treads or near the edge of the nosing.
- BCA 2014 amendments (in force 1 May 2014, subject to State/Territory transitional provisions) introduced quantified slip-resistance classifications, replacing earlier qualitative "non-slip" language.
- Classifications apply to landings and ramps in Class 2-9 buildings, and to stair treads or nosings for all classes of buildings, for all finishes and surface types (carpet, tiles, timber, vinyl, concrete, metal).
- Table 1 classifications: ramp steeper than 1:14 — P4 or R11 (dry), P5 or R12 (wet); ramp steeper than 1:20 but not steeper than 1:14 — P3 or R10 (dry), P4 or R11 (wet); tread or landing surface — P3 or R10 (dry), P4 or R11 (wet); nosing or landing edge strip — P3 (dry), P4 (wet).
- BCA requirements apply only to stairways in Volumes One and Two, plus ramps and landings in Volume One; where a general surface requirement conflicts with a stairway/ramp/landing requirement, the latter takes precedence.
- Two AS 4586 test types apply to the BCA DtS Provisions: the wet pendulum test (classifications P0-P5, in-situ or laboratory) and the oil-wet inclining platform test (classifications R9-R13, laboratory only).
- A "wet" surface includes one exposed to weather (e.g. external stairway) or one that on occasions becomes wet (e.g. entry lobby); a "dry" surface is not normally wet other than by accidental spill or cleaning.
- Timber stairs with polished treads constructed on-site may require an in-situ wet pendulum test after the clear finish is applied to confirm compliance.
- Transition table (Table 2): AS/NZS 4586-2004 classifications V, W and X are equivalent to AS 4586-2013 classifications P5, P4 and P3 respectively; pre-1-May-2014 test reports under the 2004 edition remain valid for BCA 2014 compliance.
- Alternative Solutions remain available via the Assessment Methods: documentary evidence, Verification Methods, comparison with DtS Provisions, or Expert Judgement.
- NATA-registered testing organisations are recognised by the BCA as Registered Testing Authorities for slip-resistance testing.
- The classifications do not apply to disability-access slip-resistance provisions (continuous accessible paths of travel, TGSIs etc.), which fall under the Premises Standards.

## Caveats

- Published August 2014 and references BCA 2014 clause numbering (DP2, P2.5.1); NCC 2019 and NCC 2022 restructured these provisions (e.g. NCC 2022 Volume Two uses H-series Performance Requirements), so verify current clause references before citing.
- Advisory Note is guidance only — explicitly non-mandatory and non-regulatory; State and Territory adoption/transitional arrangements vary.
- The 2016 scheduled review of the Premises Standards mentioned in the document has long since passed.
