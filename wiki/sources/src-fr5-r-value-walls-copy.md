# FirstRate5 Wall R-Value Training Examples (Cert IV CPP41119, Paul Kearney & Associates)

## Summary

A training resource from the CPP41119 Certificate IV in Home Energy Efficiency and Sustainability (Thermal Performance Assessment / Home Sustainability Assessment), developed by Paul Kearney & Associates Pty Ltd (Education In Building, RTO No 32418, © 2021), showing how common wall constructions are entered in FirstRate5 with their insulation layers, thicknesses and R-value settings. Worked examples include an internal plasterboard stud wall, Colorbond metal-clad walls with R2.5 insulation (standard and vapour-permeable antiglare foil variants), and a Hebel PowerPanel 50 mm intertenancy/party wall system.

## Key claims

- Source is a quality-assessment training toolset for conducting Thermal Performance Assessments using FirstRate5, developed in consultation with industry by Paul Kearney & Associates Pty Ltd (nationally registered training provider No 32418, eib.edu.au), under the CPP41119 Certificate IV in Home Energy Efficiency and Sustainability.
- Internal plasterboard stud wall example: 10 mm plasterboard, 70/90 mm framing, total thickness 90 mm, modelled with R0.0 added insulation (FirstRate5 entry "RO.O RAGO AG 1").
- Colorbond metal-clad external wall example: Colorbond metal cladding, 90 mm timber studs, R2.5 insulation added to the frame, 10 mm plasterboard; total thickness 110 mm (FirstRate5 entry "COLORBOND METAL CLAD R2.5 PB", R2.5).
- Variant example: Colorbond metal clad with vapour-permeable antiglare foil plus R2.5 insulation, same 110 mm total thickness (FirstRate5 entry "COLORBOND METAL CLAD VAP PERM ANTIGLARE R2.5 PB", R2.5).
- Hebel PowerPanel 50 mm intertenancy/party wall system example: 50 mm Hebel PowerPanel (autoclaved aerated concrete panel) with 70 mm stud frame, CSR Bradford insulation, wall brackets fixed to frame with screws, deflection head and track base connection, acoustic damper strip — illustrating how a party wall system is built up in FirstRate5 layers (extraction garbled; figures unreliable).
- Each example shows the FirstRate5 wall construction dialog convention: layers listed outside-to-inside with expected thickness (mm) per layer, total thickness, and the insulation R-value assigned.

## Caveats

- Training material (© Paul Kearney & Associates 2021), not a standard or manufacturer datasheet — R-value entries illustrate FirstRate5 data-entry technique, not certified system R-values; verify any system R-value against AS/NZS 4859-compliant manufacturer documentation.
- Text extraction is heavily degraded (the PDF is largely screenshots of FirstRate5 dialogs); layer thickness and R-value figures, especially in the Hebel PowerPanel example, are garbled — verify all figures against the source PDF.
- Pre-dates the FirstRate5 NCC 2022 / Chenath 3.22 release; interface and library names may differ in current software.
