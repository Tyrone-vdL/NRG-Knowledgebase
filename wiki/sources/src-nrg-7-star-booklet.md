# NRG Efficient Homes 7 Star Information Booklet (Feb 2024)

## Summary

NRG Efficient Homes' client-facing information booklet (dated 1 February 2024) introducing the NCC 2022 7-star NatHERS minimum and the new Whole-of-Home annual energy use budget, and explaining what builders/clients must do — including NRG's recommended preliminary assessment service at $250–$400 + GST per dwelling. It lists exactly what additional appliance, hot water, lighting, pool and solar PV information must be supplied for a Whole-of-Home assessment, warning that missing data forces defaults that can affect compliance.

## Key claims

- NCC 2022 raises the minimum thermal performance rating of the building shell from 6 to 7 stars (out of 10) under NatHERS for new residential buildings (detached houses, townhouses and apartments).
- NCC 2022 also introduces a Whole-of-Home annual energy use budget for fixed appliances (hot water, heating and cooling, lighting, pool and spa pumps), with potential to offset via rooftop solar.
- The Victorian variation requiring either a solar water heater or a rainwater tank is removed under NCC 2022, letting Victorians choose their preferred hot water system.
- NCC 2022 requires apartments to be designed for easy, cost-effective retrofit of solar systems and EV charging.
- The Whole-of-Home rating is a 0–100 scale covering energy used by heating, cooling and appliances minus energy generated by solar panels.
- 7-star enforcement was originally slated for 1 October 2023 but was extended to May 2024 (booklet refers readers to NRG's state-by-state summary for jurisdiction detail).
- NRG states that the majority of dwellings they assess would NOT achieve 7 stars even with major insulation and glazing upgrades — described as the industry consensus from discussion and surveys.
- NRG strongly recommends all new projects be sent for a preliminary assessment at concept stage; pricing $250–$400 + GST per dwelling, positioned as avoiding thousands of dollars in late design changes at BA approval stage.
- Whole-of-Home assessments require: heating/cooling appliance types, models, sizes and efficiencies plus a mark-up of NatHERS conditioned zones served; hot water service type, model, size and efficiency; calculated lighting power density (W/m2) complying with Section 13.7.6 of the ABCB Housing Provisions; pool dimensions (surface area m2 or volume in litres), pump model/type (single-, double- or multi-speed), cleaner type and pump star rating (energy rating label commencing 1 October 2022); PV array rated sizes (kW), azimuth and inclination per array group, rated inverter size (kW), phases and export limits (kW).
- If any required Whole-of-Home information is unavailable, default values are used, which could affect the compliance outcome.
- Key 6-to-7-star differences: better orientation/passive solar design, improved ceiling/wall/under-floor insulation for climate, and high performance glazing.

## Concepts touched

## Caveats

- Dated 1 February 2024 — the "extended to May 2024" transition statement is time-sensitive and now historical; check current state-by-state adoption for live deadlines.
- NRG marketing/client-education material, not a regulatory document; the preliminary assessment pricing ($250–$400 + GST) is NRG's own commercial offer as at Feb 2024.
