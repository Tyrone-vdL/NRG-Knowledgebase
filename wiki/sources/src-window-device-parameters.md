# External Shading Device Parameters — 80% Summer Solar Radiation Clause

## Summary

A single-clause excerpt defining what qualifies as an external shading device for windows under the NCC deemed-to-satisfy shading provisions: it must restrict at least 80% of summer solar radiation and, if adjustable, be readily operable by the building occupants. The screenshot captures only paragraph (b) of a larger clause; the parent clause number and NCC edition are not visible.

## Key claims

- The shading requirement may "(b) be provided by an external shading device, such as a shutter, blind, vertical or horizontal building screen with blades, battens or slats, which—".
- "(i) is capable of restricting at least 80% of the summer solar radiation; and".
- "(ii) if adjustable, is readily operated either manually, mechanically or electronically by the building occupants.".
- Qualifying device examples given in the clause text: shutter, blind, vertical or horizontal building screen with blades, battens or slats.
- The two tests are cumulative: an adjustable device must meet both the 80% summer solar radiation restriction and the ready-operation requirement.
