# Foilboard Insulation Panel — Floor, Wall and Ceiling Systems and R-Values

## Summary

Product brochure for FOILBOARD Insulation Panel — a fire-retardant SL-grade EPS core laminated both sides with 20-micron pure aluminium foil — covering its four thickness variants (Standard 10, Super 15, Ultra 20, Cathedral 25) and independently calculated system Total R-values for brick veneer, cavity brick, concrete, underfloor and cathedral roof applications, stated separately for heat flow out (winter) and heat flow in (summer) per AS/NZS 4859.1:2002. Also documents the floor saddle underfloor fixing system, taped-join installation method and fire/material specifications.

## Key claims

- Construction: fire retardant SL Grade EPS core (nominal density 13.5 kg/m³) with 100% pure aluminium 20-micron foil laminated to both sides; anti-glare UV-stabilised green ink on one face that does not impede reflectiveness.
- Variants and thicknesses: Standard 10 (10 mm), Super 15 (15 mm), Ultra 20 (20 mm), Cathedral 25 (25 mm); sheet sizes 2440 × 1200 mm and 2700 × 1200 mm; manufacturable up to 100 mm thick where volume is sufficient.
- Surface properties (all variants): reflectance 95% (anti-glare face) / 97% (reflective face); emittance E0.05 (anti-glare face) / E0.03 (reflective face).
- R-value basis: Total R-values for the building system, independently calculated, checked by a second thermal engineer and submitted to the Building Regulations Advisory Committee; in-service condition per AS/NZS 4859.1:2002. Heat flow OUT values use ΔT 6 K (indoors 18°C, outdoors 12°C, mean 15°C); heat flow IN values use ΔT 12 K (indoors 24°C, outdoors 36°C, mean 30°C).
- Residential wall system Total R-values (out / in): brick veneer — Ultra 20 R2.6/R2.6, Super 15 R2.2/R2.2, Standard 10 R1.8/R1.8; cladded wall — Ultra 20 R2.4/R2.4, Super R2.0/R2.0; double brick — Super 15 R2.2/R2.2, Standard 10 R1.8/R1.8. An uninsulated cavity brick wall is quoted at R0.5.
- Underfloor Total R-values (out / in): floor saddle system — Super 15 R2.6/R1.3, Standard 10 R2.2/R1.2; retrofit (fixed under joists) — Super 15 R3.5/R1.5, Standard 10 R3.1/R1.3.
- Concrete systems (out / in): a 150 mm concrete wall with plasterboard alone rates only R0.3; single airspace on 28 mm battens — Ultra 20 R1.8, Super 15 R1.6, Standard 10 R1.4; dual airspace on 28 mm battens — Ultra 20 R2.3, Super 15 R2.2, Standard 10 R2.0; concrete floor — Ultra 20 R2.8/R1.8, Super 15 R2.8/R1.6.
- Ceiling/roof systems (out / in): cathedral roof — Cathedral 25 R2.5/R3.0, Ultra 20 R2.3/R2.7; cathedral retrofit — Super 15 R3.2/R4.0; factory/shed roof — Cathedral 25 R1.9/R4.0, Super 15 R1.5/R3.0. Two layers with a reflective air space (20 mm min) between sheets are used in cathedral ceilings to achieve higher R-values.
- Acoustic ratings available for various systems up to STC 57 / Rw 56; reflective air spaces in the systems inhibit moisture transfer and mould formation.
- Fire (AS 1530.3 early fire hazard indices): Foilboard ignitability 0, spread of flame 0, heat evolved 0, smoke developed 0–1 (vs bare EPS 12/0/3/5); zero ozone-depleting substances and zero VOC content.
- Installation: fixed to stud faces (eliminating thermal bridging through the frame), butt-joined with aluminium tape — joins need not land on studs; taping gaps/holes prevents air infiltration "which lowers R-value"; an airspace between cladding and Foilboard is essential; floor saddles sit on joists at ~600 mm centres with panels clicking into a patented lock-in flap.
- Used externally on framed walls it acts as a weatherproof shield letting internal trades proceed before brickwork/cladding; does not delaminate in prolonged wet/windy conditions and will not sag, so the full R-value lasts the life of the building (manufacturer claims).

## Caveats

- Undated brochure (testimonial dated 1995; design registration cited) referencing AS/NZS 4859.1:2002, since superseded by the 2018 edition — current Foilboard published R-values may differ.
- System R-values depend on the specified airspaces and taped joins being achieved in practice; reflective system performance degrades with dust and poor workmanship.
- The brochure's system diagrams (which define each numbered system's exact build-up) are graphical and not captured in text extraction — verify system construction details against the source PDF.
